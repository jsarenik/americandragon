HERB FORMULAS

MAI MEN DONG TANG - 麥門冬湯 - OPHIOPOGONIS DECOCTION

English: Ophiopogonis Decoction
Also Known As: Decoction of Ophiopogonis Root

HERBS AND ACTIONS

Pharmaceutical Latin
Pin Yin
Dosage
Actions
Rx. Ophiopogonis Mai Men Dong 10-70g Nourishes Lung and Stomach Yin, clears Deficiency Heat from the Stomach, generates Fluid in the Stomach and Lungs and moistens the Intestines.
With Zhi Ban Xia and Dang Shen, for chronic, dry cough due to Lung Yin Deficiency.
With Zhi Ban Xia, Geng Mi and Gan Cao, for Stomach Yin Deficiency with hiccups and vomiting.
Rx. Ginseng Ren Shen 2-9g Revives Qi and Yin and generates Fluids.
With Mai Men Dong, strongly tonifies Qi and nourishes Lung and Stomach Yin.
(Rx. Codonopsis) (Dang Shen) (6-27g) (Substitutes for Ren Shen when the digestive system is weak.)
Nonglutinous Rice Geng Mi 5-20g Tonifies Qi and generates Fluids.
With Gan Cao and Da Zao, assists Stomach Qi and generates Fluids.
Fr. Jujube Da Zao 2-12g Strengthens the Spleen, tonifies Qi, nourishes Blood and harmonizes.
Rx. Glycyrrhizae Gan Cao 2-6g Clears Heat, relieves toxins, moistens the Lungs and harmonizes.
Rz. Pinelliae Preparatum Zhi Ban Xia 4.5-18g Moves Stomach Qi, descends Qi and assists distribution of Stomach Qi to the Lungs.
With Ren Shen, for vomiting due to Stomach Deficiency.

FORMULA ACTIONS

  • Benefits the Stomach
  • Generates Fluids
  • Descends Qi
  • Harmonizes the Middle Jiao

SYNDROMES

  • Stomach Fire Blazing
  • Stomach and Lung Yin Deficiency
  • Lung Wei Syndrome (atrophy)

CLINICAL MANIFESTATIONS

  • Cough with little yellow sputum
  • Spitting saliva
  • Wheezing
  • Shortness of breath
  • Dry, uncomfortable sensation in throat
  • Hiccups
  • Dry mouth
  • Coughing up difficult-to-extectorate Phlegm
  • Nausea
  • Scanty urination
  • Hoarseness
  • Thirst
  • Asthma
  • Flushed face
  • Five Sole Heat
  • Vomiting
  • T: Red and dry
  • C: Little or None
  • P: Thready and rapid or Deficient and rapid

CONTRAINDICATIONS AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Use with caution for those with cough or hiccup due to Wind-Cold or Heat.
  • Contraindicated for those with Dampness.
  • Use with caution for those with high fever and irritability due to Exterior Heat.
  • Contraindicated for those with Lung atrophy due to Deficiency Cold.
HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS
  • This formula was found to be effective in treating enalapril-induced cough.
  • This formula was found to be effective in treating capsaicinl-induced cough.
