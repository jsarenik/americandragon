HERB FORMULAS

HAO QIN QING DAN TANG - 蒿芩清膽湯 - SWEET WORMWOOD AND SCUTELLARIA DECOCTION TO CLEAR THE GALLBLADDER

English: Sweet Wormwood and Scutellaria Decoction to Clear the Gallbladder
Also Known As: Artemisia Annua and Scutellaria Decoction to Clear the Gallbladder

HERBS AND ACTIONS

Pharmaceutical Latin
Pin Yin
Dosage
Actions
Hb. Artemisiae Annuae Qing Hao 4.5-24g Clears Summerheat or Damp-Heat.
Rx. Scutellariae Huang Qin 4.5-12g Clears Heat, dries Dampness, drains Fire and detoxifies.
Caulis Bambusae in Taeniam Zhu Ru 6-9g Clears Heat, transforms Hot Phlegm and stops vomiting.
Rz. Pinelliae Preparatum Zhi Ban Xia 4.5-12g Dries Dampness, transforms Phlegm, descends Rebellious Qi, stops vomiting and harmonizes the Stomach.
Fr. Aurantii Zhi Ke 4.5-9g Promotes the movement of Qi, reduces distention and pressure and resolves hardenings.
Per. Citri Reticulatae Chen Pi 4.5-15g Regulates Qi, adjusts the Middle, relieves the diaphragm, dries Dampness and transforms Phlegm.
With Zhi Ke, for abdominal distention, fullness and pain.
With Zhi Ban Xia, for a stifling sensation in the chest and cough with excessive white, viscous sputum due to Phlegm-Damp Obstruction.
Poria Rubra Chi Fu Ling 9-15g Clears Dampness and Heat.
Jasper Powder Bi Yu San 9g Clears Heat, relieves toxicity, promotes, urination, eliminates Summerheat, tonifies Qi, cools the Blood and arrests convulsions.

FORMULA ACTIONS

  • Clears Gallbladder Heat
  • Dispels Dampness
  • Harmonizes the Stomach
  • Resolves Phlegm

SYNDROMES

  • Gallbladder Damp-Heat
  • Damp-Heat and Phlegm in the Shao Yang channels constraining the Ying and Wei

CLINICAL MANIFESTATIONS

  • Alternating chills and fever with fever more prominent
  • A bitter taste in the mouth
  • Nausea
  • Dry heaves
  • Hiccups
  • A stifling sensation in the chest
  • Chest and hypochondriac pain and distention
  • Distending pain in the upper abdomen
  • Vomiting bitter or sour fluids that are yellow and sticky
  • Acid regurgitation
  • Thirst
  • T: Red
  • C: Yellowish or White and greasy or Yellow and greasy
  • P: Left: Wiry and rapid, Right: Slippery and rapid

TREATS

  • Cholelithiasis
  • Peptic ulcer
  • Gastritis
  • Pyelonephritis
  • Malaria
  • Leptospirosis
  • Reflux gastritis
  • Hypertension
  • Chronic pancreatitis
  • Oliguria
  • Nausea/Vomiting
  • Acid reflux
  • Indigestion
  • Myalgic encephalomyelitis
  • Vegetative neurosis
  • Influenza
  • Fever
  • Acute icteric hepatitis
  • Acute enteritis
  • Typhoid
  • Pelvic inflammatory disease
  • Pneumonia
  • Aural vertigo
  • Coronary Heart disease
  • Hiccup
  • Vertigo
  • Peptic ulcer
  • Otitis media
  • Chronic hepatitis
  • Liver cancer with jaundice, ascites and fever
  • Epstein-Barr virus syndrome

CONTRAINDICATIONS AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with Phlegm-Dampness due to Yang Deficiency.
HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

NOTES

  • Place Bi Yu San in cheesecloth and decoct with the rest of the ingredients.
