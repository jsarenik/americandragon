HERB FORMULAS

DAO CHI (RE) SAN - 導赤散 - GUIDE OUT THE RED POWDER

English: Guide Out the Red Powder
Also Known As: Rehmannia and Akebia Formula
Powder to Conduct Heart Fire Downwards
Conduct the Redness (Heat) Powder
Powder of Fresh Rehmannia and Clematis Stem to Conduct Fire Downward
Fire-Inducing Powder

HERBS AND ACTIONS

Pharmaceutical Latin
Pin Yin
Dosage
Actions
Rx. Rehmanniae Sheng Di Huang 5-30g Cools Heart Fire, nourishes Yin, cools Blood and generates Fluids.
Caulis Akebiae Mu Tong 3-12g Clears Heat from the Heart channel and clears Heat and promotes urination through the Small Intestine channel.
With Dan Zhu Ye and Sheng Di Huang, for mouth ulcers, burning throat pain, irritability and insomnia due to Heart Fire.
Hb. Lophatheri Dan Zhu Ye 3-10g Alleviates irritability by clearing Heat from the Heart.
With Mu Tong and Sheng Di Huang, for Heat in the Heart channel manifesting as chest Heat and irritability, mouth and tongue sores and dark, scanty urine.
Rx. Glycyrrhizae (tips) Gan Cao (Shao) 3-10g The tips treat painful urinary dysfunction, relieve toxicity and harmonize.

FORMULA ACTIONS

  • Clears the Heart
  • Nourishes Yin
  • Promotes urination
  • Relieves Lin Syndrome

SYNDROMES

  • Heart Fire Flaring Up
  • Small Intestine Excess Heat
  • Heart and Kidney Yin Deficiency
  • Heat in the Heart and Small Intestine Channels

CLINICAL MANIFESTATIONS

  • Irritability with a sensation of Heat in the chest
  • Thirst with a desire to drink cold beverages
  • Red face
  • Fever
  • May be sores around the mouth
  • Dark, scanty urine
  • Rough urination
  • Fidgeting
  • Warm sensations in the chest
  • Sores on the tongue
  • Painful urination (stabbing)
  • Urination short and hesitant
  • Hematuria
  • Oliguria
  • Maybe blood in the urine
  • Sluggish urination
  • Irritability
  • Fatigue
  • T: Red
  • P: Rapid

CONTRAINDICATIONS AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Use caution for those with Spleen and Stomach Deficiency.
  • This formula may not be appropriate for those with repetitive stomatitis.

NOTES
