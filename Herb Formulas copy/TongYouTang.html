HERB FORMULAS

TONG YOU TANG - 通疣汤 - DECOCTION FOR DYSPHAGIA

English: Decoction for Dysphagia
Also Known As:

HERBS AND ACTIONS

Pharmaceutical Latin
Pin Yin
Dosage
Actions
Rx. Rehmanniae Preparata Shu Di Huang 3-20g Nourishes the Blood and nourishes Liver and Kidney Yin.
With Dang Gui, for Blood Deficiency.
Rx. Rehmanniae Sheng Di Huang 3-30g Clears Heat, cools the Blood, nourishes Yin and generates fluids.
With Shu Di Huang, for Heat symptoms due to Yin Deficiency and Devastated Blood.
With Sheng Ma, cools Heat in the Lungs and Stomach, stopping hemoptysis and hematemesis.
Rx. Angelicae Sinensis Dang Gui 6-12g Tonifies, invigorates and harmonizes the Blood, reduces swelling, expels pus, generates flesh and alleviates pain.
With Tao Ren and Hong Hua, for amenorrhea, pain, swelling and other symptoms associated with Blood Stasis.
With Shu Di Huang, for Heat symptoms due to Yin Deficiency and Devastated Blood.
Sm. Persicae Tao Ren 6-12g Breaks up Blood Stasis, invigorates Blood circulation and drains abscesses.
Flos Carthami Hong Hua 3-12g Invigorates the Blood, dispels Blood Stasis, opens the channels, unblocks menstruation and alleviates pain.
With Tao Ren, invigorates the Blood for all types of Blood Stagnation.
Sm. Arecae Bing Lang 3-6g Regulates Qi, reduces accumulations, drains downward and unblocks the bowels.
Rz. Cimicifugae Sheng Ma 3-12g Clears Heat and relieves toxicity.
Rx. Glycyrrhizae Preparata Zhi Gan Cao 3-6g Moderates and harmonizes the harsh properties of other herbs and guides the herbs to all twelve channels.

FORMULA ACTIONS

  • Nourishes the Blood
  • Invigorates the Blood
  • Dispels Blood Stasis
  • Clears Heat
  • Cools the Blood

SYNDROMES

  • Blood Stagnation with Blood Deficiency and Heat
  • Dysphagia due to Blood Dryness with Blood Stasis in the chest

CLINICAL MANIFESTATIONS

  • Progressive dysphagia
  • Ingestion of food is prevented
  • Vomiting immediately after eating
  • Vomiting of saliva and undigested food
  • Vomiting with constipation (severe)
  • Chest pain (severe)
  • Swallowing eventually becomes impossible
  • Emaciation with dry skin (severe)
  • Loss of appetite, especially for meat
  • Stomach distention
  • A sensation of obstruction in the throat
  • Diminished saliva
  • Maybe fever due to pulmonary infection
  • Maybe aortic erosion
  • Liquids or semi-solids are sustained
  • Dizziness
  • Dry muscles and skin
  • Definite obstruction between the pharynx and diaphragm
  • Hard stools
  • Dull pain
  • Burning pain
  • A rancid taste in the mouth
  • Precordial discomfort
  • Constipation
  • A pale complexion
  • Pale nails
  • Pale lips
  • Maybe severe coughing
  • Maybe aspiration of Fluids
  • T: Normal or Cyanotic or Red or Pale
  • C: Little or Non
  • P: Weak and choppy or Thready and rapid

TREATS

  • Esophageal cancer
  • Constipation
  • Intestinal tuberculosis
  • Sideropenic dysphagia
  • Dysphagia
  • Esophageal ulcer
  • Esophagitis
