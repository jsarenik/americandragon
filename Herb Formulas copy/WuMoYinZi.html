HERB FORMULAS

WU MO YIN ZI - 五磨饮子 - FIVE MILLED-HERB DRINK

English: Five Milled-Herb Decoction
Also Known As: Aromatic Formula with Five Herbs

HERBS AND ACTIONS

Pharmaceutical Latin
Pin Yin
Dosage
Actions
Rx. Aucklandiae Mu Xiang 6-9g Promotes the movement of Qi, alleviates pain, adjusts and regulates stagnant Qi in the Intestines, dispels Damp-Heat and harmonizes the Liver and Spleen.
With Bing Lang, for Qi Stagnation in the Stomach and Intestines due to Food Stagnation with abdominal distention, and fullness and distention, anorexia, aversion to the smell of food, nausea without the ability to vomit and an uncomfortable urge to defecate which is often unsuccessful.
With Wu Yao, for lower abdominal Coldness and pain.
Lignum Aquilariae Resinatum Chen Xiang 6-9g Promotes movement of Qi, alleviates pain, descends Qi, regulates the Middle, dispels Cold, warms the Spleen and Stomach, stops vomiting, warms the Kidneys and aids the Kidneys in grasping Qi.
With Wu Yao, for a stifling sensation in the chest and diaphragm with wheezing, distention, and anorexia.
With Mu Xiang, for rebellious Middle Jiao Qi with belching, hiccup, nausea and vomiting, epigastric and abdominal distention stifling sensation in the epigastrium and diarrhea.
With Mu Xiang, Zhi Shi and Bing Lang, for constipation due to Liver Qi Stagnation.
With Wu Yao, Bing Lang and Mu Xiang, for chest and abdominal distention and pain due to Accumulation of Cold.
Sm. Arecae Bing Lang 6-9g Regulates Qi, reduces accumulations, drains downward and unblocks the bowels.
Fr. Aurantii Immaturus Zhi Shi 3-9g Breaks up Stagnant Qi, reduces accumulation, descends Qi, unblocks the bowels, removes Stagnant Food, transforms Phlegm, reduces distention and resolves hardenings.
Rx. Linderae Wu Yao 6-9g Promotes the movement of Qi and alleviates pain.

FORMULA ACTIONS

  • Promotes the movement of Qi
  • Descends Rebellious Qi
  • Relieves Stagnant Liver Qi in the Lung and chest

SYNDROMES

  • Liver Qi Stagnation causing chest pain

CLINICAL MANIFESTATIONS

  • Chest pain
  • Epigastric and abdominal distention and pain
  • Closed type coma (lockjaw and clenched fists)
  • Cold extremities
  • Wandering pain
  • Asthma
  • Irritability in the throat
  • Palpitations
  • Insomnia
  • T: Normal
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Wiry

TREATS

  • Chest pain due to Liver Qi Stagnation
  • Chest pain with Phlegm
  • Asthma
  • Anxiety neurosis
  • Stress-related breathing problems
  • Epigastric and abdominal distention and pain
  • Coma
  • Hysteria
  • Palpitations
