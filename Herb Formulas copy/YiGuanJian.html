ERB FORMULAS

YI GUAN JIAN - 一貫煎 - LINKING DECOCTION

English: Linking Decoction
Also Known As: The Liver Reinforcing Decoction

HERBS AND ACTIONS

Pharmaceutical Latin
Pin Yin
Dosage
Actions
Rx. Rehmanniae Sheng Di Huang 6-45g Clears Heat, cools the Blood and nourishes the Yin and Blood.
With Gou Qi Zi, softens the Liver by nourishing the Blood and Kidney Yin.
With Mai Men Dong and Sha Shen, for dry mouth, thirst and a red tongue due to Stomach Yin Deficiency.
Fr. Lycii Gou Qi Zi 3-18g Nourishes and tonifies the Liver and Kidneys, and nourishes the Blood and Yin.
 With Dang Gui, Sha Shen, and Chuan Lian Zi, for Yin Deficiency with Qi Stagnation with epigastric and flank pain, dry mouth and throat, a bitter taste and acid regurgitation.
Rx. Adenophorae/ Glehniae Sha Shen 3-12g Nourishes the Stomach and Lung Yin.
 With Mai Men Dong, enriches and nourishes Stomach and Lung Yin, protects against Liver attacking the Stomach and Lung, treats parched mouth and throat and helps soften the Liver.
Rx. Ophiopogonis Mai Men Dong 3-12g Nourishes the Stomach and Lung Yin.
With Sha Shen, for a dry throat, constipation, thirst and a red tongue with little coat due to pathogenic Warmth or Heat injuring the Stomach Yin or long-term Yin Deficiency.
Rx. Angelicae Sinensis Dang Gui 3-12g Nourishes and invigorates the Blood.
Fr. Toosendan Chuan Lian Zi 1.5-6g Disperses Liver Qi, alleviates pain and clears Heat.
With Sheng Di Huang and Gou Qi Zi, for hypochondriac and chest pain, dry mouth and acid regurgitation from Liver Qi Stagnation along with Liver and Kidney Yin Deficiency

FORMULA ACTIONS

  • Enriches Kidney and Liver Yin
  • Spreads Liver Qi

SYNDROMES

  • Liver Yin Deficiency
  • Liver and Kidney Yin Deficiency (with Liver Qi Stagnation)
  • Liver and Kidney Yin Deficiency with abdominal masses

CLINICAL MANIFESTATIONS

  • Hypochondriac and chest pain
  • Dry, parched mouth and throat
  • Bitter taste in mouth
  • Acid regurgitation
  • Dizziness
  • Epigastric and abdominal distention
  • Maybe constipation
  • T: Red and dry
  • C: Little or None
  • P: Thready and weak or Wiry and deficient
