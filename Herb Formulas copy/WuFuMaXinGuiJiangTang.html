HERB FORMULAS

WU FU MA XIN GUI JIANG TANG - 烏附麻辛桂姜汤 - DECOCTION OF ACONITE, EPHEDRA, ASARI, CINNAMON AND DRIED GINGER

English: Decoction of Aconite, Ephedra, Asari, Cinnamon and Dried Ginger
Also Known As:

HERBS AND ACTIONS

Pharmaceutical Latin
Pin Yin
Dosage
Actions
Rx. Aconiti Preparata Zhi Chuan Wu 10g Expels Wind-Dampness, disperses Cold, warms and activates the channels and stops pain.
Rx. Aconiti Lateralis Preparata Zhi Fu Zi 10g Disperses Cold and Dampness, warms the channels and stops pain.
With Gan Jiang and Gan Cao, strengthens its actions of fortifying Qi and benefiting the Yang.
With Xi Xin, disperses Cold and stops pain.
Rz. Zingiberis Gan Jiang 10g Dispels Wind-Dampness seeping into the Lower Jiao and warms the channels.
With Zhi Fu Zi, for Cold-Damp Bi.
Hb. Ephedrae Ma Huang 6gt Induces sweating, releases the Exterior, promotes urination, reduces edema and warms and disperses Cold pathogens.
With Zhi Fu Zi, for Wind-Cold Bi.
With Ma Huang and Zhi Fu Zi, for Exterior Wind-Cold with underlying Yang Deficiency.
Hb. cum Rx. Asari Xi Xin 3g Disperses Wind-Cold and Internal Cold and alleviates pain.
With Ma Huang, alleviates Wind-Cold-Damp Bi pain.
With Zhi Fu Zi, for Wind-Damp-Cold Bi with spasms of the joints and intolerable pain.
Ram. Cinnamomi Gui Zhi 6g Releases the Exterior, assists Yang, adjusts the Ying and Wei, releases the muscle layer, warms the channels and collaterals to relieve pain and warms and facilitates the flow of Qi through the channels and collaterals and Blood through the vessels.
With Ma Huang , for Wind-Cold-Damp Bi.
With Zhi Fu Zi, for chills and painful extremities due to Yang Deficiency with Wind-Cold-Damp Bi.
Rx. Glycyrrhizae Gan Cao 6g Moderates and harmonizes the harsh properties of other herbs and guides the herbs to all twelve channels.

FORMULA ACTIONS

  • Warms the Channels
  • Dispels Wind, Cold and Damp

SYNDROMES

  • Wind-Cold Damp with Cold Predominant

CLINICAL MANIFESTATIONS

  • Severe cold toe pain
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Cold extremities
  • Limited movement of the toes in the case of severe pain
  • Excruciating sharp, stabbing joint pain
  • Pain alleviated by warmth and aggravated by Cold
  • Pain with fixed location
  • Few joints are involved
  • The joints cannot be flexed or extended
  • No local redness or Heat
  • Pain worse at night and better during the day
  • Painful area often feels cold to the touch
  • Pain mostly affects low back and lower extremities
  • Pain intense but short lived
  • Pain changes over days or weeks
  • Ankylosis (stiffness) of joint
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Maybe chills with a slight fever
  • Anhidrosis
  • Frequent nocturnal urination
  • T: Pink or Pale
  • C: Thin and white (thicker than for Wind-Bi)
  • P: Superficial and tight or Wiry and tight or Deep, tight and slow

TREATS

  • Toe pain
  • Painful obstruction
  • Gout
