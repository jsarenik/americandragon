HERB FORMULAS

DA DING FENG ZHU - 大定風珠 - MAJOR ARREST WIND PEARL

English: Major Arrest Wind Pearl
Also Known As: The Big Pearl for Endogenous Wind.
Bolus for Serious Endogenous Wind-Syndrome

HERBS AND ACTIONS

Pharmaceutical Latin
Pin Yin
Dosage
Actions
Chicken Egg Yolks Ji Zi Huang 2 yolks Nourishes Yin and subdues Wind, especially Internal Wind.
Colla Corii Asini E Jiao 6-10g Tonifies Blood and nourishes Yin.
With Gui Ban and Mu Li, for Muscular spasms and contractures due to Yin Deficiency.
With Zhi Gan Cao, Mai Men Dong and Bai Shao, for Yin Deficiency with chronic, unremitting low-grade fever, five-sole-Heat and an irregular pulse.
Rx. Paeoniae Alba Bai Shao 9-18g Nourishes the Blood, astringes Yin, calms Liver Yang and Liver Wind and alleviates pain.
With Zhi Gan Cao, and Wu Wei Zi, nourishes Yin and softens the Liver.
Rx. Glycyrrhizae  Preparata Zhi Gan Cao 3-12g Moderates spasms, alleviates pain and moderates and harmonizes the harsh properties of other herbs.
With Bai Shao, moderates spasms and alleviates pain, for painful spasms of the leg or abdomen.
Fr. Schisandrae     Wu Wei Zi 6-9g Tonifies the Kidneys, astringes Jing and sweat and generates fluids.
With Mu Li, for sweating.
Rx. Rehmanniae    Sheng Di Huang 10-18g Clears Heat, cools the Blood, nourishes Yin and generates Fluids.
With E Jiao, for Heat from Deficiency.
With Bai Shao, for Blood Deficiency Heat.
Rx. Ophiopogonis      Mai Men Dong 10-18g Nourishes Yin and moistens the Lungs and Stomach.
With Sheng Di Huang, for hematemesis or epistaxis due to Yin Deficiency with Excess Heat.
Sm. Cannabis Huo Ma Ren 6-9g Nourishes Yin and moistens Dryness.
With Sheng Di Huang and Mai Men Dong, for Yin Deficiency with constipation.
Plastrum Testudinis  Gui Ban 9-12g Nourishes Yin, anchors Yang, Benefits the Kidneys, nourishes the Blood and enriches Yin.
With Bie Jia and Mu Li, enriches Yin to anchor Yang.
With Bie Jia and E Jiao, for low grade afternoon fever and night sweats due to Yin Deficiency with ascendant Yang.
Carapax Trionycis Bie Jia 9-12g Nourishes Yin, anchors Yang, invigorates the Blood and heavily anchors, descends, unblocks and facilitates the functions of the Blood vessels.
With Gui Ban, calms the Liver, subdues Yang and extinguishes Wind to treat vertigo and tinnitus associated with Yin Deficiency with Yang Rising and deficient Wind moving internally.
With Gui Ban, Bai Shao and Sheng Di Huang, for extreme injury to Yin leading to internal stirring of Liver Wind and tremors.
With Bai Shao, Sheng Di Huang, E Jiao and Mai Men Dong, for Liver Wind.
Concha Ostreae    Mu Li 9-12g Calms the Liver, benefits Yin and sedates Yang.
With Gui Ban, Bie Jia and Bai Shao, for movement of Liver Wind after a febrile disease manifesting as tremors and spasms.
With Sheng Di Huang and Bie Jia, for Internal stirring of Wind from Deficiency.

FORMULA ACTIONS

  • Nourishes Yin
  • Extinguishes Endogenous Wind

SYNDROMES

  • Endogenous Wind due to Kidney and Liver Yin Deficiency

CLINICAL MANIFESTATIONS

  • Fatigue
  • Muscle spasms with alternating flexion and extension of the extremities
  • Prostration
  • Clonic convulsions
  • Dizziness - worse with stress or anger
  • Insomnia
  • Dream-disturbed sleep
  • Night sweats
  • Sore low back and knees
  • Nocturnal emissions
  • Often the patient is on the verge of shock
  • Low-grade fever
  • Dry skin
  • T: Deep red
  • C: Thin or Peeled
  • P: Weak and faint

  • Hyperthyroidism
  • Coronary artery disease

  • Dyspnea
  • AIDS/HIV
  • Meningitis
  • Clonic convulsions
  • Wilson's disease (hepatocuticular degeneration)
  • Athetosis

  • Tongue atrophy after radiation treatment
  • Urticaria
  • Restless leg syndrome
  • Post-stroke insomnia
  • Chorea minor
  • Encephalitis B
  • Insomnia

  • Perspiration
  • Dry skin

CONTRAINDICATIONS AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with Yin and Fluid Deficiency with vigorous pathogenic Qi.
  • Contraindicated for those with Excess Heat.
HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

NOTES

  • Blend the egg yolks into the strained decoction and take warm.
