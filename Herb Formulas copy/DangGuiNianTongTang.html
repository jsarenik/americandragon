HERB FORMULAS

DANG GUI NIAN TONG TANG - 當歸拈痛湯 - DANG GUI DECOCTION TO PRY OUT PAIN

English: Tangkuei Decoction to Pry out Pain
Also Known As: Tang Kuei and Anemarrhena Combination
Decoction of Angelica Sinensis for Analgesia
Dang Gui Get Beyond the Pain Decoction

HERBS AND ACTIONS

Pharmaceutical Latin
Pin Yin
Dosage
Actions
Rz. seu Rx. Notopterygii Qiang Huo 2.5-15g Expels Wind-Cold-Dampness, unblocks painful obstruction, alleviates pain and guides Qi to the Tai Yang and Du channels.
With Fang Feng, for pain due to External Wind-Dampness.
With Cang Zhu, For a heavy-headed sensation.
Rx. Saposhnikoviae Fang Feng 2-10g Expels Wind-Dampness and alleviates pain.
With Cang Zhu, for pain due to Wind-Dampness Bi with aching joints, heavy limbs and generalized aching discomfort.
With Qiang Huo and Dang Gui, for Wind-Cold-Damp Bi.
Rz. Cimicifugae Sheng Ma 1-6g Releases the exterior, clears Heat and relieves toxicity.
Rx. Puerariae Ge Gen 2-10g Discharges Exterior conditions, releases the muscles, especially of the neck and upper back, relieves Heat and generates fluids.
Rz. Atractylodis Macrocephalae Bai Zhu 2.5-12g Tonifies the Spleen, augments Qi, dries Dampness and promotes water metabolism.
With Cang Zhu, for Damp Cold Painful Obstruction.
With Zhu Ling and Ze Xie, for edema due to Internal Dampness.
Corpus Radicis Angelicae Sinensis Dang Gui Shen 2.5-12g Tonifies the Blood.
Rz. Atractylodis Cang Zhu 2-10g Strongly dries Dampness, tonifies the Spleen, induces sweating, expels Wind-Dampness and clears Dampness from the Lower Jiao.
Rx. Ginseng Ren Shen 2-6g Tonifies Spleen and Stomach Qi, generates Body Fluids and stops thirst.
With Bai Zhu, tonifies the transformation and transportation functions of the Spleen.
(Rx. Codonopsis) (Dang Shen) (6g) (May substitute for Ren Shen)
Rx. Glycyrrhizae Gan Cao 1-15g Tonifies the Spleen, augments Qi, moderates spasms, alleviates pain, clears Heat, relieves Fire toxicity and moderates and harmonizes the harsh properties of other herbs.
Wine-fried Rx. Sophorae Flavescentis Jiu Chao Ku Shen 1-10g Disperses Wind, clears Heat and dries Dampness.
Dry-fried Rx. Scutellariae Chao Huang Qin 2.5-8g Clears Heat and dries Dampness.
With Qiang Huo, for Cold-Dampness at the Exterior with Heat in the Interior.
Wine-washed Rz. Anemarrhenae Jiu Xi Zhi Mu 2.5-9g Clears Heat and drains Fire.
Hb. Artemisiae Scopariae Yin Chen Hao 2.5-15g Clears Damp-Heat from the Liver and Gallbladder, clears Heat, facilitates the resolution of Dampness and clears and releases Dampness in the the exterior.
With Huang Qi, for Damp-Heat skin rashes.
Polyporus Zhu Ling 2.5-10g Promotes urination, leaches out Dampness and dispels Damp-Heat.
With Ze Xie, drains Damp, unblocks and drains urinary pathways and drains Heat from the Bladder.
Rz. Alismatis Ze Xie 2.5-9g Promotes urination and leaches out Dampness.

FORMULA ACTIONS

  • Resolves Dampness
  • Clears Heat
  • Disperses Wind
  • Stops pain
  • Activates the Blood
  • Tonifies Qi and Blood

SYNDROMES

  • Damp-Heat with External Wind Invasion
  • Damp-Heat Bi
  • Atrophy disorder (Wei Syndrome) due to Damp-Heat

CLINICAL MANIFESTATIONS

  • Generalized body aches and pains
  • Irritability
  • Heavy sensation in the shoulder and back
  • Discomfort or a constricted sensation in the area of the chest and diaphragm
  • Multiple sores, carbuncles furuncles or abscesses
  • Redness
  • Itching
  • Persistent swelling
  • Chest pain
  • Fever
  • Thirst
  • Aching and painful extremities
  • Aching and painful joints
  • Unbearable swelling and pain of the lower extremities
  • Shoulder stiffness
  • T: Normal
  • C: Greasy and slightly yellow
  • P: Wiry and rapid or Soft and moderate or Soft, superficial and slow or Slippery and rapid or Deep and tight

CONTRAINDICATIONS AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with Wind-Cold-Damp Bi.
  • Avoid spicy, pungent, oily or greasy foods while taking this formula.
HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

NOTES

  • This is an anti-inflammatory and immune enhancing formula.
