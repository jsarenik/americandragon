HERB FORMULAS

FANG JI HUANG QI TANG - 防己黄芪汤 - STEPHANIA AND ASTRAGALUS DECOCTION

English: Stephania and Astragalus Decoction
Also Known As: Stephania and Astragalus Combination

HERBS AND ACTIONS

Pharmaceutical Latin
Pin Yin
Dosage
Actions
Rx. Astragali Huang Qi 3.8-15g Strengthens the Spleen., tonifies Qi., tonifies Wei Qi, stabilizes the exterior, promotes urination and reduces edema.
With Bai Zhu, for Spleen Qi Deficiency with weakness, lassitude and loose stools.
With Han Fang Ji, for Wind edema with numbness, pain, swelling of the face and extremities, sweating, sensitivity to Wind, and reduced urination.
Rx. Stephaniae Tetrandrae Han Fang Ji 3-12g Promotes urination and reduces edema. (especially in the lower body), expels Wind-Dampness and alleviates pain.
With Huang Qi and Bai Zhu, for Wind-Dampness invasion with Wei Qi Deficiency causing superficial edema, heaviness of the body, and sweating.
Rz. Atractylodis Macrocephalae Bai Zhu 2.3-9g Tonifies the Spleen, tonifies Qi, dries Dampness, stabilizes the exterior and stops sweat.
Fried Rx. Glycyrrhizae Zhi Gan Cao 1.5-6g Tonifies the Spleen, tonifies Qi and moderates and harmonizes the properties of other herbs.
Rz. Zingiberis Recens Sheng Jiang 1-4g Releases the exterior, warms the Middle and adjusts the Ying and Wei.
With Da Zao, alleviates epigastric pain, nausea, and vomiting by strengthening the Spleen, protects Stomach Qi and reduces irritation of the Gastrointestinal tract by other herbs.
Fr. Jujube Da Zao 1-6g Strengthens the Spleen, tonifies Qi and moderates and harmonizes the harsh properties of other herbs.

FORMULA ACTIONS

  • Augments Qi
  • Strengthens the Spleen
  • Releases the Exterior
  • Promotes urination
  • Reduces edema
  • Regulates water circulation

SYNDROMES

  • Wind-Damp Edema
  • Wind-Damp (no edema)
  • Wind-Damp Bi
  • Wind-Damp Accumulation with Wei Qi Deficiency (Lung and Spleen Qi Deficiency)

CLINICAL MANIFESTATIONS

  • Sweating
  • Aversion to Wind
  • Heavy sensation in the body
  • Superficial edema
  • Urinary difficulty
  • Knee pain
  • Abdominal bloating
  • Edema in lower legs
  • Fatigue
  • Edema in women with constitutional Deficiency
  • T: Pale
  • C: White or Swollen and moist
  • P: Floating and weak, or soft and thready

TREATS

  • Rheumatic Heart disease
  • Ascites
  • Acute glomerulonephritis
  • Chronic nephritis
  • Dysuria
  • Cardiac edema
  • Nephritic edema
  • Ephidrosis
  • Periarthritis of the shoulder
  • Hydronephrosis
  • Edema in the legs
  • Perspiration
  • Arthritis
  • Low back pain
  • Fatigue
  • Arthralgia
  • Obesity
  • Testicular hydrocele
  • Oliguria
  • Arthralgia in knees
  • Irregular menstruation
  • Rheumatism
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Muscle pain
  • Lumbar disc disease
  • Bone fractures
  • Idiopathic edema
  • Hydrocele
  • Hircismus
  • Knee pain
  • Edema
  • Strangury

CONTRAINDICATIONS AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with acute Excess type edema.
  • Contraindicated for those with Dampness Obstructing the Protective Yang.
  • Overdose can cause nausea and anorexia.
HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

NOTES

  • It is best to re-administer the formula after a lengthy interval.
