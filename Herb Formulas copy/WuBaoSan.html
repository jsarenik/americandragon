HERB FORMULAS

WU BAO SAN - 五宝散 - FIVE TREASURE POWDER

English: Five Treasure Powder
Also Known As:

HERBS AND ACTIONS

Pharmaceutical Latin
Pin Yin
Dosage
Actions
Stalactitum Zhong Ru Shi 12g Warms Lung Qi, resolves Phlegm, tonifies the Kidneys, tonifies Yang and grasps Qi.
Cinnabaris Zhu Sha 3g Sedates the Heart, calms the Spirit, clears Heat, relieves toxicity and prevents putrefaction.
Margarita Zhen Zhu 6g Sedates the Heart and clears the Liver.
Borneol Bing Pian 3g Clears Heat, drains Fire, resolves toxicity, dissipates nodules and alleviates pain and alleviates itching.
Succinum Hu Po 6g Invigorates the Blood, dissipates Stasis, promotes urination and calms the Spirit.
(Rz. Smilacis Glabrae) (Tu Fu Ling) (500g) (Relieves toxicity, eliminates Dampness and clears Damp-Heat from the skin.)

FORMULA ACTIONS

  • Clears Heat
  • Relieves toxicity
  • Eliminates Dampness
  • Clears Damp-Heat from the skin
  • Invigorates the Blood
  • Dissipates Stasis
  • Sedates the Heart
  • Calms the Spirit
  • Tonifies the Kidneys
  • Tonifies Yang

SYNDROMES

  • Toxic Wind-Damp-Heat with Blood Stagnation and Yang Deficiency

CLINICAL MANIFESTATIONS

  • Primary chancres appear on:
    Men:
    Penis
    Anus
    Rectum
    Women:
    Vulva
    Cervix
    Perineum
    Both:
    Lips
    Tongue
    Buccal mucosa
    Tonsils
    Fingers
    A red papule appears and quickly erodes into a painless ulcer with a lacquer-like base and hard walls.
    It does not bleed.
    When scratched, it exudes a clear serum.
    A red areola may surround the lesion.
  • Secondary Stage:
    Skin rashes
    Some people have lesions affecting other tissues and organs including:
    Eyes
    Bones
    Joints
    Kidneys
    Liver
    Spleen
  • Constitutional symptoms may also be present such as:
    Persistent headache
    Malaise
    Anorexia
    Nausea
    Aching bones
    Fever
    Insomnia
    Jaundice
    Neck stiffness
    Alopecia in patches
    Lesions affecting the mucous membranes often become eroded forming grayish-white circular patches with a red areola which are usually found on the:
    Mouth
    Palate
    Pharynx
    Larynx
    Penis
    Vulva
    Anal canal
    Rectum

TREATS

  • Primary syphilis
  • Secondary syphilis

CONTRAINDICATIONS AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Avoid spicy and greasy foods, beef, lamb, goose, shrimp and alcohol during therapy.
HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

NOTES

  • Grind all of the ingredients except Rz. Smilacis Glabrae Tu Fu Ling into a fine powder. Mix 6g of the powder with 24g of unbleached wheat flour to form Wu Bao San. Administer 0.3g of the powder ten times a day mixed with a decoction of 500g Rz. Smilacis Glabrae Tu Fu Ling in 500ml boiled down to a 250ml decoction.
