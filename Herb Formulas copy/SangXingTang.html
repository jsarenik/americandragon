HERB FORMULAS

SANG XING TANG - 桑杏湯 - MULBERRY LEAF AND APRICOT KERNEL DECOCTION

English: Mulberry Leaf and Apricot Kernel Decoction
Also Known As: Morus and Armeniaca Decoction
Morus and Apricot Kernel Combination
Decoction of Morus Leaf and Apricot Kernel
Decoction of Morus and Apricot Seed

HERBS AND ACTIONS

Pharmaceutical Latin
Pin Yin
Dosage
Actions
Fol. Mori Sang Ye 3-12g Relieves Wind-Heat, clears Lung Heat, moistens Dryness and stops cough.
With Xing Ren, for headache, fever, and dry non-productive cough from External Warm-Dryness disorders.
Sm. Armeniacae Xing Ren 1.5-12g Regulates Lung Qi, stops cough and wheezing, moistens the Intestines and unblocks bowels.
With Sang Ye, for non-productive dry cough due to Wind-Heat.
Fr. Gardenia Zhi Zi 1-10g Clears Heat, reduces Fire, eliminates irritability, drains Damp-Heat, cools the Blood and relieves toxicity.
With Dan Dou Chi, for insomnia and irritability from Deficiency due to Heat lingering in the chest.
Sm. Sojae Preparata Dan Dou Chi 1-10g Expels Wind-Cold or Wind-Heat, eliminates irritability and relieves stuffy sensations in the chest.
Bul. Fritillariae Thunbergii Zhe Bei Mu 1-6g Clears Heat, transforms Phlegm and stops cough.
Rx. Glehniae Bei Sha Shen 2-15g Moistens the Lungs, stops cough, nourishes Stomach Yin, generates fluids, clears Heat and moistens the Exterior.
Fr. Pyri Li Pi 1-6g Moistens the Lungs and stops cough.
With Bei Sha Shen, nourishes Yin and clears Heat

FORMULA ACTIONS

  • Clears and Disperses Wind-Heat and Dryness
  • Moistens the Lungs

SYNDROMES

  • External Wind, Heat and Dryness

CLINICAL MANIFESTATIONS

  • Dry hacking cough
  • Chest pain during severe coughing fits
  • Headache (lighter and more on the surface)
  • Moderate fever
  • Dry throat
  • Dry skin
  • Dry nose
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Headache
  • Thirst
  • Maybe hemoptysis
  • Little sticky or no Phlegm
  • T: Red or Red tip
  • C: Dry, thin and white or Dry, thin and yellow
  • P: Superficial and rapid or Rapid and big or Weak and rapid

TREATS

  • Flu with dry cough
  • Upper respiratory tract infection
  • Measles during the disappearance of the rash
  • Pharyngitis
  • Pertussis
  • Hemoptysis
  • Acute laryngitis
  • Bronchial asthma
  • Fever
  • Loss of voice
  • Sjogren's Syndrome
  • Tongue pain
  • Common cold
  • Acute and chronic bronchitis
  • Bronchiectasis
  • Pulmonary tuberculosis
  • Pleurisy
  • Nasal Dryness
  • Cough
  • Acute tonsillitis
  • COPD
  • Hoarseness
  • Headache
  • Dry skin
  • Sore throat

CONTRAINDICATIONS AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with Yin Deficiency.
