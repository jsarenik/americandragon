POINT: SI-5 (SMALL INTESTINE-5)

NAME: YANGGU

English: Yang Valley
Also Known As: Valley of Yang

LOCATION:

  • In the depression at the ulnar end of the transverse crease, on the dorsum of the wrist, between the styloid process of the ulna and the triquetral bone.
  • At the ulnar border of the wrist, in the depression between the head of the ulna and the triquetral bone.

NEEDLING:

  • Straight or slanted insertion 0.3 to 0.5 cun.
  • Perpendicular insertion 0.3 to 0.5 cun.
  • Moxibustion is applicable.

COMMAND FUNCTIONS:

  • Jing-River point
  • Fire point
  • Horary point

ACTIONS:

  • Clears Heat
  • Reduces swelling
  • Calms the Shen
  • Clears the mind
  • Clears the channel
  • Expels Exterior Damp-Heat

INDICATIONS:

  • Wrist pain
  • Parotitis (Mumps)
  • Fever
  • Insanity
  • Deafness
  • Tinnitus
  • Mania
  • Mad walking
  • Clonic spasm
  • Pain of the chest or lateral costal region, with difficulty breathing
  • Febrile disease with anhidrosis
  • Chills and fever
  • Scabies
  • Convulsions
  • Delirium
  • Tongue stiffness
  • Fright
  • Fainting
  • Facial neuralgia
  • Intercostal neuralgia
  • Excessive talking
  • Varicose veins
  • Swelling of the neck and submandibular region
  • Lockjaw
  • Stiffness of the tongue in babies preventing suckling
  • Tongue thrusting
  • Toothache in both the upper and lower jaws
  • Visual dizziness
  • Redness, swelling and pain of the eye
  • Hand pain
  • Arm pain
  • Shoulder pain with inability to dress oneself
  • Painful hemorrhoids
  • Depression
  • Incoherent speech
  • Dyspnea
  • Gingivitis
  • Bell's Palsy
  • Excessive laughter
  • Flies into a rage
  • Stuttering or stammering
