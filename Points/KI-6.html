POINT: KI-6 (KIDNEY-6)  

NAME: ZHAOHAI

English: Shining Sea
Also Known As: Luminous Sea
Illuminated Sea
Bigger Stream
Sea of Luminescence
Reflection of Dream
Dazzling Sea

LOCATION:

  • 1 cun below the prominence of the medial malleolus, in the groove formed by two ligament bundles.
    Note: the point lies between the tibialis posterior tendon anteriorly and the flexor digitorum longus tendon posteriorly. These tendons may be highlighted by flexing and inverting the foot.

NEEDLING:

  • Oblique insertion directed superiorly 0.3 to 0.5 cun.
  • Straight insertion 0.5 to 1 cun, causing soreness and numbness extending toward the calf and ankle.
  • Puncture perpendicularly 0.3 to 0.5 cun.
  • Moxibustion is applicable.

COMMAND FUNCTIONS:

  • Master (Confluent) point of the Master point of the in Qiao (Motility) channel
  • Luo-connecting point of the Yin Qiao vessel
  • Couple point of the REN channel

ACTIONS:

  • Benefits the throat
  • Nourishes the Kidneys
  • Clears Deficiency Heat
  • Regulates the Yin Qiao vessel
  • Calms the Shen
  • Regulates the Lower Jiao
  • Cools Heat
  • Tonifies Kidney Yin
  • Promotes the function of the uterus
  • Benefits the eyes
  • Cools the Blood
  • Opens the chest

CONTRAINDICATIONS:

NOTES:

  • This is the best point on the Kidney channel to nourish Kidney Yin.
  • This is the main distal point to treat sore throat with a lingering nature that worsens in the evening with tiredness.
  • This is known as the "joyous sleep" point.
  • This point is traditionally used for emergency throat problems (immediately apply pressure).
  • Use this point when you have an Excess or Deficiency of any secretions.
  • This point releases sex hormones.
