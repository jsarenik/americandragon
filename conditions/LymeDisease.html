LYME DISEASE

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • The complete clinical presentation of a patient with Lyme disease can present symptoms like many other diseases, including multiple sclerosis, rheumatoid arthritis, and many others. Because of this shape-shifting tendency, Lyme disease has been dubbed "the great imitator."
  • The symptoms of Lyme disease usually show up days or weeks after a tick bite, though 50% of all LD patients cannot recall a tick bite, and it has been estimated that only 1-3% of all tick bites lead to L D.
  • The tick introduces the pathogen into the blood stream directly.
  • The infection typically yields a blotchy red rash, often in a "bull's eye" pattern of red skin coloration and swelling, about 3-20 days after the tick bite.
  • Several co-infections are commonly found along with LD, including erhlichosis, babiosis, and other forms of bacterium that are transmitted via tick or other insect bites.
  • There may be flu-like symptoms, including:
    Fever
    Headache
    Sore throat
    Nausea
    Fatigue
    Swollen glands
    Stiff neck
    Aching muscles; and
    If unresolved there may be recurrences of fever and, usually after several months:
    Arthralgia, usually in the knees, with swelling and a hot feeling
  • End-stage patients can be severely neurologically compromised, and may resemble neurosyphilis in its clinical presentation:
    Dementia
    Confusion
    Memory loss
    Delusions
    Eventually, complete neurological breakdown

WEI STAGE: SUMMERHEAT WITH DAMP AND SUPERIMPOSED WIND-COLD

Clinical Manifestations
  • Fever and chills
  • Anhidrosis
  • Headache
  • Thirst
  • Flushed face
  • Tightness in the chest
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Fatigue
  • Aching body
  • Nausea
  • Reduced, dark urination
  • T: Red
  • C: White and greasy
  • P: Floating and rapid or Flooding on the right and Small on the left
Treatment Principle
  • Release Exterior Summerheat
  • Release External Cold
  • Clear Heat
  • Resolve Dampness
Herb Formulas
  • Xin Jia Xiang Ru Yin
  • With Heat Flooding the San Jiao: San Shi Tang
Points
  • Bleed UB-40, needle DU-14, DU-16, DU-23, DU-24, GB-20, LI-4, LI-11, LU-7, REN-9, SJ-5, SP-6, SP-9, ST-8, ST-28, Taiyang, UB-12, UB-13, UB-20

TOXIC DAMP-HEAT- (FIRST STAGE)

Clinical Manifestations
  • Erythematous skin rash
  • Pain
  • Swelling in the joints
  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Aversion to Wind
  • Headache
  • Fatigue
  • Severe muscle aches
  • A dry mouth
  • Preference for cold drinks
  • Conjunctivitis
  • Hemorrhages
  • Jaundice
  • Kidney and Liver dysfunction
  • T: Red
  • C: Slimy or greasy yellow
  • P: Floating and rapid or Floating, wiry, slippery and rapid
  • End-stage:
    Dementia
    Confusion
    Memory loss
    Delusions
    Eventually, complete neurological breakdown
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Resolve toxins
  • Cool and invigorate the Blood
Herb Formulas
  • Toxic Fire #1 + 2g Hu Zhang twice daily
  • Liang Xue Bai Du Tang
  • Lyme Disease #1
  • Anti-Spirochete Formula
  • Wu Wei Xiao Du Yin modified
  • For Exogenous pathogens invading the Wei Stage: Yin Qiao San
  • For Summerheat and Damp-Heat misting the three burners (for treatment of skin manifestations of the disease, such as rashes): San Shi San (apply topically)
  • Acute jaundice (this is the manifestation of leptospirosis as liver disease): Er Miao San
  • Summerheat injuring the Lungs: Xin Jia Xiang Ru Yin
  • Heat Invading the Pericardium with Liver Wind moving Internally: (this is the manifestation of leptospirosis as meningitis): Zhen Gan Xi Feng Tang plus An Gong Niu Huang Wan
Points
  • GB-31, LI-11, SP-10, UB-40
    With marked fatigue: + ST-36
    Witha pronounced red rash: + DU-10
    With marked muscle and joint pain: + Ahshi points, SP-9
