DYSMENORRHEA (PAINFUL MENSTRUATION)

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Recurring sharp or dull pain in the lower abdomen or pelvis 1-2 days before, during, or after the period
  • The pain can radiate to the whole abdomen, lower back and anal/genital area (Endometriosis can have a feeling of downward pulling on the anus).
  • The pain generally diminishes after the period ends.
  • The pain can be stabbing, burning, squeezing, dull, down-bearing and in severe cases, can cause nausea, vomiting, cold sweating and fainting.
  • This seems to happen mostly in younger women.
  • Primary Dysmenorrhea: Cramping pain in the lower abdomen occurring just before or during menstruation.
  • Secondary Dysmenorrhea: Menstrual cramps that are caused by an underlying condition, such as a reproductive disease, a structural abnormality or an intrauterine birth.
Treatment Principle
  • Promote blood circulation
  • Warm the uterus
  • Enhancing the flow of Qi and Blood
Herb Formulas
  • Dysmenorrhea #1
  • For membranous dysmenorrhea: Dysmenorrhea #2
  • For Wind-Damp-Heat: Dang Gui Nian Tong Tang
  • For abdominal cramping: Shao Yao Gan Cao Tang
Points

  • Excess: REN-3, UB-32, SP-8
