QI SINKING

Clinical Manifestations
  • First and second stage prolapse
  • Worse with fatigue
  • Sallow complexion
  • Anorexia
  • Four limbs feel tired and heavy
  • Abdominal and epigastric distention after eating
  • Maybe acid regurgitation or vomiting
  • Loose stools
  • Shortness of breath
  • T: Pale and flabby - may have teethmarks
  • C: White
  • P: Even or Soft and thready
Treatment Principle
  • Strengthen the Spleen
  • Reinforce the Center
  • Invigorate Yang
  • Benefit Qi
Herb Formulas
  • Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tangmodified
  • Gui Pi Tang
Points

LIVER QI STAGNATION INVADES THE SPLEEN

Clinical Manifestations
  • Abdominal distention and pain
  • Gas
  • Borborygmus
  • Diarrhea
  • Distention and pain in the sides or flanks
  • Suffocating sensation in the chest
  • Tendency to sigh
  • Melancholy
  • Depression
  • Irritability
  • Inappropriate anger
  • Women:
    Irregular menses
    Dysmenorrhea
    Breast distention and pain before and during menses
    Vaginal prolapse
  • T: Pink
  • C: White and greasy
  • P: Slippery and wiry
Treatment Principle
  • Soothe the Liver
  • Invigorate the Spleen
Herb Formulas
  • Xiao Yao San
Points
  • LIV-3, LIV-13, SP-6, ST-25, ST-36, ST-37

 

KIDNEY DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Second and third stage prolapse
  • Soreness and weakness in the lower back
  • Severe lower abdominal distention
  • Urinary frequency which is worse at night
  • Tinnitus
  • Nocturnal emission
  • Profuse pink menstrual discharge
  • Nocturia
  • Hearing loss
  • Dizziness
  • Forgetfulness
  • Weakness of the lower limbs
  • Lack of spirit
  • T: Pale-red
  • C: Normal
  • P: Deep and frail or Deep and slow
Treatment Principle
  • Tonify Yin and Blood
  • Benefit Yang and Qi
  • Strengthen Kidney Jing
  • Invigorate the Spleen
Herb Formulas
  • Da Bu Yuan Jian
