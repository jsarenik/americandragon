HYPOTENSION, LOW BLOOD PRESSURE

SPLEEN QI DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • A pale, lusterless or sallow complexion
  • Fatigue, often worse after large meals
  • Anorexia
  • Four limbs feel tired and heavy
  • Abdominal and epigastric distention after eating
  • Maybe acid regurgitation or vomiting
  • Loose stools
  • Shortness of breath
  • A weak voice
  • Disinclination to speak
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Dizziness
  • Often, a craving for sweets
  • T: Pale and flabby - may have teethmarks
  • C: White
  • P: Even, forceless and deficient or Thready, forceless, moderate or sometime slow
Treatment Principle
  • Strengthen the Spleen
  • Benefit Qi
Herb Formulas
  • Sheng Ya Tang
Points
  • DU-20, REN-6, SP-6, ST-36
    For Heart Yang Deficiency with palpitations, an empty feeling in the Heart area, cold hand, and purple nails and lips: + PC-6, UB-15, UB-17
    With Blood Deficiency: + UB-17, UB-20
    With Yin Deficiency Fire: + KI-6
    For loose stools and fatigue which are worse after large meals: + SP-3
    With Liver Qi Stagnation: + UB-18, UB-47
  • LIV-13, REN-12, SP-3, SP-5, SP-6, ST-36, UB-20

CENTRAL QI SINKING

Clinical Manifestations
  • Prolapse of one or more organs
  • Sallow complexion
  • Anorexia
  • Four limbs feel tired and heavy
  • Abdominal and epigastric distention after eating
  • Maybe acid regurgitation or vomiting
  • Loose stools
  • Shortness of breath
  • T: Pale and flabby - may have teethmarks
  • C: White
  • P: Even or Soft and thready
Treatment Principle
  • Strengthen the Spleen
  • Benefit Qi
  • Reinforce the Center
  • Invigorate Yang
Herb Formulas
  • Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang
Points
  • DU-20, other points are selected according to the type of prolapse

SPLEEN DEFICIENCY WITH DAMPNESS

Clinical Manifestations
  • Dizziness
  • Headache
  • Chest oppression
  • Nausea
  • Reduced food intake
  • Short, scanty urination
  • Slight edema in limbs, especially lower limbs
  • Maybe anasarca
  • Maybe abdominal distention
  • Loose stools
  • Physical and mental fatigue
  • T: Pale and maybe swollen
  • C: White and moist
  • P: Soft and even
Treatment Principle
  • Warm Yang
  • Strengthen the Spleen
  • Benefit Qi
  • Move water
Herb Formulas
  • Shen Zhu Yi Qi Shen Shi Tang
Points
  • DU-20, REN-12, SP-5, ST-36
    For more marked Spleen Deficiency: + UB-21
    With Blood Deficiency: + UB-17, UB-18, UB-20
    With Liver Qi Stagnation: + LIV-3, PC-6
    With Heart palpitations: + HT-7, PC-6
  • REN-6, REN-9, SP-6, SP-9, ST-28, ST-36, UB-20, UB-22, UB-23

SPLEEN AND STOMACH DEFICIENCY COLD

Clinical Manifestations
  • Mild constant epigastric pain; relieved by warmth and pressure
  • Anorexia
  • Abdominal pain and fullness after meals
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Loose, unformed stools
  • Excessive salivation
  • Cold limbs
  • Lassitude
  • Weakness
  • Sallow or pale, dull complexion
  • Maybe edema
  • Maybe scanty urination
  • Maybe clear, watery, copious vaginal discharge
  • T: Pale
  • C: White and greasy
  • P: Deep, thready and forceless
Treatment Principle
  • Strengthen the Spleen
  • Warm Yang
  • Warm the Center
  • Dispel Cold
Herb Formulas
  • Fu Zi Li Zhong Wan
  • Xiao Jian Zhong Tang
Points
  • Moxa or moxa needle LIV-13, REN-12, SP-3, SP-6, ST-36, UB-20, UB-21

SPLEEN AND KIDNEY YANG DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Dizziness
  • Impaired memory
  • Fatigue
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Body cold
  • Limbs icy
  • Complexion white and shiny
  • Palpitations
  • shortness of breath
  • Reduced food intake
  • Low back, knees and abdomen feel cold and painful
  • Lower abdomen feels cold and painful
  • Loose stools containing undigested food
  • “Cock-crow” diarrhea (occurs at about 5 am)
  • Edema of face and limbs
  • Dysuria
  • Decreased sexual desire
  • Infertility
  • Irregular menstruation
  • Severe: Abdominal ascites
  • T: Pale, moist and swollen with teethmarks
  • C: Thin and white or White and greasy
  • P: Deep, thready, weak and maybe slow or Deep and moderate
Treatment Principle
  • Warm and Reinforce the Spleen and Kidneys
Herb Formulas
  • Jin Gui Shen Qi Wan  modified
Points
  • Moxa or moxa needle DU-20, REN-4, REN-6, ST-36
    For Heart Yang Deficiency with palpitations, an empty feeling in the Heart area, cold hand, and purple nails and lips: + PC-6, UB-15, UB-17
    With Blood Deficiency: + UB-17, UB-20
    With Yin Deficiency Fire: + KI-6
    For loose stools and fatigue which are worse after large meals: + SP-3
    With Liver Qi Stagnation: + UB-18, UB-47 or PC-5
    For impaired memory: + Sishencong
    For severe fatigue: + SP-3
    With severe Heart palpitations: + PC-6
    loose stools with reduced food intake: + SP-9, ST-21
    For low back weakness and pain: + KI-7
  • Moxa or moxa needle REN-4, REN-12, SP-6, SP-9, ST-36, UB-20, UB-23

HEART QI DEFICIENCY

  • Weakness

  • C: White
  • P: Weak or Big and weak or Intermittent

Herb Formulas
  • Si Jun Zi Tang
  • Bao Yuan Dan
  • Zhi Gan Cao Tang
Points
  • HT-7, PC-6, REN-6, REN-14, REN-17, ST-36, UB-15

  • C: White

Points

LIVER BLOOD DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Pale complexion
  • Pale lips
  • Blurred or failing vision
  • Spots before the eyes
  • Dizziness
  • Dry eyes
  • Pale nail beds
  • Irritability
  • Frightens easily
  • Insomnia with much dreaming
  • Tinnitus
  • Numbness of the limbs
  • Light menses or
  • Amenorrhea
  • Late menses
  • T: Pale
  • C: Little
  • P: Thready
Treatment Principle
  • Nourish the Liver
  • Reinforce the Blood
Herb Formulas

  • Bu Gan Tang

  • For menstrual disorders: Hei Xiao Yao San
Points
  • Moxa or needle LIV-3, REN-4, SP-6, ST-36, UB-17, UB-18, UB-20

KIDNEY YANG DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Poor memory
  • Mental and physical fatigue
  • Lumbago: low back feels cold, weak and aching
  • Feeling chilled
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Cold limbs
  • General debility
  • Pale face
  • Urinary frequency with abundant, clear urine or
  • Oliguria with edema
  • Maybe nocturia
  • Lassitude
  • Withdrawn, subdued behavior
  • Knees feel cold and aching
  • Dizziness, worse with exertion
  • Tinnitus
  • Loss of hearing
  • Impotence
  • Spermatorrhea
  • Premature ejaculation
  • Low libido
  • Fluid retention
  • Female infertility ("uterus cold and infertile")
  • Maybe anorexia
  • Maybe loose stools
  • Maybe chronic diarrhea or "cock crow" diarrhea
  • T: Pale and swollen
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Deep and thready - maybe slow
Treatment Principle
  • Warm and Reinforce Kidney Yang
Herb Formulas
  • Jin Gui Shen Qi Wan (Ba Wei Di Huang Wan)
  • You Gui Wan
  • Zhen Wu Tang
  • Niu Che Shen Qi Wan
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Jin Kui Shen Qi Wan
    Ren Shen Lu Rong Wan
    Ge Jie Bu Shen Wan
    Quan Lu Wan
Points
  • Moxa or moxa needle DU-4, KI-3, REN-4, UB-23
    For chronic diarrhea, rectal prolapse and Vanquished Yang: + moxa DU-20, REN-8
  • Moxa or moxa needle DU-20, DU-4, KI-3, REN-4, SI-3, UB-23, UB-62
  • For Kidney Yang Deficiency impotence: moxa or moxa needle DU-4, DU-20, KI-3, REN-4, UB-23
  • For Kidney Yang Deficiency (Cock Crow) Diarrhea: moxa or moxa needle DU-20, KI-3, REN-4, UB-23
