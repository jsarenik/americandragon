TRAUMATIC PARAPLEGIA

OBSTRUCTION OF THE DU AND/OR DAI CHANNELS

Clinical Manifestations
  • Atrophy
  • Paralysis of the limbs
  • Paralysis of the bladder and rectum
Treatment Principle
  • Invigorate Qi and Blood
Herb Formulas
  • Paraplegia #1
  • Yi Sui Ke Li ( Yisui particles) (Manufacturers Huinan Huifa Pharmaceutical Co., Ltd. 【Address] Huinan County, Jilin Province, Chaoyang Zhen Yong Kang, No. 8 )
Points
  • GB-26, GB-30, Shenji, Tiaoyue, Siqiang
  • Electroacupuncture SI-3 to UB-62, Bilateral ST-36 , SP-10 to SP-4
  • 1. Huatoujiaji between the 1-2 spinal processes above and below the level of injury, DU-4, GB-30, UB-23, UB-25, UB-28, UB-32, UB-36, UB-37, UB-40, UB-57, UB-60
    2.  GB-31, GB-34, GB-40, LIV-3, REN-3, REN-4, REN-6, REN-12, SP-6, ST-25, ST-36
    For quadriplegia: + GB-20, LI-4, LI-11, LI-15, SJ-3, SJ-4, SJ-5
    For urinary incontinence:
    Group 1: UB-23, UB-25, UB-28, UB-32, UB-40, UB-57, UB-60
    Group 2: GB-34, LIV-3, REN-3, REN-4, REN-12, REN-6, SP-9, SP-6, ST-25
  • Huatoujiaji below below the level of injury,
    Upper limbs: HT-l, LU-5, LI-4, LI-11, SJ-5
    Lower limbs UB-40, SP-6, GB-34, LIV-3
    Pelvic cavity organ dysfunction: REN-3, REN-4, ST-25
  • Main points (upper limb): GB-21, LI-15, LI-11, LI-4, SJ-3
    Brachial plexus: Yeling, SI-9
    Radial nerve: LU-5, LI-11  
    Median nerve: PC-3, PC-6
  • Main points (lower limb): GB-21, GB-38, GB-34, ST-36, UB-60
    Femoral nerve: LIV-11, SP-12
    Sciatic nerve: UB-52, UB-37
    Common peroneal nerve: Houyangquan
    Superficial peroneal: Lingxia 
    Deep Peroneal: ST-36
    Tibial: UB-54, UB-57
  • Shoulder:
    Abduction (deltoid and supraspinatus): Naoshang, LI-15
    Forward movement (coracobrachialis, anterior insertion of the deltoid): Jianneiling
    Raising the arm backward (latissimus dorsi, teres major, posterior insertion of the deltoid): UB-17, UB-19, SI-9, SJ-14
  • Elbow:
    Lifting the lower arm at the elbow (biceps brachii, brachialis): Gongzhong, electroacupuncture  Gongzhong to Jubi
    External lower arm at the elbow (triceps brachii): Yingshang + electroacupuncture LI-15 to Yingshang
  • Wrist:
    External wrist (ulnar, radial extensors): Yingxia, SJ-9, LI-10, LI-8
    Wrist flexion (ulnar and radial flexors): Bizhong
    Wrist drop: electroacupuncture LI-11 to LI-6
  • Fingers:
    Extended: LI-4 through to PC-8 or electroacupuncture LI-4 to PC-8
    Curled: LI-4 through to PC-8 or electroacupuncture LI-4 to PC-8 or LI-4 through to SI-3 or electroacupuncture LI-4 to SI-3
  • Hip:
    Flexor muscles (psoas major): GB-27 horizontally along the hip or Shuxi, GB-27
    Extensor muscles (gluteus maximus, biceps femoris, semimembranosis, semitendinosis): UB-37 or Zhili, electro-acupuncture UB-53 to GB-30
    Obstruction (gluteus medius, gluteus minimus): Tiaoyue or electro-acupuncture Tiaoyue to GB-29 or electroacupuncture GB-33 to GB-31
    Adduction (adductor magnus, adductor longus, adductor brevis): Jiejian, Jixia, LIV-9 + electroacupuncture Houxuehai to Jiejian
  • Knee:
    Extension (quadriceps femoris): ST-32, Maibu, Jianxi, electro-acupuncture Jianxi to ST-32
    Flexion: UB-37, Zhili or electroacupuncture UB-37 to UB-40
  • Foot:
    Plantar flexion: UB-55, UB-57, Genjin
    Dorsal extension: ST-36, Lishang, Jingxia
    Medial deviation:UB-57, Jiuwaifan #2
    Lateral deviation: GB-34, Jiuneifan, GB-39
    Drop foot: electroacupuncture ST-36 to ST-34
    Rigid foot: electroacupuncture UB-40 to UB-57
    Inward club foot: electroacupuncture GB-34 to GB-39
    Outward club foot: electroacupuncture UB-58 to KI-8
  • Fingers and toes: The twelve Jing-Well points, Shiwang, Qiduan
  • Pelvic cavity organ dysfunction:
    Bladder:
    REN-3, UB-30, Yinbian
    Electroacupuncture REN-3 to REN-4, Bilateral UB-32
    Rectum: SP-15, Tongbian, SJ-6
