PANARTHRALGIA

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Joint pain
  • Swelling and deformity
  • Inability to move joints
  • Pain spreads through all the joints of body
  • Latent: A prolonged history of attacks
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
  • For joint pain as intense as a tiger bite with scary pain in the bones and joints: Panarthralgia #2
Points
  • General pain: GB-30, GB-34, GB-39, LI-4, LI-11, LI-15, SJ-4, SP-21, ST-41, UB-17, UB-62
  • Pain in the shoulder joint: LI-15, SI-10, SJ-14, SJ-15, SJ-19, Jianneiling
  • Pain in the scapula: LI-11, UB-43, SI-12, SI-14, UB-13
  • Pain in the elbow: LI-4, LI-11, LI-10, LU-5, PC-3, SJ-5, SJ-6, SJ-10
  • Pain in the wrist: SJ-4, SJ-5, LI-4, LI-5, LI-11, LU-8, LU-9, SI-3, SI-5
  • Stiffness of the fingers: SI-5, SI-3, LI-4
  • Numbness and pain in the fingers: SI-3, SI-4, LI-3, Bafeng, Baxie, Shixuan
  • Pain in the back: DU-3, DU-12, DU-26
  • Pain in the spine: DU-2, DU-14, DU-16, GB-30, UB-13, UB-14, UB-40, UB-60
  • Lumbar pain: DU-2, DU-3, DU-12, DU-26, UB-23, UB-25, UB-31, UB-32, UB-33, UB-34
  • Hip joint pain: GB-29, GB-30, GB-39, ST-32, UB-54
  • Thigh pain: GB-31, GB-34, ST-33, UB-36, UB-54
  • Knee joint pain: GB-34, SP-9, ST-32, ST-35 (lateral), ST-35 (medial). UB-40, Heding
  • Numbness and pain in the legs: GB-39, ST-36, UB-57, UB-58
  • Ankle pain: GB-40, KI-2, KI-3, KI-6, LIV-4, SP-5, SP-21, UB-60
  • Numbness and pain in the toes: GB-44, LIV-2, SP-4, ST-44, UB-65, Bafeng
  • To calm the mind and ease pain: DU-20, HT-3, HT-7, LI-4, LIV-3, PC-6, Taiyang

WIND-HEAT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Red, swollen, numb fingers
  • Inflammation of throat and/or tongue
  • Fever and chills with more fever than chills
  • Perspiration
  • Cough with sticky yellow sputum
  • Aversion to Wind and Heat
  • Yellow nasal discharge
  • Thirst with desire for cold drinks
  • Red bumps around mouth and lips (herpes simplex)
  • Headache
  • Nasal obstruction
  • Sore, swollen throat
  • Dyspnea
  • Chest pain
  • Dry mouth
  • Rapid breathing
  • Maybe constipation
  • Maybe scanty urine
  • T: Red tip
  • C: Thin and yellow
  • P: Floating and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Disperse Wind
  • Clear Heat
  • Clear the Lungs
Herb Formulas
  • Panarthralgia #1
Points
  • Bleed UB-40, needle Dingchuan, DU-14, DU-16, DU-23, DU-24, GB-20, LI-4, LI-11, LU-5, LU-7, LU-10, REN-22, SJ-5, ST-8, ST-40, Taiyang, UB-12, UB-13
  • For a mild case with cough, fever is not high, slight thirst:
    Bleed UB-40, needle DU-14, GB-20, LI-4, LI-11, LU-7, REN-22, SJ-5, UB-12, UB-13
  • For fear of Cold at the beginning stage: Bitong, DU-16, GB-20, KI-7, LI-4, LI-20, LU-7, SJ-5, UB-12, UB-13, Yintang

WIND-COLD-DAMPNESS

Etiology
  • Yuan Qi Deficiency
  • Irregular lifestyle
  • Excess- Exhausts Qi, Blood, Liver and Kidney
Clinical Manifestations
  • Joint deformation
  • Joints of limbs are sore and painful
  • Excruciating sharp, stabbing joint pain
  • Pain alleviated by warmth and aggravated by Cold
  • The pain is better during the day and worse at night
  • The body is crooked and gaunt
  • The fingers are crooked
  • The joints are like mallets
  • Pain with fixed location
  • The joints cannot be flexed or extended
  • No local redness or Heat
  • Painful area often feels cold to the touch
  • The legs are severely swollen
  • Maybe only the feet are swollen
  • Pain mostly affects low back and lower extremities
  • Ankylosis (stiffness) of joint
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Maybe chills with a slight fever
  • Anhidrosis
  • Frequent nocturnal urination
  • T: Pink or Pale
  • C: Thin and white (thicker than for Wind-Bi)
  • P: Superficial and tight or Wiry and tight - maybe slow
Treatment Principle
  • Warm the channels
  • Activate Qi and Blood.
Herb Formulas
  • Ma Huang Tang
  • Ma Huang Jia Zhu Tang
  • Ge Gen Tang
  • Wu Tou Tang Jia Jian
  • Qu Han Bi Tang
  • Yue Bi Tang
  • With signs of toxicity: Lu Dou Gan Cao Tang
  • Early stage:
    Ma Huang Jia Zhu Tang
    Wu Ji San
  • With exterior Deficiency: Gui Zhi Jia Fu Zi Tang
Points
  • To warm and circulate channels and collaterals:
    LIV-3, moxa LI-4, ST-36, GB-34, GB-31, SJ-5, SJ-4, UB-23, REN-4
    or
    Moxa UB-23, REN-4, GB-39
  • + General points

WIND-COLD-DAMP (ACUTE EXACERBATION STAGE)

Clinical Manifestations
  • When Cold predominates: Chills and Fever
    Aversion to Cold
    Shang Han (anhidrosis) or
    Zhong Feng (sweat)
    Severe joint pain (multiple, successive or simultaneous)
    Decreased range of motion
    Pain worse with Wind and Cold
    Pain better with topical warmth
    T: Pale
    C: Thin and white
    P: Floating, tight and deep
  • When Wind and Cold predominate:
    Fever is high
    No sweat
  • When Wind and Damp predominate:
    Fever is mild
    Non-productive sweat
    Fever lingers
  • Wind-Cold-Damp stirs up latent pathogens on path to the Interior
Treatment Principle
  • Warm Yang
  • Dispel Cold
  • Alleviate Pain
Herb Formulas
  • Wu Tou Tang
  • For joint swelling with Excess Dampness: Wu Ji San
Points
  • General points

HEAT (HEAT PREDOMINANT) BI (RE BI)

Etiology
  • Underlying Yin Deficient constitution with Blood loss
  • Underlying Yin Deficient constitution with chronic disease
  • Underlying Yin Deficient constitution with Damp-Heat
  • Underlying Yin Deficient constitution with Toxic heat
  • Above etiologies cause local redness and Heat and pain which is worse at night
Clinical Manifestations
  • Prolonged history of attacks
  • Joint pain
  • Joints feel contracted
  • Limited motion
  • Slight topical burning sensation with redness and swelling
  • Pain worse at night
  • Maybe no clear joint problems
  • Light-headedness
  • Vertigo
  • Tinnitus
  • Dry mouth
  • Irritability
  • Five Sole Heat
  • Insomnia and Vivid dreams
  • Low back and knees weak
  • T: Shiny and red
  • C: Normal
  • P: Thin and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Nourish Yin and Blood
  • Clear Heat
  • Harmonize the Blood
  • With Excess Heat:
    Nourish Yin
    Decreases Fire
Herb Formulas
  • Liu Wei Di Huang Wan + Si Wu Tang
  • For red, swollen joints: Er Miao San modified
Points

YANG DEFICIENCT BI

Clinical Manifestations
  • Painful, numb bones and joints
  • Pain so excruciating it is like being ponded by a hammer
  • Deformed bones and joints
  • Localized Cold sensation
  • Muscles and sinews atrophied
  • Pale, lusterless face
  • Cold body and limbs
  • Spine bent forward (hunched)
  • Weak, sore low back
  • Weak, sore knees
  • Profuse urination
  • Watery stools
  • Daybreak diarrhea
  • T: Pale
  • C: White P:
  • Deep and Weak
Treatment Principle
  • Warm the Yang
  • Augment Qi
  • Alleviate pain
Herb Formulas
  • Fu Zi Ba Wu Tang  modified

YANG DEFICIENCY (LATENT)

Etiology
  • Chronic Cold-Damp Stagnation
  • Long term disease harms the Kidney Qi (Kidney Yin and Yang)
Clinical Manifestations
  • Joints stiff
  • Impaired motion
  • Pain is mild, dull or non-existent
  • Spontaneous sweat
  • Pale face
  • Light headedness
  • Tinnitus
  • Aversion to cold
  • Sore, weak lower back and legs
  • Profuse clear urine
  • Frequent nocturnal urination
  • Dribbling urine
  • T: Pale
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Deep,thin and weak
Treatment Principle
  • Warm Yang
  • Tonify Qi
Herb Formulas
  • Jin Gui Shen Qi Wan modified
  • For Wind Invasion With Underlying Yang Deficiency:
    Xiao Xu Ming Tang     modified
  • For long-standing Yang Deficiency leading to Damp-Phlegm with swollen, painful and cold joints: Yang He Tang modified

BLOOD DEFICIENCY WITH BLOOD STAGNATION

  • Palpitations

  • Dry skin
  • Dry hair
  • Poor memory
  • Paleness

  • Dark complexion
  • Fixed, palpable masses
  • Bleeding with clots
  • Purple lips
  • Purple nails
  • Dry skin

  • Varicose and spider veins
