HAY FEVER

SHANG HAN-TAI YANG LEVEL: WIND-COLD ATTACKS THE LUNGS

Clinical Manifestations
  • Watery nasal discharge
  • Sneezing
  • Itching eyes and nose
  • Irritated, watery eyes
  • Pale, baggy mucus membranes
  • Red, swollen conjunctiva
  • Edema and vasodiation of the nasal mucus membrane
  • Reduction or loss of the sense of smell
  • Maybe nasal polyps
  • Maybe nasal obstruction
  • Severe chills
  • Mild or no fever
  • Frontal or maxillary headache
  • Generalized discomfort
  • Stiff neck and shoulders
  • Body aches
  • Joint ache
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Hiccups
  • Anhidrosis
  • Gasping
  • Stabbing joint pain
  • Itchy throat
  • Cough
  • Asthma
  • Aversion to Wind and Cold
  • Maybe low back pain
  • Maybe frozen shoulder
  • Maybe dyspnea
  • Maybe epistaxis (severe)
  • T: Normal
  • C: Thin, white
  • P: Superficial and tight
Treatment Principle
  • Relieve Exterior
  • Expel Wind-Cold
  • Warm the Lungs
  • Disperse the Lungs
  • Stop Asthma
  • Transform Phlegm-Fluids
  • Descend Lung Qi
Herb Formulas
  • Ma Huang Tang
  • Jing Fang Bai Du San
  • Xing Su San
  • Xiao Qing Long Tangmodified
  • Gui Zhi Tang
  • Ma Huang Jia Zhu Tang
  • Xin Yi San
  • For pain in the nasal cavity: Jia Wei Xin Yi San
  • If Wind-Cold transforms into Heat: Sang Ju Yin modified
  • Patent Medicines:
      Bi Min Gan Wan (Pe Min Kan Wan)
      Chuan Xiong Cha Tiao Wan
      For signs of Heat:
       Bi Yan Pian
Points
  • LI-4, LI-20, LI-19, UB-7, LU-7, ST-36
  • GB-20, GB-11, LI-20, LI-11, LI-4
  • UB-12, UB-13, GB-20, LU-7, LU-5, LI-4, KI-7, DU-16
  • UB-6, UB-2, SI-3
  • LU-7, LI-4, LI-11, Yintang, LI-20, DU-23, UB-2, Bitong
  • To Expel Wind Cold: GB-20, DU-16, UB-12
  • To Disperse the Lung: UB-13
  • To clear nasal obstruction: LI-20, Bitong, Yintang
  • To Promote Sweat: LI-4, KI-7, LU-7, SJ-5
  • LU-7, LI-4, LI-20, UB-12, UB-13, DU-23, GB-20, Yintang
  • Moxa Yintang
  • With copious mucus: SP-3, ST-40
  • With Heat: LU-10, LI-11

WIND-COLD ATTACKS THE LUNGS WITH DAMPNESS ACCUMULATION

Clinical Manifestations
  • Itching or tickling in the throat
  • Wheezing
  • Sneezing
  • Gasping
  • Cough with expectoration of thin, watery or frothy white sputum
  • General body aches
  • No thirst
  • Dyspnea
  • Headache
  • Body aches
  • A sensation of heaviness
  • Chills with low grade fever
  • Maybe asthma
  • Intolerance of Cold
  • Anhidrosis
  • Blocked and/or runny nose with watery nasal discharge
  • Chest discomfort
  • Neck stiffness
  • Irritability
  • T: Pink
  • C: Thin, white and moist
  • P: Superficial and tight
Treatment Principle
  • Disperse Wind-Cold
  • Induce diaphoresis
  • Dispel Dampness
Herb Formulas
  • Ma Huang Jia Zhu Tang
  • Xiao Qing Long Tang
  • Chinese patent medicines:
    Xiao Qing Long Wan
Points
  • SJ-5, SP-3, ST-40, UB-12, UB-13
  • To Expel Wind Cold: DU-16, GB-20, UB-12, LI-4, SJ-5 (Sedating technique)
  • To Disperse the Lungs: UB-13, LU-7, REN-17
  • To Expel water: SP-6, SP-9, Moxa REN-4, REN-6, DU-4
  • To Resolve Phlegm:  ST-40

WIND-COLD ATTACKS THE LUNGS WITH PHLEGM-FLUIDS

Clinical Manifestations
  • Cough with a heavy sound
  • Choking cough
  • Stuffy and runny nose
  • Copious thin, watery nasal discharge
  • Excessive lacrimation
  • Wheezing with expectoration of copious thin watery or string mucus
  • Orthopnea
  • Thin white sputum
  • Chills and fever
  • Headache
  • Nasal obstruction
  • Itching larynx
  • Aching of limbs
  • Muscle soreness
  • Chilliness
  • Anhidrosis
  • T: Normal
  • C:Thin and white
  • P: Floating or Floating and tense
Treatment Principle
  • Disperse Wind and Cold
  • Warm the Lungs
  • Warm and transform Phlegm Fluids
  • Stop wheezing
Herb Formulas
  • Xiao Qing Long Tang
  • To strengthen Spleen and Kidney Yang and transform Fluids:
    Ling Gui Zhu Gan Tang
    Liu Jun Zi Tang
    Jin Gui Shen Qi Wan
  • Chinese patent medicines:
    Qi Guan Yan Ke Suo Tan Chuan Wan
    Xiao Ke Chuan
    Xiao Qing Long Wan
Points
  • Dingchuan, LU-6, LU-7, PC-6, REN-17, SP-3, ST-40, UB-12, UB-13
  • SJ-5, SP-3, ST-40, UB-12, UB-13

LUNG QI DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Chronic, weak cough
  • Coughing fatigues the patient
  • Continuous cough
  • Cough is aggravated by fatigue, exertion or exposure to Wind
  • Aversion to Wind
  • Sneezing
  • Frequent colds
  • Shortness of breath
  • Clear thin or white mucus
  • Spontaneous perspiration
  • Weary Spirit
  • Catches cold easily
  • Weak, low voice or
  • Reluctance to speak
  • Dry, white face
  • Dry throat
  • Dyspnea on movement
  • Parched mouth
  • T: Pale
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Forceless and weak
Treatment Principle
  • Tonify Lung Qi
  • Transform Phlegm
  • Stop cough
Herb Formulas
  • Bu Fei Tang
  • Bu Fei Tang + Yu Ping Feng San
  • Ren Shen Yang Rong Tang
  • Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang
  • With Heat in the Lungs: Ren Shen Ge Jie San
  • With Spleen Qi Deficiency: Liu Jun Zi Tangmodified
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Bu Zhong Yi Qi Wan
    Ge Jie Da Bu Wan
    Ren Shen Yang Ying Wan
    Ping Chuan Wan
Points
  • LU-1, REN-6, REN-17, ST-36, UB-13
  • REN-6, REN-12, ST-36, UB-42, UB-43
  • LU-9, REN-17, ST-36, UB-13, UB-43
  • LU-7, LU-9, REN-12, SP-6, moxa or moxa needle DU-14, ST-36, UB-13, UB-20, UB-43

LUNG QI AND YIN DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Chronic cough with sparse sputum
  • Sputum difficult to expectorate
  • Sneezing
  • Shortness of breath
  • Spontaneous perspiration
  • Dry mouth and tongue
  • Palpitations with a stifling sensation in chest
  • Fatigue
  • Irritability
  • Thirst
  • Dry throat
  • Insomnia
  • Listless speech
  • T: Red and dry or Pink and dry
  • C: Thin and dry
  • P: Deficient and rapid or Irregular and slow or Thready and deficient or Consistently irregular
Treatment Principle
  • Augment Qi
  • Clear Heat
Herb Formulas

  • Jiu Xian San
  • Due to Heat and Dryness of the Lungs: Qing Zao Jiu Fei Tang

DEFICIENCY COLD

Clinical Manifestations
  • Cold extremities
  • No thirst
  • Dry throat
  • Excessive salivation with spitting up of clear Fluids
  • No coughing
  • Spontaneous sweating
  • A bland taste in the mouth
  • Cold breath
  • Frequent, clear urination
  • Irritability
  • Dizziness
  • Frequent urination
  • Cold limbs
  • A pale complexion
  • Abdominal pain which is alleviated by warmth and pressure and aggravated by Cold
  • T: Pale and moist
  • C: None
  • P: Frail or Deep and slow or Wiry and tight
Treatment Principle
  • Warm the Lungs
  • Strengthen the Stomach
Herb Formulas
  • Gan Cao Gan Jiang Tang
