NEO-NATAL JAUNDICE

GENERAL

Etiology
  • Indigestion
  • Spleen Qi Deficiency due to fatigue or poor Lung function
  • In utero stress causing Liver Qi Stagnation and Heat
  • Transmission of Toxic Heat at conception
  • Mother's diet during pregnancy causing Heat
  • Mother's milk too hot
  • Baby's inherent Damp and hot constitution
  • Stagnant Blood due to birthing trauma causing Stagnant Heat
Clinical Manifestations
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
Points
  • LIV-14, REN-6, REN-12, REN-14, ST-25, ST-36, UB-13, UB-15, UB-18
    Caution: Do not needle,. Use pediatric needles or acupressure. When using herbs, place some powder on the nipple and allow the baby to ingest while suckling.

SPLEEN-DAMPNESS

Clinical Manifestations
  • Loose stools or watery diarrhea
  • Weak extremities
  • Weight loss
  • Distention and a stifling sensation in chest and epigastrium
  • Pale wan complexion
  • Abdominal distention
  • Indigestion
  • Vomiting
  • Fatigue
  • Chills
  • Yin jaundice
  • Maybe vomiting
  • Leukorrhea
  • Reduced appetite
  • Borborygmus
  • Edema
  • Reduced appetite
  • Diarrhea with onset of menstrual flow
  • Cough with copious sputum
  • T: Pale or Pale red
  • C: White - maybe greasy
  • P: Thready and even or Even and Deficient
Treatment Principle
  • Augment Qi
  • Strengthen the Spleen
  • Resolve Dampness
  • Stop diarrhea
Herb Formulas
  • Shen Ling Bai Zhu San
  • Cold-Damp Distresses the Spleen: Li Zhong Tang modified
Points
  • UB-20, UB-22, UB-23, LIV-13, SP-3, SP-5, SP-6, SP-9, ST-28, ST-36, REN-6, REN-9, REN-12

BLOOD STAGNATION

Clinical Manifestations
  • Abdominal masses which are visible when lying down
  • Piercing hypochondriac pain
  • Irritability
  • Indigestion
  • Constipation
  • Dysmenorrhea
  • Amenorrhea
  • Chronic diarrhea
  • Daybreak diarrhea
  • Palpable abdominal masses
  • Irregular menstruation
  • Breast distention
  • Abdominal tumors
  • Hypochondriac distention
  • T: Purple with purple spots on the side
  • C: Normal
  • P: Wiry, choppy and tight
Treatment Principle
  • Invigorate Blood
  • Dispel Blood Stasis
  • Move Qi
  • Alleviates pain
Herb Formulas
  • Ge Xia Zhu Yu Tang
  • Xue Fu Zhu Yu Tang
Points
  • LIV-3, LIV-13, SP-6, ST-36, ST-25, ST-37

COLD-DAMPNESS

Clinical Manifestations
  • Sudden onset of Cold-type abdominal pain
  • The pain typically focuses around or lateral to the umbilical region or lower abdomen
  • Epigastrium cold with pain
  • Cramping
  • The pain is alleviated by warmth and aggravated by pressure
  • Chilliness
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Cold extremities
  • Lack of thirst
  • Clear urine
  • Normal or soft stools
  • Mild cases:
      Constant pain
  • Severe cases:
      Sudden bouts of sharp   pain
      Pain is aggravated by Cold   and relieved by warmth
      and hot drinks
      Cold hands and feet  
  • T: Normal
  • C: White and sticky
  • P: Deep, wiry and slow
  • Cold Penetrates the Stomach and Intestines:
      Pain is sudden and sharp
      Pain relieved by warmth
      Nausea
      Vomiting
      Diarrhea, loose stools
      May be constipation
      T: Pink
      C: White
      P: Deep and tight
  • Retention of Fluid in the Stomach due to Cold:
      Pain is dull
      Pain relieved by warmth
      Succussion sound in the   epigastrium on palpation
      Vomiting of clear, watery
      fluid
      T: Pink
      C: White and sticky
      P: Thready or Thready and
          slow
  • Stomach Deficiency with Stagnation of Cold:
      Pain is dull
      Pain is relieved by warmth
      and pressure
      Vomiting clear, watery fluid
      T: Pale
      C: Thin and white
      P: Deep and slow or Deep
          and thready
Treatment Principle
  • Warm the Interior
  • Dispel the Cold
  • Support the Yang
  • Stop pain
Herb Formulas
  • Liang Fu Wan with modifications
  • Liang Fu Wan + Zheng Qi Tian Xiang San
  • Mild cases: Gan Jiang Ren Shen Ban Xia Wan
  • Severe cases:
      Xiang Sha Liu Jun Zi Tang + Liang Fu Wan
      Si Ni Tang
  • External Cold Attack:
      Li Zhong Tang
      Fu Z i Li Zhong Wan
  • With Constipation: Da Huang Fu Zi Tang
  • With severe pain:  Da Jian Zhong Tang
Points
  • REN-6, REN-12, St-36, SP-4, GB-34, LI-4
    With pain above the navel: REN-10, ST-24
    With pain below the navel: ST-27
    With pain along the sides of the abdomen: LIV-13
    With pain around the navel: ST-25, REN-9, REN-7
    With lower abdominal pain: REN-14
    With severe pain:  SP-6, REN-6
    With abdominal distention    :  SP-4
  • REN-6, REN-12, UB-20, UB-21, ST- 21, ST-36, PC-6, SP-4
  • REN-12, REN-6, ST-25, ST-34, ST-36, ST-37, SP-4, SP-6
    With vomiting:  PC-6
    With severe pain or diarrhea: Strongly moxa REN-8
  • Ear Points: Abdomen, Large Intestine, Small Intestine, Stomach, Spleen, Shenmen, Sympathetic

TOXIC HEAT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Alternating chills and fever
  • Colic
  • Abdominal pain
  • Jaundice
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Toxic shock (acute obstructive suppurative cholecystitis)
    Exhausted Yang:
    Shortness of breath
    Profuse perspiration
    Cold skin
  • Peritonitis
    Tenderness
    Guarding
    Blumberg's sign
  • T: Deep red
  • C: Dry and yellow
  • P: Thready and rapid or Disappearing (low Blood pressure)
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Purge Fire
  • Relieve toxicity
  • Cool the Blood
Herb Formulas
  • Wu Wei Xiao Du Yin
  • Huang Lian Jie Du Tang
  • Qing Ying Tang
Points
  • DU-14, DU-26, GB-24, GB-34, KI-1, LI-11, PC-6, ST-36, UB-19, UB-48
