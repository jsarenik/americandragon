GLOBUS HYSTERICUS(PLUM-PIT QI STAGNATION)

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Globus hystericus is defined as the sensation of having a lump in the throat when there is nothing there. Sometimes simply called globus.

    Globus hystericus is a symptom of some physical disorders such as reflux laryngitis as well as a classic sign of hysterical neurosis, a psychosomatic disorder characterized by a change or loss of physical function (such as blurred vision or paralysis of the legs) that suggests a physical disorder but instead is an expression of a psychological conflict or need.

LIVER QI STAGNATION: PHLEGM AND QI STAGNANT AND BLOCKED

Clinical Manifestations
  • Feeling of obstruction in throat as if a slice of roast meat were stuck there which cannot be coughed up nor swallowed down. This sensation becomes more prominent during emotional upset and subsides when relaxed
  • Swallowing and vomiting are difficult
  • Distention and pain in the sides or flanks
  • Suffocating sensation in the chest
  • Nausea/vomiting
  • A lack of facial luster
  • Tendency to sigh
  • Melancholy
  • Depression
  • Irritability
  • Inappropriate anger
  • Irregular menses
  • Dysmenorrhea
  • Breast distention and pain before and during menses
  • T: Pink
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Slippery and wiry (slippery due to Phlegm)
Treatment Principle
  • Spread Qi
  • Resolve Phlegm
Herb Formulas
  • Ban Xia Hou Po Tang
Points
  • LI-18, LIV-3, PC-5, SI-17, SJ-6
  • LIV-3, PC-6, REN-12, SP-17, ST-36
  • HT-5, LIV-3, REN-12, REN-14, SP-17
