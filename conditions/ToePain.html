WIND-COLD-DAMP WITH WIND PREDOMINANT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Wandering toe pain which is aggravated on exposure to Wind
  • Difficult movement of the toes
  • Migrating pain in the joints especially the :
    Shoulders
    Elbows
    Knees
    Wrists
    Fingers
    Upper back and neck
  • Multiple joints
  • Pain quality changes
  • May move from soreness to numbness to aching
  • Sensitivity varies
  • Pain often appears abruptly, acutely or paroxysmal and then disappears
  • Limited range of motion
  • Chills and fever
  • Aversion to Wind
  • Symptoms are worse in windy weather
  • Sore throat
  • General body aches
  • Maybe perspiration
  • T: Pink
  • C: Thin and white or Sticky and yellow
  • P: Superficial and moderate
Treatment Principle
  • Eliminate Wind
  • Warm Cold
  • Expel Damp
  • Clear the Channels
  • Promote the circulation of Qi and Blood
  • Alleviate pain
Herb Formulas
  • Fang Feng Tang  modified
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Feng Shi Pian
    Tian Ma Qu Feng Bu Pian
    Han Shi Bi Chong Ji
Points
  • Bafeng, GB-40, LI-4, SJ-5, SP-6, UB-12, UB-60
    With fever and chills: + DU-14
    With swelling of the toes and foot: + SP-9
    With redness, swelling and a hot sensation in the toe: + LI-11, ST-44
    With a headache: + GB-20
    With a poor appetite: + SP-4, ST-36

WIND-COLD-DAMP WITH COLD PREDOMINANT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Severe cold toe pain
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Cold extremities
  • Limited movement of the toes in the case of severe pain
  • Excruciating sharp, stabbing joint pain
  • Pain alleviated by warmth and aggravated by Cold
  • Pain with fixed location
  • Few joints are involved
  • The joints cannot be flexed or extended
  • No local redness or Heat
  • Painful area often feels cold to the touch
  • Pain worse at night and better during the day
  • Pain mostly affects low back and lower extremities
  • Pain intense but short lived
  • Pain changes over days or weeks
  • Ankylosis (stiffness) of joint
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Maybe chills with a slight fever
  • Anhidrosis
  • Frequent nocturnal urination
  • T: Pink or Pale
  • C: Thin and white (thicker than for Wind-Bi)
  • P: Superficial and tight or Wiry and tight or Deep, tight and slow
Treatment Principle
  • Warm the channels
  • Dispel Cold
  • Dispel Wind and Damp
  • Invigorate Qi and Blood
Herb Formulas
  • Wu Fu Ma Xin Gui Jiang Tang
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Han Shi Bi Cheng Ji
Points
  • Bafeng, SP-6, UB-12, UB-63, moxa or moxa needle GB-40, LI-4, SJ-5, UB-60
    With swollen feet: + SP-9
    With a cold sensation in the body and cold limbs: + moxa REN-8
    With a runny nose and coughing: + LU-7
    With a headache: + GB-20

WIND-COLD-DAMP WITH DAMP PREDOMINANT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Pain and swelling of the toes with a heavy sensation
  • Limited movement of the toes
  • Numbness and heaviness of the extremities
  • Soreness and fixed pain of joints
  • Stiffness or numbness of the muscles or skin
  • Pain worse on cloudy or rainy days
  • Maybe peripheral or systemic edema
  • Pain is alleviated by pressure and warmth
  • Pain lingers and is recalcitrant to treatment
  • Worse during cloudy or wet weather
  • Limited movement
  • Edema
  • Maybe stifling sensation in chest
  • Maybe anorexia or indigestion
  • Maybe abdominal distention
  • Maybe loose or semi-liquid stools
  • T: Pink or Pale
  • C: Greasy and white
  • P: Slow, moderate and soft
Treatment Principle
  • Expel Damp
  • Clear the channels
Herb Formulas
  • Yi Yi Ren Tang  modified
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Tian Ma Qu Feng Bu Pian
Points
  • Bafeng, GB-40, LI-4, SJ-6, SP-9, ST-40, UB-63, UB-64
    With swollen feet: + SP-3
    With a cold sensation in the body and cold limbs: + moxa REN-8
    With nausea, vomiting or diarrhea: + REN-12, ST-25
    With scanty urination: + KI-3, SP-6
    With a general feeling of heaviness in the body: + UB-22, UB-40

DAMP-HEAT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Pain and swelling of the toes which is aggravated by touch
  • Redness and a burning sensation in the toes
  • Limited movement of the toes
  • Irritability
  • Restlessness
  • A slight fever
  • Poor appetite
  • Scanty, yellow urine
  • constipation or
  • Loose stools
  • A desire for cold drinks
  • T: Normal
  • C: Yellow and greasy
  • P: Slippery and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Dispel Wind
  • Eliminate Dampness
  • Alleviate pain
Herb Formulas
  • Bai Hu Jia Gui Zhi Tang  modified
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Si Miao Wan
    Feng Shi Xiao Tong Pian
Points
  • Bafeng, GB-34, GB-40, GB-41, LI-4, SJ-6, SP-6, SP-9, ST-44, UB-60
    With redness and pain in the toes: + SP-10
    With swollen feet: + DU-14, LI-11
    With generalized pain and body aches: + GB-20
    \With scanty, yellow urination: + REN-3
    With nausea or vomiting and diarrhea: + REN-12, ST-25
    With a general feeling of heaviness in the body: + UB-22, UB-40

WIND-COLD-DAMPNESS WITH LIVER AND KIDNEY DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • A constant but not severe pain in one or more toes
  • Pain worse when walking
  • Rigidity of the toe in the morning
  • Hyperosteogeny (excessive bone growth)
  • Muscular atrophy
  • Low back and knee pain and weakness
  • Fatigue
  • T: Pale
  • C: thin and white
  • P: Thready and weak
Treatment Principle
  • Replenish Qi and Blood
  • Strengthen the Liver and Kidneys
  • Dispel Wind, Cold and Damp
  • Unblock the channels and collaterals
Herb Formulas
  • Du Huo Ji Sheng Tang   
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Hai Ma Bu Shen Pian
    Du Huo Ji Sheng Wan
    Kang Gu Zheng Sheng Pian
Points
  • Bafeng, GB-34, GB-39, KI-3, LIV-3, SJ-6, ST-36
    With swollen toes and feet: + SP-9
    With swelling and possible deformity of the toes due to Qi and Blood Stagnation: + SP-10
    With severe aversion to Cold and a cold sensation in the joints: + moxa the toes
    With restlessness and insomnia: + HT-3, PC-6

WIND-COLD-DAMPNESS WITH QI AND BLOOD STAGNATION

Clinical Manifestations
  • Fixed pain with swelling in the toe(s)
  • Toe(s) purplish
  • Pain worse with pressure and moment
  • Pain worse at night
  • A history of trauma to the toe(s)
  • T: Normal
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Wiry and tight
Treatment Principle
  • Promote the circulation of Qi and Blood
  • Eliminate Blood Stasis
  • Unblock the channels and collaterals
  • Alleviate pain
Herb Formulas
  • Acute stage: Qi Li San  topically
  • Middle and restoration stages: Ba Xian Xiao Yao Tang
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Xiao Huo Luo Dan
    Jin Gu Die Shang Wan
Points
  • Bafeng, GB-34, LI-4, LIV-3, SP-6, SP-10, UB-17
    With restlessness and insomnia: + HT-3, PC-6
    With swollen toes and feet: + SP-3
    With a purplish colored toe: + UB-41, UB-67
