ENDOCRINE DYSCRASIAS, ANOVULATION, LUTEAL PHASE DEFECT

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Endocrine Dyscrasia is an abnormal or unbalanced state of the endocrine system. The endocrine system is made up of a network of glands. These glands secrete hormones to regulate many bodily functions, including growth, metabolism kidney function; blood pressure; cardiovascular function; the body’s response to stress; the immune system; and sexual development and function. Endocrine diseases are common and usually occur when glands produce an incorrect amount of hormones.

LUTEAL PHASE DEFECT

Clinical Manifestations
  • The luteal phase is one stage of menstruation. It occurs after ovulation (when the ovaries release an egg) and before the period starts. During this time, the lining of the uterus normally gets thicker to prepare for a possible pregnancy.

    If there is a luteal phase defect, that lining doesn't grow properly each month. This can make it difficult to become or remain pregnant.

  • TCM assumes that the women suffering this condition suffer from some type of Kidney Deficiency, including:
    Kidney Yin Deficiency:
    Early or late periods
    Scant Blood
    No clots
    Thin vaginal discharge
    T: Red
    C: Little or None
    P: Thready and rapid
    Kidney Yang Deficiency:
    Late menarche
    Delayed period
    Scanty, light colored Blood
    Lack of sexual desire
    Low back and knee soreness and weakness
    T: Pale
    C: White
    P: Deep and slow
    Spleen and Kidney Yang Deficiency:
    Menorrhagia
    Pink Blood
    No clots
    Profuse, thin vaginal discharge
    Fatigue
    Weakness
    Poor appetite
    Loose stools
    T: Pale
    C: White
    P: Deep and weak
    Kidney Yin Deficiency with Liver Qi Stagnation:
    Delayed or irregular menstruation
    Profuse or scanty dark Blood with small clots
    Premenstrual breast distention and pain
    Low back pain
    Distention and pain in the lower abdomen during menstruation
    Profuse thick or thin vaginal discharge
    T: Pale
    C: Thin and white
    P: Wiry

  • Phase 4:
