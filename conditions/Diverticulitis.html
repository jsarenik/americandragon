DIVERTICULITIS

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Diverticulitis is a digestive disease in which pouches within the large bowel wall become inflamed. Symptoms typically include lower abdominal pain of a sudden onset. Onset however may also occur over a few days. In North America and Europe pain is usually on the left side while in Asia it is often on the right. There may also be fever, nausea, diarrhea or constipation, or blood in the stool. Repeated attacks may occur.

YANG MING FU

Clinical Manifestations
  • Abdominal pain (worse with pressure)
  • The skin over the pain may feel warmer to the touch
  • High fever (sometimes rises and falls)
  • Great thirst
  • A dry mouth
  • Profuse perspiration
  • Aversion to Heat
  • Abdominal fullness and distension (worse with pressure)
  • Dryness
  • Constipation (hard dry knots)
  • Dislikes pressure
  • Restlessness
  • Irritability
  • Concentrated urine
  • Mental disturbances:
    Mentally unclear
    Delirious speech
  • T: Red and dry, or black and moist

  • P: Deep, strong and forceful

  • Purge

DAMP-HEAT IN THE LARGE INTESTINE

Clinical Manifestations
  • Diarrhea with tenesmus which intensifies after defecation
  • Dysentery
  • Frequent stools which are small in quantity
  • Stools are yellow or brown, thin, watery, with foul smell or sticky and pasty like jam or sticky fluid or containing pus and blood
  • Burning hot sensation in anus
  • Abdominal pain
  • Severe lateral or central abdominal pain
  • Urine short and red
  • Sustained fever despite profuse perspiration or raging fever in the afternoon or in severe cases, high fever and coma
  • Mouth dry but no desire to drink
  • Body heavy
  • Limbs tired
  • Fullness in chest
  • Lump in epigastrium
  • Anorexia
  • Vomiting
  • T: Red
  • C: Yellow and greasy
  • P: Slippery and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Eliminate toxins
  • Transform Dampness
  • Stop dysentery
Herb Formulas
  • Shao Yao Tang
  • Bai Tou Weng Tang
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Huang Lian Jie Du Wan
    Huang Lian Su Pian
    Chuan Xin Lian Kang Yan Pian
    Long Dan Xie Gan Wan
Points
  • ST-25, ST-37, ST-44, UB-25, LI-4, LI-11, SP-9

QI AND BLOOD STAGNATION

Clinical Manifestations
  • Acute, intermittent, spasmodic lower abdominal pain
  • The pain is usually in the left or right iliac fossa
  • The pain may be quite severe and is worse with pressure
  • There may be rebound pain
  • The abdominal muscles over the site of pain may be tight
  • The pain may have begun over the navel
  • Qi Stagnation predominant: The pain may move around
  • Blood Stagnation predominant: The pain is fixed
  • Abdominal distention
  • Belching
  • Loss of appetite
  • Nausea
  • Maybe vomiting
  • Maybe constipation
  • Maybe a mild fever
  • Maybe chills and fever
  • T: Normal
  • C: Thin and white or Thin and yellow
  • P: Wiry or Tight
Treatment Principle
  • Open the bowels
  • Move Qi
  • Invigorate the Blood
  • Eliminate Blood Stasis
  • Clear Heat
Herb Formulas
  • Da Huang Mu Dan Tang
  • For Liver Qi and Blood Stagnation: Chai Hu Shu Gan Tang modified
  • During pregnancy: Qing Chang Yin
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Niu Huang Qing Huo Wan
    Huang Lian Jie Du Wan
    Chuan Xin Lian Kang Yan Pian
Points
  • Ahshi points on the Stomach channel between ST-36 and ST-37 (strong electro-acupuncture), SP-8, ST-25, ST-37
    With appendix affected: + Lanweixue
    With fever: + LI-4, LI-11, ST-44
    With severe pain: + SP-4, ST-34, ST-43
    With nausea and vomiting: + PC-6, REN-13
    With abdominal distention: + GB-34, SJ-6, SP-14
    With constipation: + LIV-3, LIV-5
    With a history of loss or grief: + LU-3, LU-7, UB-43
  • Ear: Appendix, Large Intestine, Abdomen, Sympathetic, Shenmen

BLOOD STAGNATION

Clinical Manifestations
  • Pain that is fixed, stabbing and /or severe
  • Pan is worse with pressure
  • Dark complexion
  • Fixed, palpable masses
  • Bleeding with clots
  • Purple lips
  • Purple nails
  • Dry skin
  • Blue, dark or purplish discoloration of skin in small or large areas,
  • Varicose and spider veins
  • Cold hands and feet
  • Tendency to hemorrhage dark blood or clots
  • T: Purple, possible purple or red spots
    Red purple if there is Heat
    Blue purple if there is Cold
  • C: White
  • P: Choppy or Wiry and choppy
Treatment Principle
  • Invigorate the Blood
  • Dispel Blood Stasis
  • Spread Liver Qi
  • Unblock the channels
  • Stop pain
Herb Formulas
  • Shao Fu Zhu Yu Tang
  • With Heat: Shao Yao Tang
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Xue Fu Zhu Yu Wan
    Fu Ke Wu Jin Wan
    With bleeding: add Sheng Tian Qi Pian to either of the above formulas
Points
  • SP-6, SP-10, ST-36, UB-17, UB-40
  • Ahshi points over the abdomen, Ahshi points along the Stomach channel between ST-36 to ST-39, LI-4, LIV-3, REN-6, SP-6, SP-8, SP-10, ST-25, UB-17, UB-25
