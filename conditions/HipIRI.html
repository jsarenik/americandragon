HIP INTERNAL ROTATION INJURIES

WIND-COLD-DAMP INVASION

Clinical Manifestations
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Stuffy nose
  • Neck and back spasms
  • Joint pain
  • Headache
  • Anhidrosis
  • Muscular pain
  • Joint swelling
  • Slight fever
  • Slight expectoration of white Phlegm
  • Heaviness of the body and joints
  • Cold feeling in the joints
  • Cough
  • Heaviness of the head
  • Preference of the joints for warmth
  • Limited movement of the joints
  • T: Normal
  • C: Thin and white or Thin, white and greasy
  • P: Superficial and tight
Treatment Principle
  • Warm the channels
  • Activate Qi and Blood.
Herb Formulas
  • Du Huo Ji Sheng Tang
  • With Heat: Jiang Huang Wan
Points
  • Wind: LI-4, SJ--5, UB-12, Ahshi, SP-6, SP-10, GB-40, GB-41
  • Cold: Moxa LI-4, SJ--5, ST-36, UB-60, GB-40
  • Damp: SJ-6, SP-6, SP-9, ST-40, GB-40, UB-63, UB-64
  • Damp-Heat: ST-44, SP-6, SP-9, GB-34, GB-40, UB-60, SJ-6
  • Kidney channel: KI-1, KI-3, (same side) REN-3, HT-7 (opposite side), LU-7, KI-6
  • Electro-acupuncture: UB-23 (+) - SP-11 (-) or KI-9 (-) for 20-30 minutes

LIVER AND KIDNEY DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Painful joints
  • Heavy and painful sensations at fixed locations in lower back and extremities with weakness and stiffness or hypertonicity and immobility
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Attraction to warmth
  • Palpitations
  • Pain in the midline and back during pregnancy
  • Pain worse at night General coldness
  • Sallow complexion
  • Wandering pain
  • Pale complexion
  • Shortness of breath
  • May be paresthesias and numbness
  • Cold pain in the low back and knees
  • Limited movement
  • Soreness, weakness and numbness of the joints
  • Tinnitus
  • Muscle spasms
  • Dizziness
  • T: Pale
  • C: Thin and white or White and moist
  • P: Thin, weak and slow or Deep, thready and weak or slippery
Treatment Principle
  • Disperse Wind-Dampness
  • Stop Bi pain
  • Tonify the Kidneys and Liver
  • Tonify Qi
  • Nourish the Blood
Herb Formulas
  • Du Huo Ji Sheng Tang
  • With Heat: Jiang Huang Wan
Points
  • KI-3, LIV-3, GB-34, GB-39, ST-36, SP-6
  • Kidney channel: KI-1, KI-3, (same side) REN-3, HT-7 (opposite side), LU-7, KI-6
  • Electro-acupuncture: UB-23 (+) - SP-11 (-) or KI-9 (-) for 20-30 minutes

QI AND BLOOD STAGNATION

Clinical Manifestations
  • Qi Stagnation: wandering and distended pain
  • Blood Stagnation: stabbing and fixed pain
Treatment Principle
  • Maintain the flow of Qi and Blood
Herb Formulas
  • Die Da Wan
  • Excess: Shu Jing Huo Xue Tang
  • Deficiency: Du Huo Ji Sheng Tang
  • With Heat: Jiang Huang Wan
Points
  • Ahshi points, SP-10, LI-4, LIV-3, GB-34, SP-6, UB-17
  • Kidney channel: KI-1, KI-3, (same side) REN-3, HT-7 (opposite side), LU-7, KI-6
  • Electro-acupuncture: UB-23 (+) - SP-11 (-) or KI-9 (-) for 20-30 minutes
