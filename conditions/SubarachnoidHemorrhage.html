SUBARACHNOID HEMORRHAGE

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Subarachnoid hemorrhage (SAH) refers to bleeding within the subarachnoid space, which is the area between the brain and the tissues that cover the brain.
    The subarachnoid space is the space where the cerebrospinal fluid circulates, and it’s responsible for protecting the brain from injury by serving as a cushion. A hemorrhage in this space can cause a coma, paralysis and even death.
    This condition can occur quickly, and is often the result of head trauma. This life-threatening condition is also rare. According to the Brain Aneurysm Foundation, SAH accounts for between 0.01 and 0.08 percent of visits to the emergency room.
    Symptoms:
    When SAH develops, it has several symptoms. The main symptom is a sudden, severe headache, which is more intense at the base of the skull. It is often described as the worst headache people have ever experienced. Some people may even feel a popping sensation in their head before the hemorrhage begins.

    There may also be:
    neck pain
    numbness throughout your body
    shoulder pain
    seizures
    confusion
    irritability
    sensitivity to light
    decreased vision
    double vision
    nausea
    vomiting
    rapid loss of alertness

    The symptoms of SAH come on suddenly, and one may lose consciousness quickly. Seek emergency medical attention right away if you experience any of these symptoms combined with a severe headache.

    Causes:
    SAH can occur spontaneously, or as a result of head trauma. Spontaneous SAH is often related to brain aneurysms, which are abnormalities within the brain’s arteries. The most common cause of primary SAH is a berry aneurysm. It’s called a berry aneurysm because it forms a cluster of sac-like pouches in a cerebral vessel that looks like a cluster of berries. These aneurysms swell up and weaken the walls of the arteries over time.
    When an aneurysm erupts, it quickly bleeds and forms a clot. This condition is responsible for most SAH cases. Aneurysmal hemorrhage may occur at any age, but it’s most common between age 40 and 65. Brain aneurysms are more common in women, in smokers and in those with high blood pressure. In some cases, trauma to the brain during an injury can cause aneurysms and result in a subarachnoid hemorrhage.

    Other causes of SAH include:
    bleeding from an arteriovenous malformation (AVM)
    bleeding disorders
    use of blood thinners
    A serious head injury, such as one that occurs in a car crash or when an older person falls and hits their head, can also lead to an SAH.

STOMACH FIRE FLARING

Clinical Manifestations
  • Severe frontal headache
  • The entire head may be painful
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Bad breath
  • Dry mouth
  • Tongue sores
  • Thirst with a desire for cold drinks
  • Insomnia
  • Restlessness
  • Constipation
  • Reddish-yellow urine
  • T: Red
  • C: Yellow and slippery
  • P: Slippery, wiry and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear the Stomach
  • Drain Fire
  • Unblock the channels
  • Stop pain
Herb Formulas
  • San Huang Xie Xin Tang  modified
Points
  • DU-22, DU-23, DU-24, GB-14, LI-4, ST-8, ST-44
    For generalized pain: - DU-24, ST-8 + DU-7, DU-19, DU-21, Sishencong
    For deranged speech: + DU-15, LIV-3

LIVER FIRE

Clinical Manifestations
  • Severe headache mostly located at the vertex and back of the neck or starting at the vertex and migrating to the back of the neck
  • Maybe a forehead headache
  • Maybe ocular pain
  • Maybe generalized head pain
  • Rashness
  • Impatience
  • A quick temper
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Stiff neck
  • Insomnia
  • Restlessness
  • Red face and eyes
  • Dry stools
  • Yellow urine
  • T: Normal
  • C: Thin or thick and yellow
  • P: Wiry and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear the Liver
  • Drain Fire
  • Unblock the channels
  • Stop pain
Herb Formulas
  • Long Dan Xie Gan Tang  modified
Points
  • DU-7, DU-19, DU-21, DU-23, GB-39, LIV-3, Sishencong
    For marked somnolence: + LI-3
    For deranged speech: + DU-15
    For spasms and contractures: + LI-4

BLOOD STAGNATION OBSTRUCTING THE CHANNELS

Clinical Manifestations
  • Enduring headache which does not heal
  • Pain with a fixed location
  • Emotional tension
  • A quick temper
  • Bi-lateral flank pain and distention
  • Maybe a history of traumatic injury to the head
  • T: Dark purplish or Possible static macules or spots
  • C: White
  • P: Wiry and choppy
Treatment Principle
  • Invigorate the Blood
  • Transform Stasis
  • Unblock the channels
  • Stop pain
Herb Formulas
  • Tong Qiao Huo Xue Tang  modified
  • Xue Fu Zhu Yu Tang
  • For a severe headache: Tao He Cheng Qi Tang
Points
  • DU-7, DU-19, DU-21, DU-23, LI-4, LIV-3, Sishencong
    For severe Blood Stasis with a severe headache: + SJ-3, SP-6
    For nausea and vomiting: + PC-6, REN-12
    For severe emotional tension: + PC-5

PHLEGM-HEAT ATTACKING UPWARD

Clinical Manifestations
  • Severe headache
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Dry mouth with no desire to drink
  • A bland, tasteless sensation in the mouth
  • Abdominal distention
  • Poor appetite
  • Heart vexation
  • Insomnia
  • Constipation
  • T: Red
  • C: Thick, yellow and greasy
  • P: Slippery, wiry and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Dispel Phlegm
  • Unblock the channels
  • Stop pain
Herb Formulas
  • Huang Lian Wen Dan Tang modified
Points
  • DU-7, DU-19, DU-21, DU-23, REN-12, Sishencong, ST-40, ST-44
    For a tasteless feeling in the mouth: + PC-6, SP-4

    With fatigue: + ST-36
