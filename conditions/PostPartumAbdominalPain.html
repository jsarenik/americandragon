QI AND BLOOD DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Profuse bleeding
  • Continuous mild abdominal pain
  • Pain slightly relieved by pressure
  • No jerky or colliding pain
  • Dizziness
  • Blurred vision
  • Slight fever
  • General fatigue
  • T: Normal
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Thready and slow
Treatment Principle
  • Warm and replenish Qi and Blood
Herb Formulas
  • Dang Gui Jian Zhong Tang
  • Si Wu Tang modified
  • Chang Ning Tang
Points
  • REN-4, REN-6, SP-6, ST-36, UB-17, UB-20, UB-23

BLOOD STAGNATION

Clinical Manifestations
  • Scant lochia or
  • Immediate cessation of lochia after delivery
  • Lower abdomen hard and painful
  • Paroxysmal spasms of lower abdominal pain which feels as if something were jerking or colliding in the area
  • Worse with pressure
  • Stabbing chest and abdominal pain radiating downward into the low back and flanks or upward into the upper back and arms
  • Difficult menstruation
  • Late menstruation
  • Irregular menstruation
  • Smooth urination
  • Polyuria 
  • T: Dark purple
  • P: Choppy
Treatment Principle
  • Invigorate the Blood
  • Break up Stasis
  • Move Qi
  • Generate new tissue
Herb Formulas
  • Sheng Hua Tang  modified
  • Shi Xiao San
  • Yan Hu Suo San
  • With fever, delirium, nervous agitation and a weak pulse: Da Cheng Qi Tang
Points
  • KI-14, REN-4, SP-6, SP-10, ST-25

  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Weak and slow

  • Warm the channels
  • Regulate the Blood

  • With Blood Stagnation: Sheng Hua Tang
Points
  • REN-4, REN-6, REN-12, SP-6, ST-25, ST-36
