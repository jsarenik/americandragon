GENERAL

  • Inflammatory reaction through the bowel wall
  • Diarrhea
  • Abdominal pain
  • Anemia
  • Weight loss
  • Fistula formation
  • Intestinal blockage
  • Soft, grayish or brown stools
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
Points

YANG MING FU

Clinical Manifestations
  • Abdominal pain (worse with pressure)
  • The skin over the pain may feel warmer to the touch
  • High fever (sometimes rises and falls)
  • Great thirst
  • A dry mouth
  • Profuse perspiration
  • Aversion to Heat
  • Abdominal fullness and distension (worse with pressure)
  • Dryness
  • Constipation (hard dry knots)
  • Dislikes pressure
  • Restlessness
  • Irritability
  • Concentrated urine
  • Mental disturbances:
    Mentally unclear
    Delirious speech
  • T: Red and dry, or black and moist
  • C: Thick yellow or dry yellow
  • P: Deep, strong and forceful
Treatment Principle
  • Purge
Herb Formulas
  • Da Cheng Qi Tang
  • Xiao Cheng Qi Tang
  • Tiao Wei Cheng Qi Tang
  • With Qi and Blood Deficiency: Huang Long Tang
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Niu Huang Qing Huo Wan
    Qing Fei Yi Huo Pian
Points
  • DU-14, LI-4, LI-11, REN-12. ST-25, ST-34, ST-36, ST-37, ST-39, ST-44, SJ-5, SJ-6, UB-25
  • Ahshi points on the Bladder channel between UB-18 and UB-28, ST-25, ST-37, ST-39, ST-44, UB-25
    With severe pain: + ST-34
    With fever: + DU-14, LI-4, LI-11
    With Shao Yang symptoms: + GB-34, GB-39, SJ-5
    With constipation: +SJ-6
    With diarrhea: + LI-11
  • Ear: Abdomen, Subcortex, Large Intestine, Small Intestine, Shenmen, Sympathetic

WEI STAGE: SUMMERHEAT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Acute abdominal pain
  • Fever and chills, fever more pronounced or
  • Tidal fever
  • Fever is unrelieved by sweating
  • Abdominal distention
  • Thirst
  • Sweat
  • Stifling sensation in the chest
  • Restlessness
  • Foggy headaches
  • Irritability
  • Heaviness
  • Nausea
  • Severe diarrhea
  • Scanty urine
  • T: Red tip and edges
  • C: Greasy white or yellow
  • P: Floating or Rapid and soft

  • Resolve Damp

DAMP-HEAT IN THE LARGE INTESTINE

Clinical Manifestations
  • Diarrhea with tenesmus which intensifies after defecation
  • Dysentery
  • Frequent stools which are small in quantity
  • Stools are yellow or brown, thin, watery, with foul smell or sticky and pasty like jam or sticky fluid or containing pus and blood
  • Burning hot sensation in anus
  • Abdominal pain
  • Severe lateral or central abdominal pain
  • Urine short and red
  • Sustained fever despite profuse perspiration or raging fever in the afternoon or in severe cases, high fever and coma
  • Mouth dry but no desire to drink
  • Body heavy
  • Limbs tired
  • Fullness in chest
  • Lump in epigastrium
  • Anorexia
  • Vomiting
  • T: Red
  • C: Yellow and greasy
  • P: Slippery and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Eliminate toxins
  • Transform Dampness
  • Stop dysentery
Herb Formulas
  • Shao Yao Tang
  • Bai Tou Weng Tang
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Huang Lian Jie Du Wan
    Huang Lian Su Pian
    Chuan Xin Lian Kang Yan Pian
    Long Dan Xie Gan Wan
Points
  • ST-25, ST-37, ST-44, UB-25, LI-4, LI-11, SP-9

SPLEEN YANG DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Chronic loose stools or diarrhea which may contain undigested food
  • The stools may be urgent
  • Early morning diarrhea
  • Dark, purplish or pale Blood preceding, following or
  • Black, tarry or Blood-streaked loose stools (the bleeding may also be occult)
  • Dull, persistent abdominal or epigastric cold-pain which improves with warmth and pressure
  • Sallow complexion
  • Anorexia (maybe with vomiting)
  • Four limbs feel tired and heavy
  • Abdominal and epigastric distention after eating
  • Maybe acid regurgitation or vomiting
  • Intolerance to Cold
  • Shortness of breath
  • Cold abdomen
  • Cold limbs
  • Feeling chilled
  • Frequent, clear urination
  • Mental fatigue
  • Weakness
  • Exhaustion
  • Maybe scanty urine with edema
  • Maybe clear, watery vaginal discharge
  • T: Pale, wet and swollen with toothmarks
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Deep and weak or Deep, weak and slow
Treatment Principle
  • Warm the Center
  • Dispel Cold
  • Nourish the Blood
  • Stop bleeding
Herb Formulas
  • With relatively severe bleeding: Huang Tu Tang modified
  • With relatively mild bleeding: Fu Zi Li Zhong Wan modified
  • When the bleeding has stopped:
    Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang
    Si Jun Zi Tang
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    For Spleen Yang Deficiency with severe Cold: Fu Zi Li Zhong Wan
    For Spleen Yang Deficiency: Li Zhong Wan
    For bleeding:
    Yun Nan Bai Yao
    Sheng Tian Qi Pian
Points
  • Moxa or moxa needle DU-20, REN-6, REN-12, SP-1, SP-6, ST-25, ST-36, UB-20, UB-25, needle SP-4
    With Kidney Yang Deficiency: + moxa or moxa needle REN-4, UB-23
  • Moxa box REN-8
  • Moxa LIV-13, REN-12, SP-3, SP-5, SP-6, ST-36, UB-20
  • Ear: Spleen, Stomach, Large Intestine, Small Intestine, Adrenal, Subcortex, Shenmen

SPLEEN AND KIDNEY YANG DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • “Cock-crow” diarrhea (occurs at about 5 am)
  • The diarrhea is often watery with undigested food
  • borborygmus and/or periumbilical pain may precede the diarrhea and ease after defecation
  • The patient may be exhausted
  • Body cold
  • Limbs icy
  • Complexion white and shiny
  • Low back, knees and abdomen feel cold and painful
  • Lower abdomen feels cold and painful, better with warmth and pressure
  • Intolerance of Cold
  • Anorexia
  • Poor digestion
  • Abdominal distention
  • Copious, clear urine
  • Nocturia
  • Fatigue
  • Exhaustion
  • General disinterest
  • Increased desire to sleep
  • Loose stools containing undigested food
  • Edema of face and limbs
  • Dysuria
  • Severe:
    Abdominal ascites
    Fecal incontinence
  • T: Pale, moist and swollen with toothmarks
  • C: Thin and white or White and greasy
  • P: Deep, thready, weak and maybe slow

  • Warm and Reinforce the Spleen and Kidneys

    Fu Zi Li Zhong Wan

BLOOD STAGNATION

Clinical Manifestations
  • Pain that is fixed, stabbing and /or severe
  • Pan is worse with pressure
  • Dark complexion
  • Fixed, palpable masses
  • Bleeding with clots
  • Purple lips
  • Purple nails
  • Dry skin
  • Blue, dark or purplish discoloration of skin in small or large areas,
  • Varicose and spider veins
  • Cold hands and feet
  • Tendency to hemorrhage dark blood or clots
  • T: Purple, possible purple or red spots
    Red purple if there is Heat
    Blue purple if there is Cold
  • C: White
  • P: Choppy or Wiry and choppy
Treatment Principle
  • Invigorate the Blood
  • Dispel Blood Stasis
  • Spread Liver Qi
  • Unblock the channels
  • Stop pain
Herb Formulas
  • Xue Fu Zhu Yu Tang
  • Shao Fu Zhu Yu Tang
  • With Heat: Shao Yao Tang
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Xue Fu Zhu Yu Wan
    Fu Ke Wu Jin Wan
    With bleeding: add Sheng Tian Qi Pian to either of the above formulas
Points
  • SP-6, SP-10, ST-36, UB-17, UB-40
  • Ahshi points over the abdomen, Ahshi points along the Stomach channel between ST-36 to ST-39, LI-4, LIV-3, REN-6, SP-6, SP-8, SP-10, ST-25, UB-17, UB-25
    With Bleeding: + Ahshi points over the Ba Liao on the sacrum
    With Cold: + Moxa
  • Ear: Large Intestine, Distal Segment Rectum, Abdomen, Sanjiao, Shenmen, Adrenal

BLOOD STAGNATION WITH DAMP-HEAT IN THE INTESTINE

  • Purple or black stools which are sticky or tarry
  • bloody or pussy diarrhea
  • Heat
  • Fever
  • Fixed or localized sharp or stabbing abdominal pain (usually in the lower left quadrant) which is worse with pressure
  • Abdominal pain with rigidity
  • Burning around the anus
  • A dark complexion
  • Dark rings around the eyes
  • Purple nails
  • Spider naevi or vesicular abnormalities over the abdomen, face and legs (inner knee and ankle)
  • T: Purple or Brown or Purple stasis spots, maybe dark, distended sublingual veins
  • C: Greasy and yellow
  • P: Wiry or Choppy or Thready

  • Clear Heat
Herb Formulas

TOXIC HEAT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Sudden onset of frequent, explosive diarrhea with copious fresh red, dark red or purple Blood
  • The Blood may contain mucus and pus
  • There may be diarrhea like bloody water
  • High fever (abrupt onset)
  • Thirst
  • Dryness
  • Dehydration
  • Pounding headache
  • Restlessness
  • Irritability
  • Severe abdominal pain
  • Tenesmus
  • Red, purple Blood and pus in the stool
  • Malaise
  • Red lips
  • Diarrhea without eating
  • Prefers cold drinks
  • Trismus
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Severe:
    Delirium
    Confusion
    Muscular spasms
    Convulsions
    Cold limbs
    Skin rashes
    Respiratory problems
  • T: Deep red
  • C: Yellow and dry
  • P: Forceful and rapid (if shock, weakens or disappears) or Slippery and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Disperse Heat
  • Detoxify
  • Cool the Blood
  • Stop dysentery
Herb Formulas
  • Wu Wei Xiao Du Yin
  • Xiao Cheng Qi Tang
  • For Toxic Heat in the Uterus: Yin Qiao Hong Jiang Jie Du Tang
  • With chronic Damp-Heat and Blood Stagnation: Qing Re Jie Du Hua Yu Tang
Points
  • LI-4, LI-11, ST-25, ST-37, ST-44

    With Dampness prominent: + SP-9
    With nausea: + PC-6, REN-12
    With tenesmus : + DU-1, REN-4, UB-30
    For shock: DU-24, DU-26, UB-29
  • KI-7, REN-12, SP-4, ST-25, UB-20, UB-21
  • Ear: Large Intestine, Small Intestine, Subcortex, Sympathetic, Lower Portion of the Rectum
