GASPING

TAI YANG SHANG HAN

Clinical Manifestations
  • Itching or tickling in the throat
  • Cough with expectoration of thin, watery or frothy white sputum
  • Chills with low grade fever
  • Wheezing
  • Sneezing
  • Gasping
  • Anhidrosis
  • No thirst
  • Headache
  • Aversion to Wind
  • Blocked and / or runny nose with clear, watery nasal discharge
  • General body aches
  • Maybe asthma
  • T: Pink
  • C: Thin, white and moist
  • P: Superficial and tight
Treatment Principle
  • Expel Wind
  • Dispel Cold
  • Ventilate the Lungs
  • Resolve Phlegm
Herb Formulas
  • Ma Huang Tang
  • San Ao Tang
  • San You Tang
  • Shen Mi Tang
  • With Qi Stagnation in the Interior: Xiang Su San
Points
  • DU-16, GB-20, LI-4 + LU-7 or LI-4 + KI-7, LU-5, UB-12, UB-13

TAI YANG - YANG MING

Clinical Manifestations
  • Fever
  • Sweating
  • Thirst
  • Dysenteric diarrhea
  • Foul-smelling stools
  • A burning sensation around the anus
  • Gasping
  • Ephidrosis
  • Palpitations
  • Tenesmus
  • Maybe red tinged urine
  • Irritability and Heat in the chest and epigastrium
  • Stiffness and spasms at the back of the neck
  • Dry mouth
  • Dyspnea with perspiration
  • Maybe wheezing
  • T: Red
  • C: Yellow (Greasy)
  • P: Rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Release the Exterior
  • Clear Internal Yang Ming Heat
  • Raise Yang to stop diarrhea
Herb Formulas
  • Ge Gen Huang Qi Huang Lian Tang

LUNG QI DEFICIENCY

  • Shortness of breath
  • Spontaneous perspiration
  • Occasional chills and fever
  • Cough
  • Wheezing with thin, scanty sputum
  • Low energy
  • Gasping
  • Apathy
  • A propensity to catch cold easily
  • Chest oppression
  • Weak and low voice
  • Dyspnea
  • Fatigue
  • Taciturnity
  • Intolerance of Cold
  • Pale face
  • T: Pale
  • C: Thin white
  • P: Weak or Feeble and weak or Big and Deficient

  • Augment Qi
  • Stabilize the exterior
  • Resolve Phlegm
Herb Formulas
  • Bu Fei Tang
  • With acute Cold Accumulation: Wen Fei Tang
Points
  • LU-9, REN-17, ST-36, UB-13, UB-43

LUNG AND KIDNEY QI DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Shortness of breath (difficult to inhale)
  • Dyspnea exacerbated by exertion
  • Asthma (inhalation difficult)
  • Wheezing and gasping for breath
  • Soft, weak voice
  • Spontaneous perspiration
  • Aversion to Wind
  • Emaciated body
  • Exhausted Shen
  • Dispirited
  • Limbs cold
  • Face blue-green-colored
  • Maybe superficial edema of limbs and body
  • Maybe dysuria
  • Maybe stress incontinence on coughing
  • T: Pale, moist and swollen
  • C: White
  • P: Deep and thready or Weak
Treatment Principle
  • Nourish the Lungs
  • Soothe asthma
  • Reinforce the Kidneys to Grasp Qi
Herb Formulas
  • Hei Xi Dan
Points
  • DU-4, KI 3, LU 9, REN-4, REN-6, REN-17, REN-22, ST-36, UB-13, UB-23

PHLEGM FIRE

Clinical Manifestations
  • Paroxysmal gasping coughs with a strong barking sound
  • Thick, difficult to expectorate sputum
  • Noisy inhalation
  • Vomiting of mucus
  • Red eyes
  • Occasional Blood in the sputum
  • T: Red
  • C: Sticky
  • P: Rapid, slippery and forceful
Treatment Principle
  • Stop cough and wheezing
  • Drain Heat from the Lungs
  • Dissolve Phlegm
  • Drain Dampness
  • Detoxify
Herb Formulas
  • Sang Bai Pi Tang
