PURPURA

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Purpura is a condition of red or purple discolored spots on the skin that do not blanch on applying pressure. The spots are caused by bleeding underneath the skin usually secondary to vasculitis or dietary deficiency of vitamin C. They measure 0.3–1 cm (3–10 mm), whereas petechiae measure less than 3 mm, and echymoses greater than 1 cm
