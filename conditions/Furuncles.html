FURUNCLES, BOILS

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • They usually occur on the neck, face, breast, back and buttocks
  • It starts out moist and red
  • Develops quickly into a painful swelling
  • Central necrosis follows
  • Discharge of necrotic tissue with yellow, bloody purulent exudate
  • When there are many recurrent lesions there may be a slight fever
  • Nodular Furuncles:
    Found on the head, neck, face or arm
    Initially    :
    They are very hard, the size of a cherry and have a burning sensation
    Later:
    They become pustulant and yellow with a bloody, purulent exudate
    Severe Cases:
    High fever
    Aversion to Cold
    Nausea
    Vomiting
    Constipation
    Yellow urine
    T: Red
    C: Yellow and greasy
    P: Rapid
  • Multiple Furuncles:
    Usually reddish and painful with a white tip
    They are purulent and recurrent
  • Purulent Folliculitis:
    Usually found in the hair follicles or the lower cheek
    The area around the follicle will be inflamed and have a purulent infection
  • Mastoid-type Furuncles:
    Usually found in the mastoid region
  • They are hare, inflamed and purulent
    When healing, the skin color will become dark purple and hard
    They leave scars, are recurrent and difficult to treat
  • Infantile Multiple Abscesses:
    Usually found on the head
    At first they are the size of a kidney bean and are very hard with a purulent exudate
    They usually leave scars, are connected, recurrent and difficult to treat
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
  • External and vestibular auditory canals and vestibulus nasi: Long Dan Xie Gan Tang
  • Ru Yi Jin Huang San Jia Jian
  • Topical:
    Topical #1
    Yu Huang Gao
    San Huang Gao
    For early-stage: Stir-fry 3g dried Raw Ginger Sheng Jiang until brown. Grind into a powder and mix with vinegar to form into a paste. Apply to the lesion, leaving the head clear.
Points
  • HT-9, LI-11
  • DU-14, UB-15, UB-16, UB-17
