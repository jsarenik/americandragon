AIDS (ACQUIRED IMMUNODEFICIENCY SYNDROME)/HIV (HUMAN IMMUNO-DEFICIENCY VIRUS) / ARC (AIDS RELATED COMPLEX)

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • There are four types:
    1. Lung type
    2. CNS ( Central Nervous System) type
    3. Stomach and Intestine type
    4. Fever type
  • Common symptoms:
    Fever
    Night sweats
    Unexplained weight loss
    Swollen lymph glands in the neck, under the arms or in the groin area
    Excessive fatigue
    Diarrhea
    White spots or unusual blotches in the mouth
    Persistent coughing
    Dyspnea
    Multiple purplish blotches and bumps on the skin
    Loss of hemoglobin producing functions
  • Complications:
    Pneumocystitis carinii
    Pulmonary failure
    Karposi's sarcoma (red or purple nodules which start at the head and neck, develop quickly and move to the lymph nodes, oral cavity, Liver, Spleen, anus, bone marrow and eventually to the whole body)
    Candida albicans
    Skin infections
    Herpes zoster
    Fungal infections
  • Transmitted by:
    Sexual contact
    Blood transfusion
    Injection
    Mother to baby (through the placenta or through nursing)
  • Aids Related complex (ARC):
    Fever
    Diarrhea
    Weight loss
    Skin lesions
    Generalized lymphadenopathy
