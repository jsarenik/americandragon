WIND-COLD ATTACKS THE LUNGS (SEVERE)

Clinical Manifestations
  • Fever and chills (chills predominant)
  • Anhidrosis
  • Cough
  • Wheezing (asthma)
  • Copious, white, stringy, difficult to expectorate sputum
  • Stifling sensation in the chest
  • Lacrimation
  • General heaviness (especially upper body)
  • General body aches
  • No thirst
  • Floating edema (severe)
  • Considerable difficulty breathing while lying down (severe)
  • Intolerance of Cold
  • Periorbital edema
  • Dyspnea
  • Vomiting of frothy saliva during pregnancy
  • Chest distention
  • Dry heaves
  • Decreased urine
  • Subcardiac edema
  • Stridor
  • Occasional chills on the back
  • Hiccups
  • Retching
  • Dysphagia
  • Diarrhea
  • Dysuria
  • Flushed face
  • Dizziness
  • Headache
  • Inflammation of the tear ducts
  • T: Pink
  • C: Moist or White and greasy
  • P: Superficial and tight
Treatment Principle
  • Release the Exterior
  • Transform congested Fluids (Thin Mucus)
  • Transform Phlegm
  • Warm the Lungs
  • Descend Qi
  • Relieve wheezing and cough
Herb Formulas
  • Xiao Qing Long Tang
Points

WIND-COLD ATTACKS THE LIVER CHANNEL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Predisposing condition: Blood Deficiency
  • Cold tears appear on exposure to Wind
  • Emaciation
  • Pale face
  • Pale lips
  • Pale nails
  • T: Pale
  • P: Thready
  • If Yang Deficiency also is present:
    Chills
    Cold extremities
    T: Pale
    C: White and moist
    P: Deep and slow
Treatment Principle
  • Nourish the Blood
  • Expel Cold
Herb Formulas
  • Yang Xu Qu Han Yin
Points

WIND HEAT ATTACKS THE LIVER CHANNEL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Hot tears appear on exposure to Wind
  • Red eyes
  • Eyes feel gritty
  • Dry mouth
  • Dry nose
  • Dizziness
  • Vertigo
  • Tinnitus
  • T: Red
  • C: Thin white
  • P: Wiry or Thready and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Mild cases:
    Cool the Liver
    Expel Wind
  • Severe cases:
    Expel Wind-Heat
    Nourish Yin
    Clear Fire
Herb Formulas
  • Mild cases:
    Ling Yang Jiao San
    Bai Jiang Can San
  • Severe cases:
    Long Dan Xie Gan Tang
Points

KIDNEY AND LIVER YIN DEFICIENCIES WITH KIDNEY YANG DEFICIENCY

Etiology
  • Excess sexual activity weakens Jing and Blood.
  • Continuous crying weakens Yin and fluids.
  • When Yin is weakened, Yang is also damaged leading to Kidney Yang Deficiency.
Clinical Manifestations
  • Frequent watery eyes with cold tears
  • Worse in cold weather
  • Dry, gritty eyes
  • Blurry vision
  • Dizziness
  • Vertigo
  • Weak, aching low back and legs
  • Insomnia
  • Spermatorrhea
  • T: Variable
  • C: Thin
  • P: Variable
Treatment Principle
  • Warm the Liver and Kidneys
  • Nourish Jing and Blood
Herb Formulas
  • Zhu Jing Wan modified + She Xiang San (sniffed)
Points

KIDNEY AND LIVER YIN DEFICIENCY WITH EMPTY FIRE FLARING

Clinical Manifestations
  • Predisposing conditions:
    Kidney and Liver Yin Deficiencies
  • Precipitating factors:

  • Dizziness
  • Blurred vision
  • T: Red
  • C: Little or None
  • P: Thready and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Tonify the Liver and Kidneys
Herb Formulas
  • Jia Wei Dang Gui Yin Zi
  • Qi Ju Di Huang Wan modified
  • Zuo Gui Yin modified
