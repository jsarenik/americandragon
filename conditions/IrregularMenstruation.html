SPLEEN QI DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Irregular menstruation, either early or profuse
  • Either heavy volume or
  • Continual dripping
  • The Blood is pink
  • Sallow complexion
  • Fatigue
  • Anorexia
  • Four limbs feel tired and heavy
  • Abdominal and epigastric distention after eating
  • Maybe acid regurgitation or vomiting
  • Loose stools
  • Shortness of breath
  • Lassitude
  • May be much Blood due to Spleen Not Governing Blood
  • May be little Blood due to Spleen Qi Sinking
  • The Blood is pale-red in color and watery
  • An empty feeling in the lower abdomen
  • Spleen Not Governing Blood:
    Shortness of breath
    Dizziness
    Palpitations
    Functional Uterine Bleeding
    Purpura
    Epistaxis
    Hemafecia
    Hematuria
    Menorrhagia
    Metrorrhagia
  • Spleen Qi Sinking:
    Prolapse of one or more organs
  • T: Pale and flabby - may have teethmarks
  • C: White or Sticky and white
  • P: Even, forceless and deficient or Slow and weak
  • If transportation of nutrients is the main defect:
    Menstruation tends to be late
    Menses decreased
    Menses are generally light-colored and thin
  • If control of the Chong and Ren Meridians is the main defect:
    Menstruation tends to be early or prolonged or
    Menses are excessive
    Menses are generally light-colored and thin
  • If both functions are impaired:
    Menstruation tends to be irregular
    Menses are generally light-colored and thin
Treatment Principle
  • Tonify The Spleen
  • Nourish Middle Jiao Qi
Herb Formulas
