CUSHING'S SYNDROME - HYPERCORTISOLISM

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Cushing's syndrome happens when the body has abnormally high levels of cortisol. This can happen for a variety of reasons, the most common of which is overuse of corticosteroid medications. Symptoms include:
    A a round-shaped face
    Upper body weight gain
    Skin that bruises easily
    Women:
    Increased body hair
    Menstrual irregularities
    Men:
    Erectile and fertility problems
    Children:
    Often obese
    A slowed rate of growth.
  • There is no single definitive test for Cushing's syndrome. In addition to a physical examination, blood, saliva, and urine tests are usually required. After diagnosis, additional tests are needed to identify the cause.
