ULCERATIVE COLITIS

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Passage of watery stool and pus
  • Abdominal pain or tenderness
  • Colic
  • Intermittent or irregular fever
  • May be hemorrhage
  • May be perforation
Treatment Principle

  • Chronic:

Points

TAI YIN EXCESS

Clinical Manifestations
  • Chronic colitis
  • Nausea
  • Clear, frequent urination
  • Lack of thirst
  • Anorexia
  • Vomiting of clear fluid
  • Abdominal distension and pain (sharp)
  • Diarrhea or loose stool
  • Prefers warmth
  • T: Pale or pink
  • C: White
  • P: Slow and tight or Soft
Treatment Principle
  • Regulate the middle
  • Warm the Middle Jiao
  • Disperse Cold
  • Relieve pain
Herb Formulas
  • Gui Zhi Jia Shao Yao Tang
  • Li Zhong Tang
  • With Excess Cold: Fu Zi Li Zhong Tang
  • With a need to purge: Fu Gui Li Zhong Tang
Points
  • For pain and Cold: moxa the abdomen
  • To strengthen the Spleen and Stomach: LIV-13, REN-10, ST-36, UB-20, UB-21 (moxa if severe)
  • For abdominal pain and nausea: PC-6, SP-4, ST-25
  • For frequent, clear urination: moxa REN-4, REN-6, UB-23
  • For diarrhea: moxa REN-8
  • Use few needles with deep insertion and moxa

Clinical Manifestations
  • Knotted constipation or
  • Watery, thin diarrhea
  • Burning hot sensation in the anus
  • Abdominal distention and pain (dislikes pressure)
  • High fever or infantile tidal fever
  • Hands and feet dripping with perspiration
  • Vomiting
  • Irritating thirst
  • Delirium
  • T: Pink, maybe prickles or Red
  • C: Thick, yellow and dry
  • P: Deep and Excess

  • Clear Excess Heat with bitter, cold, strong purgative herbs

  • Xiao Cheng Qi Tang

LARGE INTESTINE DAMP-HEAT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Diarrhea with tenesmus which intensifies after defecation
  • Frequent stools which are small in quantity
  • Stools are yellow or brown, thin, watery, with foul smell or sticky and pasty like jam or sticky fluid or containing pus and Blood
  • Burning hot sensation in anus
  • Abdominal pain
  • Urine short and red
  • Sustained fever despite profuse perspiration or
  • Raging fever in the afternoon
  • Belching
  • A bitter taste in the mouth
  • Flatulence
  • Heartburn
  • Maybe nausea and vomiting
  • Sacral pain
  • Dry, itchy eyes
  • Maybe weakness
  • Maybe dizziness
  • Mouth dry but no desire to drink
  • Body heavy
  • Limbs tired
  • Fullness in chest
  • Lump in epigastrium
  • Anorexia
  • Yellow urine
  • Severe cases:
    High fever and coma
  • T: Red or Pale with red edges
  • C: Yellow and greasy
  • P: Slippery and rapid or Rapid and deep or Thin and soggy
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Transform Dampness
  • Regulate Qi
  • Harmonize the Blood
  • Stop dysentery
Herb Formulas
  • Shao Yao Tang
  • Bai Tou Weng Tang
  • Ulcerative Colitis #1
  • Huai Hua San
  • Zhu Ling Tang
  • Zi Sheng Wan
Points
  • LI-4, LI-11, SP-9, ST-25, ST-37, ST-44, UB-25
  • DU-20, LI-4, REN-12, SP-6, SP-9, ST-25, ST-36, ST-37, UB-25
    To stop bleeding:
    1. moxa SP-1, needle REN-6, SP-9, ST-25, ST-36, UB-17, UB-20, UB-24

  • LI-4, SP-6, SP-9, ST-37, ST-39

INTESTINAL WIND (WIND-HEAT IN THE STOMACH AND INTESTINES)

Clinical Manifestations
  • Bleeding of fresh Blood from the bowel which often precedes the bowel movement
  • The bleeding may follow the passage of stool
  • The bleeding may be alarmingly copious
  • Abdominal pain or
  • Absence of pain, itching, swelling or distention or
  • A burning sensation in the rectum and anus
  • Dry mouth
  • Tendency toward constipation
  • Dry or difficult stools
  • T: Red
  • C: Yellow
  • P: Slippery and rapid or Thready and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat from the Intestines
  • Cool the Blood
  • Stop bleeding
Herb Formulas
  • Huai Hua San modified
Points
  • Ahshi points, DU-1, Erbai, SP-1, ST-25, ST-37, UB-30, UB-32, UB-57
    With pain in the rectum or lower Intestine: + UB-2, UB-52
    With severe bleeding: + PC-4, SP-10
  • Ear:
    1. Large Intestine, Small Intestine, Distal Segment Rectum, Subcortex, Shenmen
    2. Hemorrhoids, Large Intestine, Distal Segment Rectum, Shenmen

SPLEEN YANG DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Chronic loose stools or diarrhea which may contain undigested food
  • The stools may be urgent
  • Early morning diarrhea
  • Dark, purplish or pale Blood preceding, following or
  • Black, tarry or Blood-streaked loose stools (the bleeding may also be occult)
  • Dull, persistent abdominal or epigastric cold-pain which improves with warmth and pressure
  • Sallow complexion
  • Anorexia (maybe with vomiting)
  • Four limbs feel tired and heavy
  • Abdominal and epigastric distention after eating
  • Maybe acid regurgitation or vomiting
  • Intolerance to Cold
  • Shortness of breath
  • Cold abdomen
  • Cold limbs
  • Feeling chilled
  • Frequent, clear urination
  • Mental fatigue
  • Weakness
  • Exhaustion
  • Maybe scanty urine with edema
  • Maybe clear, watery vaginal discharge
  • T: Pale, wet and swollen with toothmarks
  • C: Thin and white

  • Indigestion

  • Moodiness
  • Inappropriate anger
