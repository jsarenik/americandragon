Clinical Manifestations

  • Nasal obstruction

  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Superficial and slow or Moderate and maybe weak
Treatment Principle
  • Create sweat to relieve the Exterior and expel Wind
  • Harmonize Ying and Wei to protect digestion and nourish Wei Qi
Herb Formulas

    Gui Zhi Jia Fu Zi Tang
  • With sustained fever of long duration accompanied by severe chills:
    Gui Zhi Er Yue Bi Yi Tang
  • With slightly severe or severe chills after diaphoresis:
    Gui Zhi Qu Shao Yao Jia Fu Zi Tang
  • If after diaphoresis, there is severe perspiration, marked distress, prolonged thirst, and a large pulse: Bai Hu Jia Ren Shen Tang
  • If no result after diaphoresis with recurring malaria-like (Shao Yang) symptoms (alternating chills and fever and anhidrosis):
    Gui Zhi Er Ma Huang Yi Tang
  • If, after diaphoresis, there is still headache, stiff neck, persistent fever, anhidrosis, fullness beneath the Heart, mild aching, and dysuria: Gui Zhi Qu Gui Jia Fu Ling Bai Zhu Tang
  • If, after diaphoresis, there is gasping without severe fever:
    Ma Xing Shi Gan Tang
  • With a persistent fever for six to seven days, anxiety, intense thirst and vomiting after drinking: Wu Ling San
