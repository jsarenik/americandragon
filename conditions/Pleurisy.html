PLEURISY,PLEURITIS

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Pleurisy is a condition in which the pleura, a membrane consisting of a layer of tissue that lines the inner side of the chest cavity and a layer of tissue that surrounds the lungs, becomes inflamed. Also called pleuritis, pleurisy causes sharp chest pain (pleuritic pain) that worsens during breathing.
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
Points
  • Choose from: GB-34, GB-35, GB-37, HT-3, KI-23, KI-27, LI-7, LIV-3, LIV-13, SI-9, SJ-5, SP-4, SP-21, ST-40, UB-11, UB-12, UB-13, UB-17, UB-43
  • With a headache and fever: DU-13, GB-20, GB-34, LI-4, LU-5, SJ-5, UB-12
  • Dry pleurisy: Ahshi points over the ribs, DU-12, GB-34, GB-41, LU-5, LU-9, UB-12
  • With less fever but the chest is still painful: DU-9, DU-10, DU-13, DU-14, GB-34, LIV-13, SP-6, UB-18

SHAO YANG JING WITH PHLEGM-HEAT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Dry pleurisy (early-stage):
    Alternating chills and fever
    Hypochondriac pain and fullness
    Dry coughing with little or no sputum
    Poor appetite
    T: Normal
    C: Thin and yellow
    P: Wiry and rapid
  • Exudative pleurisy:
    Pleural exudates
    Alternating chills and fever
    Hypochondriac pain and fullness
    Dry coughing with little or no sputum
    Poor appetite
    T: Normal
    C: Thin and yellow
    P: Wiry and rapid
  • Symptoms common to both:
  • Restlessness
  • Irritability
  • Sometimes fatigue
  • Nausea
  • Occasional vomiting
  • Headaches (side of head)
  • Rib pain
  • Achiness
  • Ear infection
  • Hearing difficulty
  • Red eyes
  • Stiff shoulders
  • Throat pain
  • Hip pain
Treatment Principle
  • Dry pleurisy (early-stage):
    Harmonize the Shaoyang
    Clear Heat
    Resolve Phlegm
  • Exudative pleurisy:
    Harmonize the Shaoyang
    Clear Heat
    Dispel Dampness
Herb Formulas
  • Xiao Chai Hu Tang  modified
  • Chai Xian Tang
Points
  • GB-41, SJ-5
  • To harmonize the Liver and Gall Bladder: GB-34, LIV-3, LIV-14, SJ-5
  • To stop nausea and vomiting: PC-6, SP-4, ST-36
  • To treat headache: GB-1, GB-8, GB-14, Taiyang
  • To treat constipation: SJ-6, ST-25
  • To treat fever: DU-13, DU-14
  • To support the San Jiao and Regulate Yin: PC-5, PC-6
  • To support Yin: KI-7
  • To support the Ying: SP-10
  • Head: GB-8, SJ-20, Yintang
  • Eyes: GB-14, GB-20, ST-1, UB-1, UB-2
  • Ear: GB-2, SJ-5, SJ-17, SJ-21
  • Hypochondriac area: GB-34, GB-40
    Ear: Shenmen, Gall Bladder, Point 0

WIND-COLD-DAMP BI WITH DAMPNESS PREDOMINANT

Clinical Manifestations
  • A stifling sensation in chest
  • Numbness and heaviness of the extremities
  • Soreness and fixed pain of joints
  • Stiffness or numbness of the muscles or skin
  • Pain worse on cloudy or rainy days
  • Maybe peripheral or systemic edema
  • Pain is alleviated by pressure and warmth
  • Pain lingers and is recalcitrant to treatment
  • Worse during cloudy or wet weather
  • Limited movement
  • Edema
  • Maybe anorexia or indigestion
  • Maybe abdominal distention
  • Maybe loose or semi-liquid stools
  • T: Pink or Pale
  • C: Greasy and white
  • P: Slow, moderate and soft
Treatment Principle
  • Expel Damp
  • Clear the channels
Herb Formulas
  • Yi Yi Ren Tang
Points
  • SP-6, SP-9 + General points

  • Dry heaves
  • Shortness of breath
  • Headache
  • Abdominal distention
  • Wheezing
  • Fullness in the chest
  • Dyspnea caused by extreme chest and back pain
  • Dizziness
  • Vertigo
  • Asthma
  • Rapid breathing
  • Generalized edema which is worse in the lower body

  • Attack and Dispel water
Herb Formulas

Points
