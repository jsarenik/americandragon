SCHIZOPHRENIA

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Yang Type:
    Hallucinations
    Discussions with imaginary partners
    Delusions
    Paranoia
    Jealousy
    Hate
    Guilt
    Religious delusions
    Strange movements
    Strange clothing
    A tendency to attack others
    Repetitious speech or actions
    Answers do not match questions
    Sentences are not logically connected
    A constant change of topics
  • Yin Type:
    Dull emotional expression
    Dull eyes
    A decrease in self-initiated activity
    Talks little
    Sentences have little content or are unfinished
    Slow response
    Poor hygiene
    Loss of concentration
    Decreased sex drive
    Loner behavior
    Low excitability
    Disregard of social codes
  • Paranoid Subtype:
    Delusions or hallucinations
    N no disorganized or catatonic behavior
  • Disorganized Subtype:
    Disorganized speech and behavior or
    Inappropriate affect
    No catatonic behavior
  • Catatonic Subtype:
    Motor immobility (including posturing) or
    Excessive motor activity
    Extreme negativity
    Repeats words or actions of others
    Undifferentiated Subtype:
    Meets criteria of schizophrenia in general but no specific distinguishing symptoms
    Residual Subtype:
    Symptoms are present but in an undifferentiated form
  • Schizophreniform Disorder:
    Same symptom pattern as schizophrenia
    Symptoms last 1-6 months
