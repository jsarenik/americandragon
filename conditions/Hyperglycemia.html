YANG MING JING WITH QI AND BODY FLUID DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • High fever
  • Profuse sweat
  • Generalized weakness
  • Severe chills
  • Aversion to Heat
  • Red face
  • Severe thirst
  • Thirst may be unquenchable
  • Irritability
  • Anxiety
  • Pruritis
  • Headache
  • Toothache
  • Gum bleeding
  • Epistaxis
  • Dry lips
  • Dry tongue
  • Dry mouth
  • May be sensitive to Cold in the upper back
  • Polyuria
  • Delusions
  • Eye congestion
  • Burning sensation on the skin
  • Itching skin
  • Abdominal fullness without resistance
  • Dry, course skin
  • Listlessness
  • May be heavy labored breathing.
  • Delirious speech (serious cases)
  • Mania (serious cases)
  • T: Red tip and sides
  • C: Yellow, dry or white
  • P: Large and forceless
Treatment Principle
  • Drain Stomach Fire
  • Generate Fluids
  • Alleviate thirst
  • Nourish Qi
Herb Formulas
  • Bai Hu Jia Ren Shen Tang

  • Lassitude

  • C: White or Little or None
  • P: Deficient, weak and thready
Treatment Principle
  • Augment Qi
  • Nourish Yin
  • Generate Fluids
  • Moisten Dryness
  • Alleviate thirst
Herb Formulas
  • Yu Ye Tang
  • With Heat: Yu Quan Wan

YANG MING FU WITH BLOOD STAGNATION

  • Pitting edema
  • Low back pain, aching and heaviness
  • Abdominal distention with full sensations
  • Oliguria
  • Cold limbs
  • Face gray and congested or pale
  • Feeling nervous and cold with mental weariness
  • In severe cases:
    Ascites
    Edema of scrotum

  • C: Thin, white or Thick, white and sticky
  • P: Deep and thready

    Cough with copious, thin watery phlegm
    Asthma with phlegmy sound (wheezing)
    Dyspnea
    Facial edema
    Edema of limbs
    Dysuria

STOMACH AND LUNG YIN DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • High fever (sometimes rises and falls)
  • Great thirst
  • Profuse perspiration
  • Aversion to Heat
  • Abdominal pain (worse with pressure)
  • Abdominal distension
  • Dryness
  • Constipation (hard dry knots)
  • Dislikes pressure
  • Restlessness
  • Irritability
  • Mental disturbance:
    Mentally unclear
    Delirious speech
  • Sharp, stabbing epigastric pain with fixed location
  • Pain is severe after eating
  • Dislike of pressure and heat on epigastrium
  • Pain predominates over distention or
  • Pain but no distention
  • Epigastric rumbling
  • Vomiting purple/black blood
  • Stools with blood like tar (melaena)
  • May be abdominal or epigastric lumps or masses
  • T: Red and dry, or black and moist or Dark purple with purple spots or dots
  • C: Thick yellow or dry yellow
  • P: Wiry and choppy or Deep, strong and forceful
Treatment Principle
  • Drains Heat
  • Breaks up Blood Stasis
  • Purge
Herb Formulas
  • Tao He Cheng Qi Tang
Points
  • For Blood Stagnation: SP-4, SP-6, SP-10, ST-21, ST-34, UB-17
  • For Heat: DU-14, LI-4, LI-11, SJ-5, ST-44
  • For Stagnation: REN-12, ST-34, ST-36, ST-37, ST-39
  • For Constipation: SJ-6, ST-25, UB-25

DRY HEAT WITH BLOOD AND PHLEGM STAGNATION (METABOLIC SYNDROME)

Clinical Manifestations
  • Early-Stage: Food Stagnation + Qi and Blood Stagnation with Heat
    Alternating fever and chills
    Fullness in the chest and hypochondrium (with or without pain)
    Bitter taste in mouth
    Nausea
    Continuous vomiting
    Hard focal distension in the epigastrium
    Fullness and pain in the epigastrium
    Burning diarrhea or no bowel movement
    Despondency
    Slight fidgeting
    Slight irritability
    Headache
    Constipation or discharge of fluids rectally
    Tinnitus
    Diminished hearing
    Diminished vision
    Red eyes
    Manic behavior
    Asthma
    Mental instability
    T: Normal or red
    C: Yellow or Thick and yellow
    P: Wiry and forceful or Wiry tight and forceful
  • Middle-Stage: Both Excess and Deficiency + Qi and Blood or Qi and Yin Deficiency of multiple organs + External Excess or Phlegm Stagnation or Blood stagnation
    Lassitude
    Fatigue
    Shortness of breath
    Dizziness
    Tinnitus
    Palpitations
    Emaciation
    Lack of strength
    Dry, rough eyes
    Possible distended feeling in eyes
    A pale, lusterless face
    Possible malar flush
    Restless
    Insomnia
    Poor memory
    Dry, parched throat
    Tasteless feeling in the mouth
    Low back and knee soreness and
    weakness
    Low grade fever
    Five sole heat
    Possible sweating
    Possible tremors of the hands and tongue
    Scanty intake
    Abdominal distention after meal
    Loose stools
    T: Red
    C: Thin or Peeled
    P: Empty and rapid or Thready and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Early-Stage:
    Relive Stagnation
    Clear Heat
  • Middle-Stage:
    Reduce Excess
    Tonify Deficiency
    Resolve Phlegm
    Invigorate the Blood
    Dispel Stasis
