WIND INVADING THE CHANNELS WITH QI AND BLOOD DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Generalized pains
  • Convulsions
  • Edema
  • Flaccid hemiplegia
  • The patient is unable to recognize friends or identify the source of pain
  • Stiffness with inability to rotate the trunk
  • Confusion
  • Muscular numbness and pain
  • Cough
  • Flushed face
  • Fever
  • Aphasia
  • Dry stools
  • Low-grade fever
  • T: Normal
  • C: Thin and yellow or Yellow and greasy
  • P: Slippery and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Support the Normal Qi
  • Clear Heat
  • Relax muscle tension
  • Nourish the Blood
  • Disperse Wind
  • Dispel Wind-Damp
Herb Formulas
  • Xu Ming Tang

PHLEGM OBSTRUCTS THE HEART ORIFICE: YANG OBSTRUCTION

Clinical Manifestations
  • Sudden collapse with loss of consciousness
  • Lockjaw (trismus)
  • Eyes open
  • Both fists clenched
  • Retention of urine
  • Constipation
  • Stiffness and spasm of limbs
  • Red face
  • Body feels hot
  • Coarse breathing
  • Bad breath
  • Restlessness
  • Involuntary movements
  • Usual sequelae of C.V.A. such as:
    Hemiplegia
    Aphasia
    Facial paralysis
  • T: Pink
  • C: Yellow and greasy
  • P: Slippery, wiry and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Phlegm
  • Open the Orifices
Herb Formulas
  • (Ju Fang) Zhi Bao Dan
  • An Gong Niu Huang Wan
  • Ling (Yang) Jiao Gou Teng Tang modified
Points
  • Bleed the Jing-Well points of the Six Hand channels, needle DU-20, DU-26, KI-1, LIV-3, ST-40

PHLEGM OBSTRUCTS THE HEART ORIFICE: YIN OBSTRUCTION

  • Lockjaw (trismus)

  • Both fists clenched
  • Retention of urine
  • Constipation
  • Stiffness and spasm of limbs

    Aphasia
    Facial paralysis

  • Di Tan Tang

PHLEGM OBSTRUCTS THE HEART ORIFICE: EXHAUSTION SYNDROME

Clinical Manifestations
  • Sudden collapse with loss of consciousness
  • Mouth open and hands relaxed
  • Eyes closed
  • Limbs cold
  • Snoring
  • Perspiration ("flows like oil")
  • Incontinence of urine and feces
  • T: Not described
  • P: Minute and thready - about to collapse or Superficial and big but with no root (with a red face)
Treatment Principle
  • Support Zheng Qi
  • Consolidate Exhaustion
  • Strengthen Qi
  • Return Yang
Herb Formulas
  • Di Huang Yin Zi
  • Aphasia #1
Points
  • Moxa REN-4, REN-6, REN-8, ST-36

LIVER WIND STIRRING INTERNALLY: LIVER YANG (FIRE) GENERATES WIND

Clinical Manifestations
  • Dizziness and vertigo to the point of falling over
  • Headache with a drawing sensation
  • Tinnitus
  • Blurred vision
  • A distended feeling in the eyes
  • A red face and ears
  • Numbness and tremor of limbs
  • Twitching and involuntary writhing of hands and feet
  • Dysphasia
  • Unsteady gait
  • Heart vexation
  • Irascibility
  • With Wind-Stroke:
    Tongue stiffness with aphasia
    Facial paralysis
    Sudden onset of hemiplegia often after an outburst of violent anger and often preceding
    Sudden loss of consciousness
    Deviated mouth and eyes
  • clouded Spirit
  • Maybe coma
  • Sometimes death
  • T: Red
  • C: Slimy
  • P: Thready and wiry or Wiry and slippery
Treatment Principle
  • Nourish Yin
  • Pacify the Liver
  • Subdue Wind
Herb Formulas
  • Tian Ma Gou Teng Yin
  • Xu Ming Tang
  • Zhen Gan Xi Feng Tang
Points
  • Bleed the Jing-Well points of Six Hand Channels, needle DU-20, DU-26, GB-20, GB-34, KI-1, LIV-2, LIV-3, PC-8, UB-18, UB-23
  • GB-30, GB-34, KI-3, LI-4, LI-11, LI-15, LIV-3, SJ-5, SP-6,, ST-36, ST-40, ST-41, UB-60
    For a fever: + DU-14
    For a headache: + GB-20
    For body aches: + REN-12
    For aversion to Cold: + UB-12
    For reduced sleep and profuse dreams: + HT-7
    For palpitations and deficiency vexation: + PC-6
    For night sweats: + HT-6
    (early stage: treat only points on the affected side, chronic: treat bilaterally)

WIND-COLD-DAMP BI - WIND PREDOMINANT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Migrating pain in the joints especially the :
    Shoulders
    Elbows
    Knees
    Wrists
    Fingers
    Upper back and neck
  • Multiple joints
  • Pain quality changes
  • May move from soreness to numbness to aching
  • Sensitivity varies
  • Pain often appears abruptly, acutely or paroxysmal and then disappears
  • Limited range of motion
  • Chills and fever
  • Aversion to Wind
    • Symptoms are worse in windy weather
  • Sore throat
  • General body aches
  • Maybe perspiration
  • T: Pink
  • C: Thin and white or Sticky and yellow
  • P: Superficial and moderate
Treatment Principle
  • Eliminate Wind
  • Warm Cold
  • Expel Damp
  • Clear the Channels
