INSOMNIA

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • An inability to fall or stay asleep
  • Waking in the middle of the night
  • Waking too early in the morning
  • Being unable to fall back asleep
  • Awaking fatigued after a full night's sleep
  • A perception that sleep was not restful, regardless of amount
  • Anxiety
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Drowsiness
  • Fatigue
  • Impaired memory
  • Irritability
  • Apathy
  • Headache
  • Hypertension
  • General malaise
  • Weight gain
  • Dream disturbed sleep
  • Frequency:
    Transient: Lasts only a few days (always less than one week) It generally is the result of stress and requires little or no treatment. It resolves itself after the stressor abates.
    Intermittent: Lasts one to four weeks with visible effects. Usually related to an ongoing major stress situation.
    Daytime alertness is affected.
    Anxiety
    Chronic: Lasts one month or more.
  • Depth:
    Sleep Onset Insomnia: Difficulty falling asleep- sixty or more minutes
    Sleep Maintenance Insomnia: Difficulty staying asleep - waki8ng in the middle of the night even after falling asleep normally.
    Sleep Onset Insomnia: Waking up much earlier than desired.
