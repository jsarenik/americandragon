LOCHIORRHEA, LOCHIORRHAGIA

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Lochiorrhea or lochiorrhagia is an abnormally profuse discharge of lochia which lasts more than twenty days.
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
  • Chinese Patent Medicine:
    Yun Nan Bai Yao
    An Gong Zhi Xue Ke Li
Points
  • Ear: select from: Uterus, Shenmen, Sympathetic, Endocrine, Kidney, Spleen, Liver

QI DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Continuous pale, watery lochia
  • No foul odor
  • Persists for more than twenty days after childbirth
  • A sensation of sinking or prolapse in the abdomen
  • Fatigue
  • Exhaustion
  • Facial pallor
  • T: Pale
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Moderate and weak
Treatment Principle
  • Tonify Qi
  • Stop bleeding
Herb Formulas
  • Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang
Points
  • Moxa needle REN-4, SP-6, ST-36
    For especially profuse lochiorrhagia: + REN-6, DU-20
    For a sinking sensation: + DU-20, REN-12

BLOOD HEAT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Abundant red, thick, foul-smelling lochia
  • Persists for more than twenty days after childbirth
  • Hot flashes
  • Dry mouth and throat
  • T: Red
  • C: Yellow
  • P: Thready and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Nourish Yin
  • Stop bleeding
Herb Formulas
  • Bao Yin Jian
  • Qing Hua Yin
Points
  • KI-10, LIV-6, REN-3, REN-6, SP-10
    For irritability and a restless, agitated sensation in the chest: + LIV-2
    For hot flashes: + KI-3
    For a dry mouth and throat: + KI-6

BLOOD STASIS

Clinical Manifestations
  • Continuous irregular flow of lochia containing purple coots
  • Persists for more than twenty days after childbirth
  • Lower abdominal pain not improved by pressure
  • T: Dark red with purple spots on the edges
  • C: White
  • P: Wiry and choppy or Deep and full
Treatment Principle
  • Invigorate the Blood
  • Transform Stasis
Herb Formulas
  • Sheng Hua Tang  modified
  • Shi Xiao San  modified
Points
  • REN-3, REN-5, SP-8
    For severe lower abdominal pain: + ST-29
    For pain and cold sensations around the umbilicus: + moxa REN-7, REN-8

INCONSOLIDATION OF THE CHONG AND REN CHANNELS

Clinical Manifestations
  • Continuous, slightly yellow lochia
  • Persists for more than twenty days after childbirth
  • A pale or sallow complexion
  • Fatigue
  • Weakness
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Dizziness
  • Blurred vision
  • Palpitations
  • Chest stuffiness
  • Tinnitus
  • T: Pink
  • C: Scant
  • P: Weak, thready and hollow
Treatment Principle
  • Warm the Chong and Ren channels
  • Strengthen astringency
Herb Formulas
  • To tonify Qi and Blood: Shi Quan Da Bu Tang
  • To restore astringency: Mu Li San
  • For collapse of Qi: Decoct and drink Rz. Cimicifugae Sheng Ma with wine.
  • For pain due to Blood Deficiency: Jia Wei Xiao Yao San
  • For weakness due excess hemorrhaging during deliver: Si Wu Tangmodified
