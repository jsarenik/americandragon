OTITIS MEDIA (LABYRINTHITIS)

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • A feeling of distention, fullness, and closure inside the ear
  • Diminished hearing
  • Ear pain
  • May be tinnitus
  • Slight reddening and inward sinking of the tympanic membrane
  • Acute: Fluid or a bulging of the eardrum
  • Chronic:
    The ear drum is greatly sunken
    The ear drum is dull or lusterless
    The ear drum is stuck together
    The ear drum is exhibits inhibited mobility
  • An ear conduction test with a tuning fork is positive for hearing loss
  • Purulent:
    Infection and pus formation in the middle ear
    It may be an acute or a chronic condition
  • Chronic Purulent:
    There is a longstanding and repeated history of purulent discharge from the ear.
    Patients often complain of tinnitus and hearing loss.
    Essentials of diagnosis:
    There is intermittent or continuous purulent discharge from the ear accompanied by various degrees of tinnitus and hearing loss.
    The eardrum is perforated and pus is secreted into the ear canal and middle ear.
    Edematous swelling of the mucous membranes in the middle ear which may protrude in a granulometous appearance (fresh tissue or polyps) which may also fall down into the external auditory meatus.
    Appearance of cottage cheese like material is not uncommon.
    Examination for hearing loss reveals bone conduction deafness and, in severe cases, mixed deafness.
    X-ray studies may reveal bony erosion.
    If there are symptoms such as vertigo, facial paralysis, excruciating headache and chills with high fever, the condition has deteriorated to both an intra and extra cranial infection.
