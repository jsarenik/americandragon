ANKYLOSING SPONDILITIS

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Pelvic Syndrome affecting:
    Sacroiliac joints
    Pain in buttocks
    Occurs in A.M.
    Better with warmth
  • Spinal Syndrome: (precedes deformation stage)
    Stiffness which progresses upward with night or morning pain
    Better after warming up
  • Articular Syndrome:
    a. Peripheral arthritis mostly affecting lower limbs
    b. Arthritis affecting hips and shoulders
  • Inflammation at insertion of the ligaments, tendons and bone capsules (enthesitis)
    a. most commonly affects heel, spinous process and great trochanter
  • Extra-Articular complications:
    Iridocyclitis (inflammation of the iris and ciliary body)
    Anterior uveitis
    Cardiac condition characterized by:
       Aortic insufficiency
       Uremia from amyloidosis (wax-like starch) which may form in any organ    near  the end of a disease
       upper like fibrosis of the lungs
  • Targets young men between ages 20-30 (hereditary predisposition)
  • With Yuan Qi Problem: (Yuan Qi promotes proper function in: the pelvis, the Du Channel and the Yin Qiao and Yang Qiao vessels.)
    R rigidity and stiffness
    Morning pain
    Mostly hips, heels, shoulders and eyes
  • Yang Deficiency (Yin type stagnation):
    Worse at night or in the morning
    Progressive stiffness- moves toward kyphosis
    Better with warmth, massage and movement
  • Cold and Dampness:
    Triggering factors
    Must use prolonged moxa and deep massage (Tuina or Shiatsu)
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
  • For severe pain: Gan Cao Fu Zi Tang
Points
  • Pelvic: DU-4, REN-4, UB-23 moxa REN-8
  • With DU Channel involvement: DU-1, UB-11
  • With involvement of the Yin Qiao and Yang Qiao Channels: GB-29, KI-6, KI-8, UB-31, UB-59, UB-61, UB-62

WIND-COLD

  • Sacroiliac joints
  • Pain in buttocks

KIDNEY YANG DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Chronic lumbago: low back feels cold, weak and aching
  • The symptoms are improved by massage and rest
  • Frequent recurrences
  • The pain is aggravated by exertion, prolonged standing, sexual activity and fatigue
  • Weak, sore legs and knees
  • Knees feel cold and aching
  • Feeling chilled
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Cold limbs
  • General debility
  • Pale face
  • Urinary frequency with abundant, clear urine or
  • Oliguria with edema
  • Maybe nocturia
  • Lassitude
  • Withdrawn, subdued behavior
  • Dizziness
  • Tinnitus
  • Impotence
  • Spermatorrhea
  • Premature ejaculation
  • Low libido
  • Female infertility ("uterus cold and infertile")
  • Maybe anorexia
  • Maybe loose stools
  • Maybe chronic diarrhea or "cock crow" diarrhea
  • T: Pale and swollen
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Deep and thready - maybe slow
Treatment Principle
  • Warm and Reinforce Kidney Yang
Herb Formulas
  • Jin Gui Shen Qi Wan (Ba Wei Di Huang Wan)
  • You Gui Wan modified
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Shen Rong Hu Gu Wan
    Jia Kui Shen Qi Wan
    Ren Shen Lu Rong Wan
    Ge Jie Bu Shen Wan
Points
  • Moxa or moxa needle DU-4, Huatuojiaji when tender, KI-3, REN-4, REN-6, UB-23, UB-25, UB-52, UB-60
  • Moxa or moxa needle DU-4, KI-3, REN-4, UB-23
    For chronic diarrhea, rectal prolapse and Vanquished Yang: + moxa DU-20, REN-8
  • For Kidney Yang Deficiency impotence: moxa or moxa needle DU-4, DU-20, KI-3, REN-4, UB-23
  • For Kidney Yang Deficiency (Cock Crow) Diarrhea: moxa or moxa needle DU-20, KI-3, REN-4, UB-23
