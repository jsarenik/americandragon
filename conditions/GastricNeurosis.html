GASTRIC NEUROSIS, GASTROINTESTINAL NEUROSIS

COLD-DAMP DISTRESSES THE SPLEEN

Clinical Manifestations
  • Distention and fullness of epigastrium and abdomen
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Anorexia
  • Loose stools
  • Diarrhea
  • Reduced sensation of taste
  • No thirst or preference for hot drinks
  • Sticky sensation in mouth
  • Borborygmus
  • Lassitude
  • Yin jaundice
  • Heavy head
  • Maybe edema
  • Maybe copious white vaginal discharge
  • Maybe cold limbs, fear of Cold or feeling chilled
  • T: Pink
  • C: Thick, white and greasy or sticky
  • P: Deep and slow
Treatment Principle
  • Warm Cold
  • Transform Dampness
  • Harmonize the Stomach
  • Strengthen the Spleen
Herb Formulas
  • Ping Wei San
  • Wei Ling Tang
Points
  • Moxa or moxa needle REN-6, REN-12, SP-6, SP-9, ST-36, UB-20
  • Cold-Damp Dysentery: Moxa or moxa needle LI-4, REN-6, REN-12, SP-6, ST-25, ST-37
  • Cold-Damp Diarrhea: Moxa or moxa needle REN-6, REN-12, ST-25, ST-36, ST-37, UB-25
  • Cold-Damp Vaginal Discharge: Moxa or moxa needle GB-26, REN-4, REN-6, SP-6, ST-36, UB-30
  • Cold-Damp Jaundice (Yin Jaundice): Moxa or moxa needle DU-9, GB-24, LIV-13, SP-9, ST-36, UB-19, UB-20 , UB-48

DAMP STAGNATION IN THE SPLEEN AND STOMACH

  • Abdominal pain
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Postpartum abdominal fullness and distention

  • T: Pale and swollen
  • C: Thick, white and greasy
  • P: Slippery and even or Slow or Moderate

  • Strengthen the Spleen and Stomach
  • Harmonize the Middle Jiao
Herb Formulas
  • Jia Wei Ping Wei San
Points

SPLEEN QI DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Sallow complexion
  • Fatigue
  • Anorexia
  • Four limbs feel tired and heavy
  • Abdominal and epigastric distention after eating
  • Maybe acid regurgitation or vomiting
  • Loose stools
  • Shortness of breath

  • C: White
  • P: Even, forceless and deficient

LIVER QI STAGNATION: PHLEGM AND QI STAGNANT AND BLOCKED

Clinical Manifestations
  • Feeling of obstruction in throat as if a slice of roast meat were stuck there which cannot be coughed up nor swallowed down. This sensation becomes more prominent during emotional upset and subsides when relaxed
  • Distention and pain in the sides or flanks
  • Suffocating sensation in the chest
  • Tendency to sigh
  • Melancholy
  • Depression
  • Irritability
  • Inappropriate anger
  • Irregular menses
  • Dysmenorrhea
  • Breast distention and pain before and during menses
  • T: Pink
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Slippery and wiry (slippery due to Phlegm)
Treatment Principle
  • Spread Qi
  • Resolve Phlegm
Herb Formulas
  • Ban Xia Hou Po Tang
Points
  • LI-18, LIV-3, PC-5, SI-17, SJ-6

LIVER QI STAGNATION INVADING THE SPLEEN WITH CONCURRENT WIND-COLD INVASION

Clinical Manifestations
  • Recurrent borborygmus
  • Diarrhea with pain (starts with the urge to defecate and subsides on completion)
  • Patient is more comfortable after each bowel movement
  • Recurs during times of stress
  • Diarrhea is small in amount
  • Recurrent abdominal pain
  • T: Pink
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Wiry and even or Thready and wiry (maybe transposed)
Treatment Principle
  • Spread Liver Qi
  • Tonify the Spleen
  • Expel Dampness
  • Expel Wind
  • Stop diarrhea
Herb Formulas
  • Tong Xie Yao Fang
Points
