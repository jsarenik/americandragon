WEI SYNDROME DUE TO SPLEEN AND STOMACH QI DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Gradual onset of flaccidity and weakness of the extremities aggravated by exertion
  • Gradual loss of muscle mass
  • Hysterical paralysis
  • Periodic paralysis
  • Facial paralysis
  • Sallow complexion
  • Facial puffiness
  • Fatigue
  • Lassitude
  • Anorexia
  • No desire for food and drink
  • Frequent belching
  • Borborygmus
  • Loss of sensation of taste
  • Four limbs feel tired and heavy
  • Abdominal and epigastric distention after eating
  • Maybe acid regurgitation or vomiting
  • Loose stools
  • Shortness of breath
  • Urine clear and abundant
  • T: Pale and flabby - may have teethmarks
  • C: White
  • P: Even, forceless and Deficient or Thready and forceless
Treatment Principle
  • Tonify and Strengthen the Spleen and Stomach
  • Augment Qi
Herb Formulas
  • Shen Ling Bai Zhu San modified
  • Shi Quan Da Bu Tang
  • Gui Pi Tang
  • With Blood Stasis: Bu Yang Huan Wu Tang
  • For obesity with excessive Phlegm: Liu Jun Zi Tang
  • For significant Qi Deficiency or Qi Sinking: Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang
  • Chinese Patent Medicine: Ba Xian Tang
Points
  • General points + REN-4, REN-6, SP-6, ST-36, UB-20, UB-21
  • REN-12, SP-6, UB-20
  • DU-5, DU-6, LI-10, ST-36, UB-20, UB-21
  • LIV-13, REN-6, REN-12, SP-3, SP-5, SP-6, ST-21, ST-36, UB-20, UB-21

    For vertigo: + DU-20, GB-20

FENG TAN (WIND-PHLEG)

Clinical Manifestations
  • Facial paralysis
  • Numbness
  • Slurred speech
  • Deviated eyes
  • Deviated mouth (wry mouth)
  • Dizziness
  • Tinnitus
  • Sputum in the throat
  • Chest fullness and distention
  • Epigastric fullness and distention
  • Hypertension
  • Stroke
  • T: Deviated and trembling
  • C: Thick
  • P: Slippery and maybe wiry
Treatment Principle
  • Resolve Phlegm
  • Subdue Wind
Herb Formulas
  • Ban Xia Bai Zhu Tian Ma Tang
    • With Phlegm-Heat: Xing Wei Cheng Qi Tang
Points
  • Pre-stroke: ST-40, UB-40
  • Stroke (emergency): DU-26, KI-1, PC-9, Ting Points
  • Post-stroke:
    Arm: LI-4, LI-11, LI-15, SI-3, SJ-5
    Leg: GB-34, GB-35, UB-40, UB-60
    Scalp: Motor area (contralateral - upper one-fifth)
