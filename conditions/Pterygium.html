PTERYGIUM

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Triangular overgrowth of cornea
  • Slow onset with slight dryness
  • Itching of the eye
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
  • For long-term use:
    Zhi Bai Di Huang Wan
    Gan Lu Xiao Du Yin
  • For pterygium spreading from the inner canthus:
    San Huang Xie Xin Tang + Pterygium #1
    Dao Chi San + Ba Zheng San
  • For pterygium spreading from the outer canthus:
    Pterygium #2
    Pterygium #3
Points
  • GB-14, SJ-23, UB-1, UB-2
  • LI-4, SI-1, Taiyang, UB-1
  • DU-20, GB-1 or SJ-23 (usually used alternately), LI-4, UB-1, UB-2, bleed Erjian

YANG MING FU WITH BLOOD STAGNATION

Clinical Manifestations
  • High fever (sometimes rises and falls)
  • Great thirst
  • Profuse perspiration
  • Aversion to Heat
  • Abdominal pain (worse with pressure)
  • Abdominal distension
  • Foul breath
  • Red face
  • Dryness
  • Constipation (hard dry knots)
  • Dislikes pressure
  • Restlessness
  • Irritability
  • Mental disturbance:
    Mentally unclear
    Delirious speech
  • T: Red and dry, or black and moist
  • C: Thick yellow or dry yellow
  • P: Deep, strong and forceful
Treatment Principle
  • Purge
Herb Formulas
  • Tao He Cheng Qi Tang
Points
  • For Heat: DU-14, LI-4, LI-11, SJ-5, ST-44
  • For Stagnation: REN-12, ST-34, ST-36, ST-37, ST-39
  • For Constipation: SJ-6, ST-25, UB-25

HEART FIRE

Clinical Manifestations
  • Irritability with a sensation of Heat in the chest
  • Thirst with a desire to drink cold beverages
  • Red face
  • Fever
  • May be sores around the mouth
  • Dark, scanty urine
  • Rough urination
  • Fidgeting
  • Warm sensations in the chest
  • Sores on the tongue
  • Painful urination (stabbing)
  • Urination short and hesitant
  • Hematuria
  • Oliguria
  • Maybe blood in the urine
  • Sluggish urination
  • Irritability
  • Fatigue
  • T: Red
  • P: Rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear the Heart
  • Drain Fire
  • Relieve toxicity
Herb Formulas
  • Dao Chi San
  • San Huang Xie Xin Tang
  • Nu Xiao San
Points
  • General points

SPLEEN QI DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Pterygium
  • Tinnitus
  • Hearing loss
  • Diminished visual and aural acuity
  • Blurred vision
  • Dizziness
  • Light headedness
  • Anorexia
  • Spontaneous sweating
  • Chronic diarrhea
  • T: Pale
  • C; White or Thick and white
  • P: Weak
Treatment Principle
  • Strengthen and raises the Qi of the Middle Jiao
  • Improve visual and aural acuity
Herb Formulas
  • Yi Qi Cong Ming Tang
Points
  • General points
