ECZEMA

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Redness
  • Swelling
  • Vesicles
  • Weeping
  • Crusting
  • Scaling
  • Itching
  • Burning
  • Acute:
    Lesions appear abruptly on the interior aspects of the four extremities (cubital and popliteal creases)
    They may affect the face and neck
    They can appear on the dorsal aspect of the hands and feet and scrotum
    There is erythema
    They may appear as bright red and swollen plaques with papules or pustules, vesicles or watery blisters
    There is suppuration which eventually form scabs
    Two or three types of lesions may present simultaneously
    The border is always indistinct
    They are intensely itchy
    Acute lesions are often confused with contact dermatitis
  • Chronic:
    Red, scaly, rough, thickened skin (lichenification) which is dry and sometimes fissured
    Desquamation
    Pigment sedimentation
    Distinct borders
    Frequent severe itching
    Frequent acute flare ups
    Lesions frequently appear on the face, behind the auricles of the ears, shanks or on the scrotum
    Lesions may appear anywhere
Treatment Principle
  • Acute:
    Clear Heat
    Eliminate Dampness
  • Chronic:
    Nourish the Blood
    Dispel Wind
