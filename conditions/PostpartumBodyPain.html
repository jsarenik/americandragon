WIND-COLD

Clinical Manifestations
  • Very serious pain joints, back, limbs and other part of body
  • The pain is aggravated by Cold and relieved by warmth
  • Painful joints
  • Heavy and painful sensations at fixed locations in lower back and extremities with weakness and stiffness or hypertonicity and immobility
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Attraction to warmth
  • Palpitations
  • Pain in the midline and back during pregnancy
  • Pain worse at night General coldness
  • Sallow complexion
  • Wandering pain
  • Pale complexion
  • Shortness of breath
  • May be paresthesia and numbness
  • Cold pain in the low back and knees
  • Limited movement
  • Soreness, weakness and numbness of the joints
  • Tinnitus
  • Muscle spasms
  • Dizziness
  • T: Pale
  • C: Thin and white or White and moist
  • P: Thin, weak and slow or Deep, thready and weak or slippery
  • All symptoms occur after childbirth
Treatment Principle
  • Disperse Wind-Cold
  • Warm the channels   
Herb Formulas

SPLEEN YANG DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Sallow complexion
  • Anorexia (maybe with vomiting)
  • Four limbs feel tired and heavy
  • Abdominal and epigastric distention after eating
  • Maybe acid regurgitation or vomiting
  • Loose stools or watery stools containing undigested food
  • Shortness of breath
  • Pain in the epigastrium with cold sensations (pain is relieved by warmth)
  • Cold limbs
  • Feeling chilled
  • Mental fatigue
  • Maybe scanty urine with edema
  • Maybe clear, watery vaginal discharge
  • T: Pale
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Deep and weak
Treatment Principle
  • Warm the Center
  • Dispel Cold.
Herb Formulas
  • Fu Zi Li Zhong Tang
  • With constipation: Wen Pi Tang
  • Chinese patent medicines:
    Fu Zi Li Zhong Wan
Points
  • Warm-needle UB-20, LIV-13, SP-3, SP-5, SP-6, ST-36, REN-12

SPLEEN AND STOMACH DEFICIENCY COLD

Clinical Manifestations
  • Chronic, intermittent, dull abdominal pain which is better with warmth and pressure and ingesting warm fluids
  • Waxy, pale or sallow lusterless complexion
  • Anorexia (maybe with vomiting)
  • Easily satiated
  • Four limbs feel tired and heavy
  • Abdominal and epigastric distention after eating
  • Maybe acid regurgitation or vomiting
  • Loose stools or watery stools containing undigested food
  • Shortness of breath
  • Pain in the epigastrium with cold sensations relieved by warmth
  • Intolerance of Cold
  • Cold limbs
  • Feeling chilled
  • Lethargy
  • Fatigue
  • Mental fatigue
  • Depression
  • Low spirits
  • Maybe scanty urine with edema
  • Maybe clear, watery vaginal discharge
  • T: Pale maybe swollen
  • C: White or Thin and white or White and greasy
  • P: Deep, thready and forceless or Deep and weak or Thready and weak
Treatment Principle
  • Warm the Middle Jiao
  • Strengthen the Spleen and Stomach
Herb Formulas
  • Xiao Jian Zhong Tang
  • Chinese patent medicines:
    Li Zhong Wan
    With Kidney Yang Deficiency: Jin Kui Shen Qi Wan
    With Qi and Blood Deficiency: Shi Quan Da Bu Wan
Points
  • Moxa or moxa needle KI-3, LI-11, REN-4, REN-6, REN-7, REN-12, SP-4, SP-6, ST-25, ST-30, ST-36
    With nausea: + PC-6

  • Pale face

  • Oliguria with edema
