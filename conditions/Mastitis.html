MASTITIS (BREAST ABSCESS)

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Acute infection of the breast (abscess)  often seen in first-time mothers during the third or fourth week of postpartum breast feeding
  • Symptoms are usually unilateral and characterized by chills and fever and tachycardia
  • The breast is red hot
  • The main complaint is pain
  • About 10% of women with mastitis develop a breast abscess when the source of infection is the mouth and pharynx in the newborn
  • Distended, painful, swollen breast with lumps
  • If caught early, it can be massaged out and treated with warm moist herbal washes and compresses.
  • If left untreated or unnoticed it may manifest with flu like symptoms (toxic Heat). 
  • As it grows and fills with pus it can get very painful and lead to an abscess which can get red and hot. 
  • Internal blockages are deeper and generally can’t be seen on the surface.
  • They grow slower, are harder to detect and are tougher to move or break-up. 
  • The major cause is the milk stagnates (from irregular feeding, not enough stimulation, etc.) during breast feeding resulting in blocked duct(s) and creating infection.
  • There are two types:
    1. Late pregnancy
    2. Postpartum
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
  • Tuo Li Xiao Du Yin
  • Mastitis #4
  • For early-stage acute mastitis: mix equal portions of powdered Bul. Bolbostemmae Tu Bei Mu and Angelicae Dahuricae Bai Zhi and take 9g per day. If the patient is strong, take 15g per day.
  • Topical:
    Jin Huang San

    Mastitis #3
     Cong Yun Fa (Scallion Ironing Method): Apply mashed Cong Bai to the affected area. Place a piece of smoldering charcoal on this and cover with a jar. Leave in place until the patient begins to perspire profusely. The swelling should be alleviated when the jar is withdrawn.
