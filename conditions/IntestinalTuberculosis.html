INTESTINAL TUBERCULOSIS

TAI YANG - YANG MING

Clinical Manifestations
  • Fever and chills
  • Maybe fever only
  • Achiness
  • Anhidrosis
  • Headache
  • Cough
  • Asthma
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Edema
  • Thirst
  • Restlessness
  • Irritability
  • Fidgeting
  • Constipation
  • Sometimes dry achy eyes
  • T: Normal or red
  • C: Yellow or white
  • P: Floating and tight or rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Expel Wind
  • Disperse Cold
  • Cool Heat
Herb Formulas
  • Ge Gen Huang Qin Huang Lian Tang
Points
  • DU-14, DU-16, GB-20, LI-4, LI-11, LU-7, LU-9, LU-10, SJ-5, SP-1, SP-2, ST-44, UB-12, UB-13

YANG MING FU WITH QI AND BLOOD DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Constipation characterized by only clear, watery fluids upon defecation or
  • Green, watery foul-smelling diarrhea
  • Altered consciousness
  • Delirium
  • Severe cases:
    Carphology
    Hallucinations
    Grabbing the air
    Loss of consciousness with frigid, contracted extremities
  • Epigastric and abdominal hardness and pain
  • Abdominal pain that is worse with pressure
  • Thirst
  • Fever
  • A dry mouth and tongue
  • Shortness of breath
  • Low energy
  • A weak voice
  • T: Variable
  • C: Dry and yellow or Dry and black
  • P: Deficient
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Promote defecation
  • Tonify Qi and Blood
Herb Formulas
  • Huang Long Tang
Points

LUNG AND SPLEEN QI DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Protracted, incessant cough
  • Expectoration of copious, clear, watery or white Phlegm
  • Short, weak, breathing
  • Asthma
  • Four limbs tired
  • Anorexia
  • General lassitude
  • Distention and pain of the epigastrium and abdomen
  • Loose stools
  • Severe cases:
    Frightens easily
  • T: Pale
  • C: White
  • P: Thready and weak
Treatment Principle
  • Reinforce the Spleen
  • Benefit the Lungs
  • Warm and transform Phlegm-Dampness
Herb Formulas
  • Shen Ling Bai Zhu San
  • Si Jun Zi Tang
Points
  • LU-9, REN-17, SP-3, ST-36, UB-13, UB-20, UB-43

DEFICIENCY OF FLUID IN THE LARGE INTESTINE

Clinical Manifestations
  • Dry stools
  • Knotted constipation or bowel movements once a day
  • Difficulty passing stools
  • Dizziness
  • Bad breath
  • Mouth and throat dry
  • T: Red
  • C: Little
  • P: Thready and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Lubricate the Intestines
  • Relieve constipation
Herb Formulas
  • Zeng Ye Tang
Points
  • KI-1, KI-3, KI-6, SJ-6, SP-15, ST-25, ST-37, UB-25

  • Maybe acid regurgitation or vomiting
  • Loose stools

SPLEEN AND KIDNEY YANG DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Body cold
  • Limbs icy
  • Complexion white and shiny
  • Low back, knees and abdomen feel cold and painful
  • Lower abdomen feels cold and painful
  • Loose stools containing undigested food
  • “Cock-crow” diarrhea (occurs at about 5 am)
  • Edema of face and limbs
  • Dysuria
  • Severe:
    Abdominal ascites
  • T: Pale, moist and swollen
  • C: Thin and white or White and greasy
  • P: Deep, thready, weak and maybe slow
Treatment Principle
  • Warm and Reinforce the Spleen and Kidneys
Herb Formulas
  • Si Shen Wan
  • Fu Zi Li Zhong Wan + Wu Ling San
  • Ji Sheng Shen Qi Wan
  • With edema: Zhen Wu Tang
Points

  • SP-6, SP-9, moxa or moxa needle REN-4, REN-6, REN-9, ST-36, UB-20, UB-21, UB-23

BLOOD STAGNATION WITH BLOOD DEFICIENCY AND HEAT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Vomiting immediately after eating
  • Vomiting of saliva and undigested food
  • Vomiting with constipation (severe)
  • Chest pain (severe)
  • Emaciation with dry skin (severe)
  • Loss of appetite, especially for meat
  • Stomach distention
  • A sensation of obstruction in the throat
  • Diminished saliva
  • Maybe fever due to pulmonary infection
  • Maybe aortic erosion
  • Liquids or semi-solids are sustained
  • Dizziness
  • Dry muscles and skin
  • Definite obstruction between the pharynx and diaphragm
  • Hard stools
  • Dull pain
  • Burning pain
  • A rancid taste in the mouth
  • Precordial discomfort
  • Constipation
  • A pale complexion
  • Pale nails
  • Pale lips
  • Maybe severe coughing
  • Maybe aspiration of Fluids
  • T: Normal or Cyanotic or Red or Pale
  • C: Little or Non
  • P: Weak and choppy or Thready and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Nourish the Blood
  • Invigorate the Blood
  • Dispel Blood Stasis
  • Clear Heat
  • Cool the Blood
Herb Formulas
  • Tong You Tang
Points
  • PC-6, REN-12, REN-17, SP-10. ST-36

COLD DAMP

Clinical Manifestations
  • Ascites
  • Heaviness in the body and head
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Warming the abdomen makes it better
  • Edema of the extremities (usually not pitting)
  • Scanty urination
  • Loose stools or
  • Diarrhea
  • T: Normal
  • C: Greasy and white
  • P: Soft and moderate or Wiry and slow
Treatment Principle
  • Transform Dampness
  • Drain Fluids
  • Warm Yang
  • Disperse Cold
Herb Formulas
  • Shi Pi Yin modified
  • Chinese Patent Medicines: Fu Zi Li Zhong Wan
Points
  • Moxa or moxa needle REN-9, SP-6, SP-9, UB-20, UB-22, UB-23, UB-39

  • P: Choppy

  • Unblock painful obstruction
  • Alleviate pain
