SORE THROAT, PHARYNGITIS, THROAT PAIN, PAINFUL SWALLOWING

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Pharyngitis is inflammation of the back of the throat, known as the pharynx. It typically results in a sore throat and fever. Other symptoms may include a runny nose, cough, headache, a hoarse voice. Symptoms usually last three to five days. Complications can include sinusitis and acute otitis media. Pharyngitis is typically a type of respiratory tract infection. Swelling, pain and redness of the pharynx
  • Usually seen during the winter and spring months
  • The onset is rapid and acute
  • At the beginning the patient report a hot or burning sensation of the throat
  • As it progresses, the throat pain increases
  • The throat is further aggravated by swallowing
  • Fever
  • Chills
  • General malaise
  • Dry cough
  • Examination reveals:
    A bright red swelling of the pharyngeal mucosa
    Increased secretion
    Submaxillary lymph glands are swollen and tender to the touch
