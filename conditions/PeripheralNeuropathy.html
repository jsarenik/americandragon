PERIPHERAL NEUROPATHY

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • This is a symptom of many different patterns of disharmony
  • Affects the limbs
  • Numbness
  • Sensitivity to pain or temperature
  • A tingling, burning or prickling sensation
  • Sharp burning pain
  • Cramps
  • Extreme sensitivity to touch
  • Loss of balance and coordination
  • Muscle weakness
  • Muscle wasting
  • Paralysis
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
  • Topical for Damp-Heat and Blood Stasis: Hot Foot Soak #1
Points
  • DU-9, DU-12, DU-14, LI-11, UB-14, UB-16, UB-60
    Upper limb: Baxie, LI-4, LI-11, LI-15, SJ-5
    Lower limb: GB-30, GB-34, GB-39, SP-6
  • Cutaneous needling

EXTERNAL WIND

Clinical Manifestations
  • Headache due to Wind attack
  • Headache in any part of the head
  • Fever and chills
  • Dizziness
  • Nasal congestion
  • Stuffy nose
  • Clear or white nasal discharge
  • Sneezing
  • Body aches
  • Nasal obstruction
  • Vertigo
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Copious sputum
  • T: Normal
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Superficial
Treatment Principle
  • Dispel Wind
  • Alleviate pain
Herb Formulas
  • Chuan Xiong Cha Tiao San
Points

TAI YANG SHANG HAN

Clinical Manifestations
  • Severe chills
  • Mild or no fever
  • Headache
  • Generalized discomfort
  • Stiff neck and shoulders
  • Body aches
  • Joint ache
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Hiccups
  • Anhidrosis
  • Gasping
  • Stabbing joint pain
  • Itchy throat
  • Cough
  • Asthma
  • Aversion to Wind and Cold
  • Maybe low back pain
  • Maybe frozen shoulder
  • Maybe dyspnea
  • Maybe epistaxis (severe)
  • T: Normal
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Floating and tight
Treatment Principle
  • Relieve the Exterior
  • Expel Cold
  • Disperse the Lungs
  • Stop asthma
Herb Formulas
  • Ma Huang Tang
  • With Damp Accumulation: Ma Huang Jia Zhu Tang
Points
  • DU-16, GB-20, LI-4, LU-7, SJ-5, UB-12, UB-13
  • For a stiff neck: + GB-21, SI-3, Taiyang, UB-62

WIND-DAMP-BI WITH BLOOD DEFICIENCY AND BLOOD STASIS

Clinical Manifestations
  • Muscle aches
  • Joint pain
  • Radiating pain in the legs
  • Low back pain
  • Edema
  • Numbness in the lower extremities
  • Pain in the trunk and extremities
  • Abdominal pain
  • Sciatica
  • Aversion to Wind and Cold
  • Knee pain
  • T: Pale or Pink with purple spots
  • C: Normal
  • P: Choppy or Taut
Treatment Principle
  • Unblocks and relaxes the channels
  • Invigorate the Blood
  • Break up Blood Stasis
  • Dispel Wind-Dampness
  • Strengthen the tendons
Herb Formulas
  • Shu Jing Huo Xue Tang
  • With Cold and/or Heat: Shu Jin Li An San
  • With Qi and Blood Stagnation: Shen Tong Zhu Yu Tang
Points

LUNG AND STOMACH FLUID DAMAGE

Clinical Manifestations
  • Upper or lower limb weakness or loss of strength either during or after a fever associated with and External Wind Invasion
  • Inability oft the hands to grasp
  • Inability of the feet to stand and walk
  • Severe:
    Paralysis
    Progressive emaciation
    Dry, withered skin
    Abnormal sensations
    Dry throat
    Parched lips
    Heart vexation
    Hoarse voice
    Thirst
    Short, reddish, hot, painful urination
    Constipation
    T: Red
    C: Thin and dry
    P: Thready and rapid or Surging, empty and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Generate Fluids
  • Moisten Dryness
Herb Formulas
  • Qing Zao Jiu Fei Tang + Yi Wei Tang
  • For thirst leading to drinking, with a surging, large, empty or hollow pulse due to Summerheat Damaging Qi and Yin: Bai Hu Jia Ren Shen Tang
Points
  • DU-14, SJ-6, ST-36, ST-44 + affected local points
    For pain, tingling or numbness of the toes: + Bafeng
    For tingling or burning on the sole of the foot: + KI-1
    For pain in the heel: + KI-3 through to UB-60
    For constipation: + LI-4, ST-25, UB-25
    For marked Lung Dryness: + Baxie, LI-4
    For tingling or numbness of the fingers: + LI-11
    For a fever: + LI-4, LI-11

SPLEEN AND STOMACH QI DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Paralysis of the four extremities which is more severe in the lower limbs
  • Swelling and distention of the hands and feet
  • Poor appetite
  • Abdominal distention
  • Muscular cramping and pain
  • Possible emaciation
  • A sallow, yellow lusterless facial complexion
  • Loose stools
  • T: Pale
  • C: Thin and white
  • P:Thready and forceless
Treatment Principle
  • Strengthen the Spleen
  • Tonify Qi
Herb Formulas
  • Peripheral Neuropathy #1
Points
  • SP-6, SP-10, ST-36, UB-20, UB-21, local Ahshi points
    For pain in the heel: + UB-60
    For tingling or burning on the sole of the foot: + KI-1
    For pain, tingling or numbness of the toes: + Bafeng
  • For Central Qi Sinking with a sinking sensation, dizziness, fatigue and lack of strength: + DU-20

SPLEEN AND KIDNEY YANG DEFICIENCY WITH COLD-DAMPNESS

Clinical Manifestations
  • Lower limb paralysis or
  • Heaviness, numbness,t tingling and insensitivity of the four limbs
  • Body cold
  • Cold hands and feet
  • Complexion white and shiny
  • Low back, knees and abdomen feel cold and painful
  • Lower abdomen feels cold and painful
  • Loose stools containing undigested food
  • “Cock-crow” diarrhea (occurs at about 5 am)
  • Edema of face and limbs
  • Dysuria
  • Severe:
    Abdominal ascites
    Chilled body and limbs with frequent sweating
    A stagnant feeling in the chest which may progress to difficulty swallowing
    Stagnating and lingering Phlegm-Fluids
    Coughing
    Distressed, hasty breathing
    Cyanotic nails
  • T: Pale, moist and swollen
  • C: Thin and white or White and greasy
  • P: Deep, thready, weak and maybe slow or Deep and hidden
Treatment Principle
  • Scatter Cold
  • Dispel Dampness
  • Warm and Reinforce the Spleen and Kidneys
Herb Formulas
  • Ma Huang Fu Zi Xi Xin Tang modified
  • Zhen Wu Tang
  • For a lack of Cold-Dampness above and source Qi Deficiency below resulting in wilting: Er Xian Tang + Si Wu Tang
Points
  • DU-4, KI-3, SP-6, UB-20, UB-21, UB-23. local Ahshi points
    For tingling or burning on the sole of the foot: + KI-1
    For pain, tingling or numbness of the toes: + Bafeng
    For marked Dampness or Damp-Heat: + SP-9
    With Blood Stasis: + SP-10

LIVER AND KIDNEY YIN DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • A long, slow disease course with gradual, progressive atrophy
  • Weakness and loss of use of the upper or lower limbs
  • Low back and knees sore and painful
  • Facial complexion pale and sallow
  • Flaccid paralysis (Wei syndrome), weakness
  • Numbness and tingling of the hands and feet
  • Both cheeks slightly red
  • Dizziness
  • Dry eyes
  • Blurred vision
  • Spots before the eyes
  • Numbness of limbs
  • Emaciation
  • Tinnitus
  • Deafness
  • Forgetfulness
  • Throat dry and painful
  • Dry mouth
  • Hoarse voice
  • Five Sole Heat
  • Low-grade fever
  • Night sweats
  • Irritability
  • Insomnia
  • Stools difficult to pass
  • Constipation
  • Men:
    Spermatorrhea
  • Women:
    Irregular menses or
    Scanty menses or
    Amenorrhea
    Vaginal discharge
  • T: Red
  • C: None
  • P: Thready and rapid or Thready, wiry and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Nourish and Reinforce the Liver and Kidney
  • Nourish Yin
  • Clear Heats
Herb Formulas
  • Hu Qian Wan  modified
Points
  • KI-3, KI-7, SP-6, SP-10, ST-36, local Ahshi points
    For more marked Spleen Qi Deficiency: + UB-20, UB-21
    For marked Kidney Deficiency: + DU-4, UB-23
    For tingling or burning on the sole of the foot: + KI-1
    For pain, tingling or numbness of the toes: + Bafeng

BLOOD STAGNATION DUE TO A MILD ATTACK OF WIND COLD WITH QI DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Relatively superficial numbness without pain
  • Paresthesia and numbness of the skin and flesh
  • Paresthesia of the shoulders
  • Neck and shoulder stiffness and pain
  • Abdominal pain alleviated by warmth and pressure
  • Pale complexion
  • Hemiplegia
  • Stiff tongue
  • Flaccidity if the extremities
  • Migratory pain
  • Pain, weakness, tightness or spasms of the extremities
  • Spontaneous sweating
  • Shortness of breath
  • Aphasia
  • Excess salivation
  • Aversion to Wind
  • T: Normal or Thick
  • C: White or Smooth and white or White and greasy
  • P: Faint, choppy and tight or Slightly rough and tight or Large and weak or Slow and large
Treatment Principle
  • Tonify Qi and Blood
  • Warm and harmonize the channels and collaterals
  • Harmonize the Ying and Wei
  • Unblock painful obstruction
  • Warm the Middle Jiao
  • Dispel Cold
  • Alleviate pain
  • Harmonize Yin and Yang

  • Huang Qi Gui Zhi Wu Wu Tang

DAMP-HEAT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Soreness, heaviness and lack of strength of the four extremities or of the two lower extremities
  • Possible progressive paralysis or atrophy
  • Insensitivity, numbness or tingling of the four extremities
  • Limb aching and pain
  • Burning Heat which is ameliorated by Cold
  • Generalized fever which is not easily alleviated
  • Possible deviation of the face, mouth and eyes
  • Poor appetite
  • Thirst with no desire to drink
  • Nausea/vomiting
  • Abnormal vaginal discharge
  • Red, hot, rough, painful urination
  • Loose stools
  • T: Red
  • C Slimy and yellow
  • P: Rapid or Slippery and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Eliminate Dampness
Herb Formulas
  • Si Miao Wan modified
Points
  • SP-6, SP-9, SP-10, ST-36 + local points according to the area of discomfort
    For pain in the heel: + UB-60
    For tingling or burning on the sole of the foot: + KI-1
    For pain, tingling or numbness of the toes: + Bafeng
