CERVICAL SPONDYLOSIS

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Head, neck, shoulder and upper back aching and pain
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
  • For cervical spondylosis: 15-30g Hb. Pyrolae Luo Xian Cao + 15-30g Rx. Puerariae Ge Gen
  • For muscle spasms and tension: 15-30g Rx. Paeoniae Alba Bai Shao + 15-30g Fr. Chaenomelis Mu Gua
  • For osteoporosis or bone impediment: Rz. Drynariae Gu Sui Bi + Cervi Cornu Lu Jiao
  • For Wind-Damp Obstruction: Rx. Clematidis Wei Ling Xian + Rx. et Rz. Notopterygii Qiang Huo
  • For inhibited movement: Pheretima Di Long + Caulis Spatholobi Ji Xue Teng
  • For pain:
    Rx. Salviae Miltiorrhizae Dan Shen
    Rz. Chuanxiong Chuan Xiong
    Scorpio Quan Xie
    Scolopendra Wu Gong
Points
  • KI-10
  • Cheng Shan Xia (2 cun below UB-52)

WIND-COLD (TAI YANG CHANNEL DISHARMONY)

Clinical Manifestations
  • Heaviness and pain of the head
  • Severe aversion to Cold
  • Mild or no fever
  • Headache
  • Generalized discomfort
  • Pain and stiffness of the cervical vertebrae
  • Stiffness and contraction of the neck and shoulders
  • One or both hands lack strength
  • Inability to flex and extend the fingers
  • Body aches, stiffness, tightness and numbness
  • Joint ache
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Hiccups
  • Anhidrosis or
  • Spontaneous sweating
  • Gasping
  • Stabbing joint pain
  • Itchy throat
  • Cough
  • Asthma
  • Aversion to Wind and Cold
  • Maybe low back pain
  • Maybe frozen shoulder
  • Maybe dyspnea
  • Maybe epistaxis (severe)
  • T: Normal or Slightly pale
  • C: Thin and white or Greasy and white
  • P: Floating and tight or Floating and moderate or Floating and wiry
Treatment Principle
  • Expel Wind
  • Dissipate Cold
  • Regulate and harmonize the Ying and Wei
Herb Formulas
  • For sweating with aversion to Wind: Gui Zhi Jia Ge Gen Tang
  • For sweating with aversion to Cold: Ge Gen Tang
Points

WIND-COLD-DAMPNESS (BI SYNDROME)

Clinical Manifestations
  • Head, neck, shoulder and upper back aching and pain
  • Fixed neck pain
  • Tender points on the neck
  • A possible palpable cord-like feeling in the neck
  • A stiff neck
  • Inhibited movement
  • Pain, soreness and numbness of the four limbs
  • Maybe weakness and heaviness of the upper limbs
  • Heavy-headedness
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Prefers Heat
  • Severe:
    Withering of the arm muscles
    Difficulty extending and flexing the fingers
    Maybe complete loss of sensation and rigidity of the fingers
  • T: Pale
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Floating and moderate or Floating and tight
Treatment Principle
  • Dispel Wind
  • Scatter Cold
  • Unblock the channels
  • Diffuse impediments
Herb Formulas
  • Gui Zhi Jia Ge Gen Tang modified
  • Di Long Tang Jia Jian
  • For Dampness predominant with the feeling of a bag over the head: Cervical Spondylosis #1
  • For Cold predominant with aversion to Cold, a Cold sensation on the back of the neck and worse pain on exposure to Cold: Cervical Spondylosis #2
  • For Wind predominant and numbness more evident: Juan Bi Tang Er Hao
  • For Cold predominant and pain more severe: Yang He Tang
Points
  • DU-14, GB-20, LU-7, SI-3
    For Wind-Damp predominant: - DU-14, LU-7 + SP-9, UB-11
    For neck pain along the Bladder Channel: - GB-20 + UB-10, UB-60
    For neck pain along the Small Intestine Channel: + LI-10
    For neck pain along the Large Intestine Channel: + SI-11, SI-13
    For neck pain along the San Jiao Channel: - LU-7 + SJ-3, SJ-15 and/or SJ-16
    For neck pain along the DU Channel: + DU-15, DU-16
    For pain causing avoidance of backward bending of the neck: + REN-24
    For pain causing avoidance of forward bending of the head: + DU-26
    For pain involving the shoulders: + LI-15, SJ-15 or LI-15, SI-9, SJ-14
    For pain simultaneously involving the neck, trapezius and scapula: First needle GB-21, SI-11, SJ-15, SJ-16, UB-10, Then:
    If the pain is on the DU Channel: + DU-1 or SI-3
    If the pain is on the UB Channel: + UB-60 or UB-62
    If the pain is on the GB Channel: + GB-39 or GB-40
    If the pain is on the SI Channel: + SI-3 or SI-6
    If the pain is on the SJ Channel: + SJ-3 or SJ-6

LIVER AND KIDNEY YIN DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Head, neck, shoulder and upper back aching and pain
  • Maybe distending pain in the head
  • Numbness and lack of strength in the four limbs
  • Low back and knee soreness and weakness
  • Walks haltingly
  • Maybe paralysis
  • Dizziness
  • Blurred vision
  • Tinnitus
  • Deafness
  • Tidal redness
  • Night sweats
  • Hot sensations in the face
  • A dry mouth and throat
  • A bitter taste in the mouth
  • Toothache
  • Insomnia or
  • Dream disturbed sleep
  • Agitated
  • Easily angered
  • With Kidney Yang Deficiency:
    Frequent, copious urination
    Dribbling of urine
    Maybe urinary and fecal incontinence
  • T: Red
  • C: Little or none
  • P: Thready and rapid or Thready and wiry or Choppy and wiry
Treatment Principle
  • Tonify and nourish the Liver and Kidneys
  • Invigorate the Blood
  • Unblock the Channels
Herb Formulas
  • Hu Qian Wan modified
  • Jing Zhui Bing Fang
  • For cervical spondylosis with accompanying Yang Deficiency and Blood Stasis with cold feet, decreased libido and loose stools: He Shi Jing Zhui Bing Fang
  • For more pronounced Yin Deficiency with Internal Fire and more severe limitation of movement: Hu Qian Wan modified
  • For pain and contracture of the neck and arm muscles: + Shao Yao Gan Cao Tang

    For pain simultaneously involving the neck, trapezius and scapula: First needle GB-21, SI-11, SJ-15, SJ-16, UB-10, Then:
    If the pain is on the DU Channel: + DU-1 or SI-3
    If the pain is on the UB Channel: + UB-60 or UB-62
    If the pain is on the GB Channel: + GB-39 or GB-40
    If the pain is on the SI Channel: + SI-3 or SI-6

QI AND BLOOD STAGNATION

Clinical Manifestations
  • Head, neck, shoulder, upper back and upper arm aching and pain
  • Severe lancinating pain
  • Fixed pain
  • Pain worse at night and better during the day
  • Pain worse with pressure
  • The muscles of the arms are withered
  • Numbness of the fingertips
  • Maybe numbness which is worse at night
  • Spasms and tension
  • Cramping of the four limbs
  • Vexation
  • Agitation
  • A dry mouth
  • The fingernails are cyanosed, sunken, concave and lack luster
  • Dry, itchy skin
  • The arm may be contracted
  • T: Dark purple or Static maculae or Spots
  • C: White
  • P: Wiry and choppy
Treatment Principle
  • Invigorate the Blood
  • Transform Stasis
  • Unblock the Channels
  • Alleviate pain
Herb Formulas

  • Huo Luo Xiao Ling Dan

  • For dizziness and/or pain and oppression of the chest: Xue Fu Zhu Yu Tangmodified
  • For advanced Qi and Blood Stagnation with severe pain: Hua Yu Chu Bi Tang
  • For Qi and Blood Deficiency giving rise to Qi and Blood Stagnation with severe pain: Bu Yang Huan Wu Tang modified

PHLEGM AND BLOOD STAGNATION

Clinical Manifestations
  • Head, neck, shoulder, upper back and upper arm aching and pain
  • Severe lancinating pain
  • Fixed pain
  • Pain worse at night and better during the day
  • Pain worse with pressure
  • Dizziness
  • Heaviness of the head with a wrapping sensation
  • Cramping of the four limbs
  • Vexation
  • Agitation
  • Palpitations
  • Chest oppression
  • Distention of the flanks
  • Dry mouth
  • Poor appetite
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Loose or sticky stools
  • A feeling of obstruction in the throat
  • Coughing with copious sputum
  • Abdominal and epigastric fullness and distention
  • The fingernails are cyanosed, sunken, concave and lack luster
  • Dry, itchy skin
  • The arm may be contracted
  • Maybe heaviness, numbness, coldness and/or edema of the limbs
  • Severe cases:
    Mental and spiritual confusion
    Loss of consciousness
  • T: Swollen
  • C: White and greasy
  • P: Soft or Deep and weak
Treatment Principle
  • Expel Damp
  • Transform Phlegm
  • Break up Stasis
  • Unblock the channels
Herb Formulas
  • With Phlegm and Blood Stagnation equally severe: Dao Tan Tang  modified
  • With Blood Stagnation more pronounced than Phlegm Stagnation: Cervical Spondylosis #3
  • With Phlegm Stagnation more pronounced than Blood Stagnation: Di Tan Tang  modified
Points
