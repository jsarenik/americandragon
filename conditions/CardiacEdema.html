CARDIAC EDEMA

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Cardiac edema a manifestation of congestive heart failure, due to increased venous and capillary pressures and often associated with renal sodium retention.
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
Points

TAI YANG ZHONG FENG

Clinical Manifestations
  • Aversion to Wind
  • Mild, or severe chills
  • Fever which may be persistent
  • Uneasiness from fever
  • Spontaneous perspiration
  • Headache
  • Nausea
  • Nasal irritation
  • Nasal obstruction
  • Tendency to snore
  • Itchy throat
  • T: Normal
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Superficial and slow or Moderate and maybe weak
Treatment Principle
  • Create sweat to relieve the Exterior and expel Wind
  • Harmonize Ying and Wei to protect digestion and nourish Wei Qi
Herb Formulas
  • Gui Zhi Tang
  • With stiff neck and shoulders: Gui Zhi Jia Ge Gen Tang
  • With dyspnea, asthma and sputum: Gui Zhi Jia Hou Po Xing Ren Tang
Points
  • Bitong, DU-16, GB-20, KI-7, LI-4, LI-20, LU-7, SJ-5, UB-12, UB-13, Yintang
    With stiff neck and shoulders: + GB-21
    With Dampness: + SP-6, SP-9
    With dyspnea, asthma and sputum: Dingchuan, REN-22
    With nocturnal emission, excess dreams, Cold lower extremities, dizziness and hair loss: + DU-4, KI-3, UB-23
    With continuous perspiration, muscle spasms of the limbs with difficult flexion and extension and difficult urination: + REN-4
    With sustained fever of long duration accompanied by severe chills: + DU-14

TAI YANG BIAO FU

Clinical Manifestations
  • Chills and fever or
  • Fever with no thirst
  • Swollen eyes, limbs abdomen or genitals
  • Achiness
  • Stiff neck and shoulders
  • Anhidrosis
  • Headache
  • Stabbing joint pain
  • Itchy throat
  • Cough
  • Asthma
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Maybe frozen shoulder
  • Dyspnea
  • Vomiting, if the condition is tonified before the Wind is expelled
  • Urinary retention if the condition is reduced before the Wind is expelled
  • Shortness of breath
  • Mild or severe gasping
  • Hiccups
  • Fullness in chest
  • Vomiting
  • Heaviness of the body
  • Edema
  • Dysuria
  • Maybe thirst
  • Maybe diarrhea
  • Maybe scanty urine
  • Maybe lower abdominal fullness
  • T: Normal
  • C: Greasy and white
  • P: Floating and slippery or tight
Treatment Principle
  • Relieve the Exterior
  • Expel Cold
  • Disperse the Lungs
  • Stop asthma
  • Expel water or Phlegm
Herb Formulas
  • Xiao Qing Long Tang
  • With Internal Heat and irritability: Xiao Qing Long Jia Shi Gao Tang
  • With indigestion leading to diarrhea: Si Ling San
  • Severe: Wu Pi Yin modified
Points
  • Moxa Dingchuan, DU-4, REN-4, REN-6, needle DU-16, GB-20, LI-4, LU-7, REN-17, REN-22, SP-6, SP-9, SJ-5, ST-40, UB-12, UB-13
    With Internal Heat and irritability: - moxa + LI-11, ST-44
  • With Qi Deficiency: KI-7, REN-8, REN-9, SP-6, SP-9, ST-28
  • With Excess type swelling (severe swelling, oliguria, constipation): DU-2, DU-6, LI-4, LU-7, REN-9, SP-6, SP-9, ST-28, ST-36, ST-43, UB-27

  • Cold-Damp Dysentery: Moxa or moxa needle LI-4, REN-6, REN-12, SP-6, ST-25, ST-37
  • Cold-Damp Diarrhea: Moxa or moxa needle REN-6, REN-12, ST-25, ST-36, ST-37, UB-25
  • Cold-Damp Vaginal Discharge: Moxa or moxa needle GB-26, REN-4, REN-6, SP-6, ST-36, UB-30
  • Cold-Damp Jaundice (Yin Jaundice): Moxa or moxa needle DU-9, GB-24, LIV-13, SP-9, ST-36, UB-19, UB-20 , UB-48

SHAO YIN HEAT

Clinical Manifestations

  • Irritability
  • Insomnia
  • Restlessness
  • Diarrhea
  • Anxiety
  • Edema
  • Lower abdominal distention and pain
  • Maybe hematuria
  • Painful urination with Yin Deficiency
  • Urinary dribbling
  • Decreased urination
  • Dry skin
  • Sunken eyes
  • Dry throat and/or mouth
  • Chronic sore throat (not acute)
  • Restlessness
  • Malar flush
  • Insomnia
  • Disturbed speech
  • Low grade fever, worse in afternoon
  • Yellow urine
  • Irritability with a sensation of Heat in the chest
  • Palpitations with anxiety
  • Sores in the mouth or on the tongue
  • More Heat than in Yin Deficiency (chronic low grade fever)
  • T: Red or Dark red and dry
  • C: None or Thin and yellow
  • P: Deep, thready and rapid or Thready and rapid or forceful and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Promote urination
  • Clear Heat
  • Nourish Yin
Herb Formulas
  • Zhu Ling Tang
Points
  • DU-14, HT-5, HT-7, KI-3, KI-6, KI-7, LIV-3, SP-6, UB-10, UB-15, UB-23

SPLEEN AND KIDNEY YANG DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Body cold
  • Limbs icy
  • Complexion white and shiny
  • Low back, knees and abdomen feel cold and painful
  • Lower abdomen feels cold and painful
  • Loose stools containing undigested food
  • “Cock-crow” diarrhea (occurs at about 5 am)
  • Edema of face and limbs
  • Dysuria
  • Severe:
    Abdominal ascites
  • T: Pale, moist and swollen
  • C: Thin and white or White and greasy
  • P: Deep, thready, weak and maybe slow
Treatment Principle
  • Warm and Reinforce the Spleen and Kidneys
Herb Formulas
  • Zhen Wu Tang
  • With Water Flooding: Shi Pi Yin
Points
  • Moxa or moxa needle REN-4, REN-12, SP-6, SP-9, ST-36, UB-20, UB-23
