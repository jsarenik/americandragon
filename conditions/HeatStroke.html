HEAT STROKE, SUN STROKE, SUMMERHEAT STROKE

YANG MING JING

Clinical Manifestations
  • High fever
  • Profuse perspiration
  • Great thirst with a desire to drink
  • Abdominal pain
  • Dryness
  • Irritability
  • Restlessness
  • Feverish body parts
  • Red face
  • Dry stools
  • No constipation
  • No aversion to Cold
  • T: Red, dry
  • C: Dry yellow
  • P: Rolling and rapid (gigantic)
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Reduce fever
  • Moisten and replenish fluids
Herb Formulas
  • Bai Hu Tang
  • With Qi and Body Fluid Deficiency: Bai Hu Jia Ren Shen Tang
Points
  • DU-14, KI-3, KI-6, LI-4, LI-11, SP-6, ST-44
  • With Qi and Body Fluid Deficiency: DU-14, LI-11, REN-6, REN-12, SP-3, ST-36, ST-44, UB-20
  • With diarrhea:
    To Expel Wind Cold: Sedate DU-16, GB-20, LI-4, SJ-5, UB-12
    To Disperse the Lungs: LU-7, UB-13
    To stop diarrhea: Moxa REN-8, REN-12, ST-25, ST-36
  • With vomiting but no diarrhea:
    To Expel Wind Cold: Sedate DU-16, GB-20, LI-4, SJ-5, UB-12
    To Disperse the Lungs: LU-7, UB-13
    To stop vomiting:  PC-6, SP-4

SHAO YANG FU WITH UNDERLYING LIVER QI STAGNATION

  • Urinary difficulty

  • Dizziness
  • Anxiety
  • Depression

  • Deafness

  • Palpitations

WEI STAGE SUMMERHEAT

  • Fever and chills, fever more pronounced or
  • Tidal fever
  • Thirst
  • Sweat
  • Stifling sensation in the chest
  • Restlessness
  • Scanty urine
  • Unclear head
  • Blurred vision
  • Lightheadedness
  • T: Red tip and edges
  • C: Greasy white or yellow
  • P: Rapid and soft
Treatment Principle
  • Disperse Wind
  • Clear Heat
  • Resolve Damp
Herb Formulas
  • Qing Luo Yin
  • With Qi and Yin Deficiency: Qing Shu Yi Qi Tang
  • With Cold complications: Huo Xiang Zheng Qi Tang
  • With Damp-Stagnation: Gui Ling Gan Lu Yin
  • With Exterior Cold and Interior Dampness: Xiang Ru San
  • With Severe early-stage Summerheat in the Lungs with superimposed Wind-Cold Invasion: Xing Jia Xiang Ru Yin
Points
  • Bleed UB-40, needle DU-14, DU-23, DU-24, GB-20, LI-4, LI-11, REN-9, SJ-5, SP-6, SP-9, ST-8, ST-28, Taiyang, UB-12, UB-20

PHLEGM OBSTRUCTS THE HEART ORIFICE: YANG OBSTRUCTION

Clinical Manifestations
  • Sudden collapse with loss of consciousness
  • Lockjaw (trismus)
  • Eyes open
  • Both fists clenched
  • Retention of urine
  • Constipation
  • Stiffness and spasm of limbs
  • Red face
  • Body feels hot
  • Coarse breathing
  • Bad breath
  • Restlessness
  • Involuntary movements
  • Usual sequelae of C.V.A. such as:
    Hemiplegia
    Aphasia
    Facial paralysis

  • C: Yellow and greasy
  • P: Slippery, wiry and rapid

  • Clear Phlegm
  • Open the Orifices
Herb Formulas
  • (Ju Fang) Zhi Bao Dan
  • An Gong Niu Huang Wan
  • Ling (Yang) Jiao Gou Teng Tang modified
Points
  • Bleed the Jing-Well points of the Six Hand channels, needle DU-20, DU-26, KI-1, LIV-3, ST-40
