EXTERNAL WIND

Clinical Manifestations
  • Fever
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Sweating or
  • Anhidrosis
  • A unilateral pain in the trunk
  • Headache
  • Stiff neck
  • Unilateral joint pain in the extremities
  • Back pain
  • No thirst
  • Clear urine
  • T: Pale
  • C: White
  • P: Floating and wiry
Treatment Principle
  • Dispel External wind
  • Regulate the Ying and Wei
  • Relieve pain
Herb Formulas
  • Gui Zhi Tang  modified
    Chinese Patent Medicine: Jing Fang Bai Du Pian
Points
  • GB-34, LI-4, SJ-5, ST-36, UB-40, UB-58, UB-63
    With a fever: + DU-14, LI-11
    With a stiff neck: + GB-20, UB-10
    With painful, swollen joints: + SP-9
    With a headache: + DU-20, LU-7
    With back pain: + GB-21, UB-23

WIND-PHLEGM OBSTRUCTS THE CHANNELS (FENG TAN)

Clinical Manifestations
  • Unilateral pain with a heavy sensation
  • Awkward or paralyzed limbs on one side of the body
  • Soreness or numbness of the muscles
  • Dizziness with a heavy feeling
  • Nausea
  • Painful, swollen joints
  • Poor appetite
  • Fullness of the Stomach
  • Loose, sticky stools
  • T: Normal
  • C: White and greasy
  • P: Wiry and slippery
Treatment Principle
  • Dispel Wind
  • Eliminate Phlegm
  • Relieve pain
Herb Formulas
  • Ban Xia Bai Zhu Tian Ma Tang
  • Chinese Patent Medicine: Tian Ma Qu Feng Bu Pian
Points
  • GB-20, GB-34, LI-4, LIV-3, PC-6, SP-6, ST-36
    With dizziness: + DU-20, ST-8
    With swelling of the limbs: + SJ-6, SP-9, ST-39
    With a feeling of dullness or fullness in the Stomach: + REN-12, SP-4
    With soreness and numbness of the limbs: + SP-1, SP-21
    With a poor appetite: + SP-3, UB-20
    With nausea and vomiting: + ST-40, UB-21

LIVER QI STAGNATION

Clinical Manifestations

  • The pain can be aggravated by a bad mood
  • Insomnia
  • Irritability
  • Migraine
  • Irregular menstruation
  • A dull feeling in the Stomach
  • Lower abdominal pain
  • T: Dark
  • C: White
  • P: Wiry

  • Soothe the Liver
  • Regulate Qi
  • Relieve pain
Herb Formulas
  • Xiao Yao San
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Xiao Yao Wan
    Shu Gan Wan
Points
  • GB-41, LIV-3, PC-6, SP-6, UB-18

    With irritability: + HT-6, LIV-2

LIVER WIND STIRRING INTERNALLY: BLOOD DEFICIENCY GENERATES WIND

Clinical Manifestations
  • Pale complexion
  • Pale lips
  • Blurred or failing vision
  • Spots before eyes
  • Dizziness
  • Dry eyes
  • Pale nail beds
  • Irritability
  • Frightens easily
  • Insomnia with much dreaming
  • Tinnitus
  • Numbness of limbs
  • Tremor, spasms or involuntary movements (chorea)
  • Involuntary shaking of head
  • Light menses or amenorrhea
  • Late menses
  • Tic or tremor of hands and feet
  • Maybe trismus
  • T: Pale
  • C: Little
  • P: Thready and wiry
Treatment Principle
  • Nourish the Blood
  • Subdue Wind
Herb Formulas
  • Si Wu Tang
  • Bu Gan Tang
  • For late stage fevers: San Jia Fu Mai Tang
Points
  • DU-16, DU-20, GB-20, LIV-3, REN-4, SP-6, ST-36, UB-17, UB-18, UB-20

PHLEGM-DAMP OBSTRUCTS THE CHANNELS (TAN YIN)

Clinical Manifestations
  • Unilateral pain
  • Swollen joints
  • Exacerbated by Cold and Dampness
  • The condition is long and seems incurable
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Dizziness
  • Lassitude
  • Anorexia
  • Fatigue
  • Abdominal pain
  • A sensation of fullness in the Stomach
  • Four limbs feel tired and heavy
  • Maybe acid regurgitation or vomiting
  • Loose stools
  • Shortness of breath
  • T: Pale and flabby - may have teethmarks
  • C: White and slimy
  • P: Slippery
Treatment Principle
  • Tonify the Spleen and Stomach
  • Resolve Dampness
  • Expel Wind-Dampness
  • Transform Phlegm
  • Unblock the channels
Herb Formulas
  • Ban Xia Fu Ling Wan Jia Wei
  • Ban Xia Bai Zhu Tian Ma Tang
  • With Blood Stasis including localized, fixed pain, severe restriction of movement and numbness of the hands and feet: Xiao Huo Luo Dan
  • Upper Jiao: Er Zhu Tang
  • Chinese Patent Medicines: Ren Shen Zai Zao Wan
