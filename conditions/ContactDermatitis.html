CONTACT DERMATITIS (LACQUER DERMATITIS)

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • An acute or chronic inflammation caused by contact with substances
    The lesions range from transient redness to severe swelling with bulla formation
    Itching
    Vesiculation
    Severe cases:
    Wheals develop almost immediately at the site of contact
    The lesions may spread to other areas
    If the cause is removed:
    Redness and swelling disappears in a few days
    Blisters dry up in a few days
    Vesicles and bullae may rupture, weep and crust
    Scaling and temporary thickening of the skin occur
    Continued exposure may cause infection
    Dry skin and bleeding may occur
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
  • Topical:
    San Huang Xi Ji
    Qing Dai San
    Zao Shi Xi Yao
    Acute: Decoct Jie Du Xi Yao + Zhi Yang Xi Yao. Apply the cool decoction to the affected area(s). Then apply Huang Bai San or Shi Zhen San mixed with sesame oil to the affected area(s). Repeat two or three times daily.
    Chronic: Apply Hei Dou Liu You Ruan Gao to the affected area(s) 2-4 times daily.
Points
  • DU-14, HT-7, LI-11, SP-6, SP-10, ST-36
    For severe itching: + HT-8
    For pronounced Dampness: + SP-9
    For Blood Deficiency: + UB-17

DAMP-HEAT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Skin lesions after contact with a foreign substance
  • Abrupt onset
  • The lesions have well defined borders
  • Short course
  • Main sites involved:
    Antecubital and popliteal fossa
  • Lesions:
    Pinpoint papules
    Papulovesicles
    Vesicles, some of which coalesce
  • Slight infiltration and severe, painful itching is likely
  • Maybe necrosis
  • Water blisters
  • The skin is rough and itchy
  • Local skin flushing
  • Skin erosion
  • Bleeding or exudation follows rupture of skin after scratching
  • Erythema
  • Disturbing sleep
  • Scratching will cause slight bleeding or result in the formation of bloody crusts
  • Severe cases:
    Burning
    Itching
    Pain
  • T: Red
  • C: Thin and yellow or white or Sticky and yellow
  • P: Soggy and rapid or Floating
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Dispel Dampness
  • Support Zheng Qi
  • Alleviate itching
Herb Formulas
  • Chu Shi Wei Ling Tangmodified
  • Xiao Feng Dao Chi Tang + Long Dan Xie Gan Tang
  • Dang Gui Nian Tong Tang
  • Bei Xie Shen Shi Tang modified
  • Dermatitis Medicamentosa #1
  • Wu Wei Xiao Du Yin
  • Pu Ji Xiao Du Yin
  • Qing Jie Pian
  • If Heat has entered the Ying Stage: Qing Ying Tang
  • Damp-Heat in the Lower Jiao: Er Miao San
  • Topical:
    Hei Dou Liu You Ruan Gao Apply 2 times/day
    Fu Fang Ku Shen Tang
    Dermatitis Compress #1
    Qing Dai Gao
    Dermatitis Compress #3
    Di Yu Er Cang Hu Gao
    For dryness and fissuring: Run Ji Gao
    For relatively severe itching without exudation: Di Yu Er Cang Hu Gao
    For recurrent severe itching without weeping: Bu Mian Ca Ji
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Long Dan Xie Gan Wan
    Fang Feng Tong Sheng Wan
