TRIGEMINAL NEURALGIA (TIC DOLOREUX)

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Sudden paroxysmal burning in the facial region
  • Sudden pain while talking
  • Often appears after exposure to Wind
  • Sometimes the patient wakes from sleep
  • The pain is severe lasts from several seconds to two minutes
  • The pain suddenly stops
  • Pain mostly occurs in the maxillary and mandibular branches
  • Pain in the optic branch is rare
  • Most commonly seen in middle aged or older people
  • Seen more in women than men
  • Optic branch:
    Forehead
    Nose
    Maxillary skin
    Eyes
  • Maxillary branch:
    Facial skin
    Upper lip
    Hard palate
    Maxillary sinus
  • Mandibular branch:
    Mandible
    Oral cavity
    Teeth
    Tongue
    External ear
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
  • Trigeminal Neuralgia #1
  • San Chong Jiao Nang
Points
  • LI-3, LI-4
  • GB-1, LI-4, Taiyang, Yintang
  • GB-20, LI-4, LI-10, SJ-17, ST-7
  • ST-2, ST-4, Yuyao
  • LI-4, ST-7, Jiachengjiang
  • Main point: ST-7
    Optic branch: + Yuyao
    Maxillary branch: + ST-2
    Mandibular branch: + Jiachengjiang
  • Optic branch:
    1. GB-14, SJ-23 through to GB-7, UB-2
    2. GB-14, SJ-5, Taiyang, UB-2
    3. GB-3, GB-14, Taiyang, UB-2
    4. GB-14, UB-2
    5. Taiyang through to ST-7 (2 cun deep along the wall of the bone)
       For pain in the supraorbital region: + GB-14, SJ-23, ST-8, UB-2,     Yuyao
  • Maxillary branch:
    1. SI-18 through to ST-3, ST-7 or SJ-21 through to ST-7
    2. DU-26, LI-4, ST-2, ST-3
    3. ST-7, Jiachengjiang
    4. GB-1, SI-18, ST-2, ST-3, ST-7
    5. ST-2, ST-3, Taiyang
    6.     ST-7 (2 cun deep along the wall of the bone), LI-20 toward
        ST-3 (1 cun deep along the bone surface), LI-19 (1.5 cun deep     along the bone surface), ST-3 towards ST-7 (3 cun deep along the bone surface)
  • Mandibular branch:
    1. ST-7 or ST-6 through to ST-5
    2. REN-24, ST-6, ST-7, ST-44

  • For Wind: + LI-4, SJ-5
  • If the patient has Heart disease, hypotension, or anemia: + PC-6 on the affected side
  • Ear: Forehead, Mandible, Maxilla, Sympathetic
  • Scalp: Lower 2/3 of the sensory area
