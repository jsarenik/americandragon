LUPUS ERYTHEMATOSIS

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Systemic:
    Lupus erythematosis is a type of autoimmune, connective tissue disease.
    Skin lesions
    Fresh, red colored, macular patches with sticky scales
    Mostly found over the nasal and cheek regions
    May be fused into a butterfly shape
    Mouth ulcers
    Loss of scalp hair
    Fever
    Arthralgia
    The Heart, Kidneys, Liver and other organs may be simultaneously involved
    May also be found on the:
    Frontal and mandibular regions
    Scalp
    Ears
    Auditory canals
    Limbs
    Trunk
  • Chronic disciform:
    Atrophic, depressed erythema with distinct borders
    Mostly found over the nasal and cheek regions
    May be fused into a butterfly shape
    Telangiectasis
    Adhesive scaling
    More or less symmetrical distribution
    The labial mucosa may exhibit grayish-white ulcerations with shallow festering
    Slow progression
    No systemic symptoms
    May also be found on the:
    Frontal and mandibular regions
    Scalp
    Lips
    Ears
    Auditory canals
    Limbs
    Trunk
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
  • Topical:
    Huang Lian Gao
    Grind 20g Hb. Artemisiae Annuae Qing Hao into a fine powder and mix thoroughly with 80g petroleum jelly and apply to the lesions.
Points
  • LI-4, LI-11, LI-20, PC-3, ST-2, every fifth session bleed DU-10
  • Ear: Heart, Liver, Lung, Adrenal, Shenmen
