WIND-COLD ATTACKS THE LUNGS (SEVERE)

Clinical Manifestations
  • Fever and chills (chills predominant)
  • Anhidrosis
  • Cough
  • Wheezing (asthma)
  • Copious, white, stringy, difficult to expectorate sputum
  • Stifling sensation in the chest
  • Lacrimation
  • General heaviness (especially upper body)
  • General body aches
  • No thirst
  • Floating edema (severe)
  • Considerable difficulty breathing while lying down (severe)
  • Intolerance of Cold
  • Periorbital edema
  • Dyspnea
  • Vomiting of frothy saliva during pregnancy
  • Chest distention
  • Dry heaves
  • Decreased urine
  • Subcardiac edema
  • Stridor
  • Occasional chills on the back
  • Hiccups
  • Retching
  • Dysphagia
  • Diarrhea
  • Dysuria
  • Flushed face
  • Dizziness
  • Headache
  • Inflammation of the tear ducts
  • T: Pink
  • C: Moist or White and greasy
  • P: Superficial and tight
Treatment Principle
  • Release the Exterior
  • Transform congested Fluids (Thin Mucus)
  • Transform Phlegm
  • Warm the Lungs
  • Descend Qi
  • Relieve wheezing and cough
Herb Formulas
  • Xiao Qing Long Tang
Points

  • Palpitations
  • Stabbing pain in the anterior region over the Heart
  • Suffocating sensation in the chest
  • Maybe drawing pain down the internal aspect of arm (especially the left arm)
  • Pain (in chest and/or arm) tends to be severe and intermittent
  • Severe cases:
    Cold limbs
    Cyanosis of lips and nails

KIDNEY YANG DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Chronic wheezing
  • Inhalation more difficult than exhalation
  • Worse with exertion, fatigue or Cold
  • Lumbago: low back feels cold, weak and aching
  • Feeling chilled
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Cold limbs
  • General debility
  • Pale or cyanotic face
  • Dark rings under the eyes
  • Facial puffiness
  • Spontaneous sweating
  • Frequent colds
  • Urinary frequency with abundant, clear urine or
  • Oliguria with pitting edema
  • Maybe nocturia
  • Lethargy
  • Listlessness
  • Withdrawn, subdued behavior
  • Knees feel cold and aching
  • Dizziness
  • Tinnitus
  • Impotence
  • Spermatorrhea
  • Premature ejaculation
  • Low libido
  • Female infertility ("uterus cold and infertile")
  • Maybe anorexia
  • Maybe loose stools
  • Maybe chronic diarrhea or "cock crow" diarrhea
  • T: Pale and swollen or Pueplish/bluish
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Deep and thready - maybe slow or Imperceptible or Large, deficient and rootless
Treatment Principle
  • Warm and Reinforce Kidney Yang
  • Support Wei Qi
Herb Formulas
  • Jin Gui Shen Qi Wan modified
  • With Phlegm Accumulation: Su Zi Jiang Qi Tang
  • With Heart Yang Deficiency: Zhen Wu Tang
  • With Heart Yang Collapse:
    Si Ni Jia Ren Shen Tang
    Hei Xi Dan
    Shen Fu Tang
  • Scarring plaster: Place a plaster of Bul. Allii Sativi Da Suan or Hb. cum Rx. Asari Xi Xin on DU-14 and/or UB-43 for two to three days to strengthen Wei Qi.
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Jin Gui Shen Qi Wan
    Ren Shen Lu Rong Wan
    Ge Jie Bu Shen Wan
Points
  • KI-3, LU-9, REN-4, REN-6, UB-13, UB-23
    With congested Fluids: + KI-7, REN-9, SP-6, SP-9, ST-28
