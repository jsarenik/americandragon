WIND-HEAT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Acute cough with yellow, blood-streaked mucus
  • Inflammation of throat and/or tongue
  • Fever and chills with more fever than chills
  • Perspiration.
  • Cough with sticky yellow sputum
  • Hemoptysis sometimes with Blood in the sputum and sometimes pure Blood
  • Headache
  • Sore throat
  • General bodily soreness and malaise
  • Aversion to Wind and Heat.
  • Yellow nasal discharge
  • Thirst with desire for cold drinks
  • Red bumps around mouth and lips
  • Nasal obstruction
  • Dyspnea
  • Chest pain
  • Dry mouth and nose
  • Rapid breathing
  • Maybe constipation
  • Maybe scanty urine
  • T: Red tip and rim
  • C: Thin and yellow or Thin and white
  • P: Rapid and floating
Treatment Principle
  • Release the Exterior
  • Clear Heat
  • Cool the Blood
  • Stop bleeding

  • Sang Xing Tangmodified
