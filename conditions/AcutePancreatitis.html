YANG MING FU

Clinical Manifestations
  • High fever (sometimes rises and falls)
  • Great thirst
  • Profuse perspiration
  • Aversion to Heat
  • Abdominal pain (worse with pressure)
  • Abdominal distension
  • Dryness
  • Constipation (hard dry knots) or
  • Watery diarrhea
  • Dislikes pressure
  • Restlessness
  • Irritability
  • Mental disturbance:
    Mentally unclear
    Delirious speech
  • T: Red and dry, or black and moist
  • C: Thick yellow or dry yellow
  • P: Deep, strong and forceful
Treatment Principle
  • Purge
Herb Formulas
  • Da Cheng Qi Tang
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Dao Chi Pian
    Qing Fei Yi Huo Pian
Points
  • LI-4, LI-11, SJ-6, ST-25, ST-37, ST-44, UB-25
  • For Heat: DU-14, LI-4, LI-11, SJ-5, ST-44
  • For Stagnation: REN-12, ST-34, ST-36, ST-37, ST-39
  • For Constipation: SJ-6, ST-25, UB-25

SHAO YANG - YANG MING

  • Burning diarrhea

STOMACH FIRE

  • Burning Heat and pain in the epigastrium
  • The pain is worse with pressure and or spicy hot foods
  • Irritating thirst: drinks copious fluids and prefers cold drinks
  • Epigastric distention
  • Excessive appetite: hungry again quickly after eating (as if food vanishes)
  • Thirst with a desire for cold drinks
  • Swelling, pain, ulceration and bleeding of gums
  • Foul breath
  • Toothache
  • Maybe vomiting right after eating
  • Vomiting acid or undigested food
  • Heartburn
  • Constipation or maybe diarrhea
  • Restlessness
  • Irritability

  • T: Red
  • C: Thick, yellow and dry or Peeled or A wide, shallow crack

BLOOD STAGNATION IN THE STOMACH

  • Pain is severe after eating
