PARASITES

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • The following symptoms which are unresponsive to normal treatment should lead to a suspicion of parasitosis:
    Altered bowel habits
    Loose, watery stools or
    Constipation
    Rectal itching and burning especially at night
    Excessive appetite
    Food allergies
    Symptoms improve after eating
    Epigastric pain
    Heartburn
    Swelling around the umbilicus
    Respiratory symptoms with hepatomegaly
    Hepatobiliary symptoms
    Depression
    Foul intestinal gas
    Weight loss
    Breast swelling and tenderness unrelated to menstruation
  • Parasites can be asymptomatic in up to 50% of the infected population.
  • Lab tests may often show a false negative.
Treatment Principle
  • Kill parasites
Herb Formulas
  • For Large Intestine flukes (Fasciolopsis):
    Bing Wu Huo Qi Tang
    Hua Chong Wan
    Dan Dao Qu Hui Tang
  • For Bladder worms (cysticercosis): Hua Chong Wan
  • Topical:
    For crabs, lice and itching associated with infestations: Zhi Yang Ding
    For dermatitis due to schistomiasis: Qing Dai Gao

ROUNDWORMS (ASCARIASIS LUMBRICOIDES)

Clinical Manifestations
  • Generally found in areas where human feces is used as fertilizer
  • Pulmonary phase:
    Cough
    Dyspnea
    Allergies
    Urticaria
    Asthma
    Eosinophilia
  • Intestinal phase:
    Abdominal discomfort
    Abdominal pain
    Occasional vomiting

HOOKWORMS (TRICHURIS TRICHURA, NECATUR AMERICANUS, ANCYLOSTOMA DUODINALE)

Clinical Manifestations
  • Found in tropical and subtropical regions
  • Early stage:
    Dermatitis with a pruritic erythematous papulovesicular lesion at the site of the larval invasion
    Episodic coughing
    Asthma
    Fever
    Eosinophilia
    Blood tinged sputum
  • Later stage:
    Diarrhea
    Abdominal discomfort
    Anorexia
    Anemia with fatigue
    Pallor
    Deformed nails
    Dyspnea on exertion
    Palpitations
    Peripheral edema
    Heart failure
Treatment Principle
  • Expel hookworms
Herb Formulas
  • Guan Zhong Tang
  • Fei Er Wan
  • Hua Chong Wan
  • Fa Mu Wan
  • Topical: Qing Dai Gao

TAPEWORMS (CESTODIASIS, TAENIASIS)

Clinical Manifestations
  • This includes:
    Beef Tapeworm (Taenia Saginata)
    Fish Tapeworm (Dyphllobothrium Latum)
    Pork Tapeworm (Taenia Solum)
  • Found worldwide
  • There are actually six types of tapeworm including rat, dog and human in addition to the three listed above. Fish and pork tapeworm find more limited distribution than the others
  • Usually asymptomatic
    There may be vague gastrointestinal symptoms such as:
    Diarrhea
    Nausea
    Abdominal pain
    Anorexia
    Fatigue
    Hunger
    Dizziness
Treatment Principle
  • Expel Tapeworms
Herb Formulas

LIVER AND GALL BLADDER STAGNANT HEAT (BILE DUCT OBSTRUCTION WITH HEAT)

Clinical Manifestations
  • Quickly developing jaundice
  • Severe, colicky, recurrent right-sided hypochondriac pain
  • Pain may radiate to the shoulder and upper back
  • Fever and chills or
  • Alternating fever and chills
  • Bitter taste in the mouth
  • Dry throat and mouth
  • Nausea
  • Anorexia
  • Aversion to fats and oils
  • Abdominal distention
  • Pale, sticky stools
  • Scanty, concentrated urine
  • Dysuria
  • T: Red
  • C: Yellow
  • P: Wiry and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Soothe the Liver
  • Drain the Gall Bladder
  • Clear Heat
  • Relieve fullness
Herb Formulas
  • Dan Dao Qi Hui Tang
  • Dang Gui Long Hui Wan
  • Jin Ling Zi San
  • Chinese patent medicines:
    Ji Gu Cao Wan
    Li Dan Pian
    Long Dan Xie Gan Wan
Points
  • DU-9. GB-24, GB-34, LIV-3, LIV-14, SI-4, UB-19
    With worms in the bile duct: + LI-20 through to ST-2
    With fever : + DU-14, LI-11
    With alternating fever and chills: + GB-41, SJ-5
    With nausea : + PC-6
