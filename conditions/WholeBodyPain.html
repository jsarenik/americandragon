WHOLE BODY PAIN

WIND-COLD

Clinical Manifestations
  • Acute onset of whole body pain
  • Stiff neck
  • Back pain
  • Joint pain
  • Itching or tickling in the throat
  • Cough with expectoration of thin, watery or frothy white sputum
  • Chills with low grade fever
  • Anhidrosis
  • No thirst
  • Headache
  • Aversion to Wind and Cold
  • Blocked and / or runny nose with clear, watery nasal discharge
  • Sneezing
  • Maybe asthma
  • Clear urine
  • T: Pink
  • C: Thin, white and moist
  • P: Floating and tight
Treatment Principle
  • Expel Wind
  • Dispel Cold
  • Ventilate the Lungs
  • Relive pain
Herb Formulas
  • Ma Huang Tang  modified
  • Chinese Patent Medicine: Jing Fang Bai Du Pian
Points
  • GB-40, LI-4, SJ-5, ST-36, UB-40, UB-60
    With a headache    : + GB-20, LU-7
    With a stiff neck: + GB-21, UB-10
    With back pain: + Ahshi, UB-58, UB-63
    With joint pain: + local Ahshi
    With a fever and aversion to Cold: + DU-14, LI-11
  • DU-16, GB-20, LI-4 + LU-7 or LI-4 + KI-7, LU-5, UB-12, UB-13

WIND-DAMP

Clinical Manifestations
  • Whole body pain
  • Heavy, painful head
  • Generalized sensation of heaviness
  • Soreness and numbness of the muscles
  • Back pain
  • Painful, swollen joints
  • Generalized pain or muscle ache
  • Difficulty bending or rotating trunk
  • Mild fever
  • Slight aversion to Wind and Cold
  • Dizziness
  • Anorexia
  • Anhidrosis
  • Stiffness of the neck
  • Sciatica
  • Chest oppression
  • Chills with a low-grade fever
  • Thirst with no desire to drink
  • No perspiration
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Lassitude
  • Scanty urination
  • Loose, sticky stools
  • T: Pink
  • C:White or Greasy and white
  • P: Superficial or Superficial and slow or Superficial and soft or Superficial and tight or Deep and slippery
Treatment Principle
  • Dispel Wind
  • Eliminate Dampness
  • Alleviate pain
Herb Formulas
  • Qiang Huo Sheng Shi Tang  modified
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Tian Ma Qu Feng Bu Pian
    Feng Shi Pian
Points
  • GB-34, LU-7, SJ-6, SP-6, SP-9, ST-36, UB-60
    With a low-grade fever: + LI-4, UB-20
    With joint pain: + local Ahshi, UB-11, UB-20
    With a headache: + DU-20, Taiyang
    With a poor appetite: + REN-12, UB-20
    With numbness in the muscles: + SP-4

WIND-COLD-DAMP

Clinical Manifestations
  • Sciatica pain
  • Whole body pain
  • Inability to rotate the body
  • Hard stools
  • The pain is intense and moves around
  • Swollen painful joints (especially lower limbs)
  • Joints are warm to the touch (may be burning and swollen)
  • The pain is worse at night
  • Reduced range of motion
  • Joint degeneration or deformity in the lumbar region
  • Cold body
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Anhidrosis
  • Weight loss
  • Loose stools or
  • Diarrhea
  • Shortness of breath
  • Muscular atrophy
  • Difficulty walking and moving
  • Thirst
  • Poor appetite
  • Hemiplegia
  • Heaviness of the body and limbs
  • Headache
  • Dizziness
  • Vertigo
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Edema
  • Emaciation
  • Swollen feet
  • Numbness of the extremities
  • Paralysis
  • Fatigue
  • T: Normal
  • C: White and greasy or Thin, yellow and greasy
  • P: Floating, thready and moderate
Treatment Principle
  • Warm the channels
  • Disperse Cold
  • Resolve Dampness
  • Stop pain
Herb Formulas
  • Bai Zhu Fu Zi Tang
  • With Cold predominant: Wu Tou Tang
  • With Qi and Blood Stagnation: Qu Feng Huo Xue Tang
  • Long-term: Wu Tou Tang Jia Jian

WIND-DAMP-HEAT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Whole body pain with acute onset or
  • Chronic pain with acute aggravation
  • A hot sensation in the whole body
  • Swelling of the body
  • Pain is worse with Heat
  • Pain is alleviated by Cold
  • Fever maybe high
  • Maybe slight chills
  • Headache
  • Perspiration
  • Nervousness
  • Thirst
  • Irritability
  • Arthralgia involving one or several joints
  • Low back pain
  • Dry mouth and tongue
  • A bitter taste in the mouth
  • Localized redness and Heat
  • Swelling of the joints with burning pain
  • Severe limitation of movement
  • Aversion to Heat
  • Restlessness
  • Flushed face
  • Desire for cold drinks
  • Pain worse at night
  • Pain relieved by Cold
  • Occasional vomiting
  • Scanty, yellow urine
  • Constipation
  • T: Pink or Red
  • C: White or Yellow or Thick and yellow
  • P: Wiry and rapid or Slippery and rapid or Big and forceful

  • Clear Heat
  • Eliminate Dampness
  • Stop pain

KIDNEY YANG DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • A weak constitution or chronic disease
  • Whole body pain with a cold sensation
  • Lumbago: low back feels cold, weak and aching
  • Feeling chilled
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Cold limbs
  • General debility
  • Pale face
  • Urinary frequency with abundant, clear urine or
  • Oliguria with edema
  • Maybe nocturia
  • Lassitude
  • Withdrawn, subdued behavior
  • Knees feel cold and aching
  • Dizziness
  • Tinnitus
  • Impotence
  • Spermatorrhea
  • Premature ejaculation
  • Low libido
  • Female infertility ("uterus cold and infertile")
  • Maybe anorexia
  • Maybe loose stools
  • Maybe chronic diarrhea or "cock crow" diarrhea
  • T: Pale and swollen
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Deep and thready - maybe slow
Treatment Principle
  • Warm and Reinforce Kidney Yang
  • Relieve pain
Herb Formulas
  • Fu Zi Tang
  • Chinese Patent Medicines: Jin Gui Shen Qi Wan
Points
  • DU-20, KI-3, REN-4, REN-6, ST-36, UB-23
    For pain with a cold sensation: + moxa REN-8
    With low back pain: + UB-25, UB-58
    With dizziness: + GB-20, GB-39
    With lassitude: + SP-3, UB-20
    With a poor appetite: + REN-12, SP-4
  • Moxa or moxa needle DU-4, KI-3, REN-4, UB-23

BLOOD STAGNATION

Clinical Manifestations
  • A history of trauma or surgery
  • Long-term body pain with a fixed location
  • Pain is stabbing and/or severe
  • Pain is worse with pressure
  • Joint pain
  • Whole body pain which is induced by a change of position or movement
  • Pain worse at night
  • Dark complexion
  • Fixed, palpable masses
  • Bleeding with clots
  • Purple lips
  • Purple nails
  • Dry skin
  • Thirst with no desire to drink
  • Blue, dark or purplish discoloration of skin in small or large areas
  • Varicose and spider veins
  • Cold hands and feet
  • Tendency to hemorrhage dark blood or clots
  • T: Purple, possible purple or red spots
  • C: White
  • P: Deep and choppy
Treatment Principle
  • Invigorate the Blood
  • Break up Stagnation
  • Relieve pain
Herb Formulas
  • Shen Tong Zhu Yu Tang  modified
  • For pain in the extremities: Zhu Yu Tang
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Yan Hu Suo Pian
    Xiao Huo Luo Dan
Points
  • GB-34, LI-4, LIV-3, SP-10, ST-36, UB-17
    With sharp pain: + PC-6, SP-6
    With joint pain: + Ahshi, the Accumulation (Xi Cleft) points of the involved channel
    With pain induced by movement: + SJ-5, GB-39
    With pain, worse at night: + HT-7, KI-6
  • SP-6, SP-10, ST-36, UB-17, UB-40
