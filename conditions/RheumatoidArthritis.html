RHEUMATOID ARTHRITIS

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Beginning Stage:
    Disabling inflammatory disease
    Chronic Synovitis
    Joint Lesions
    Pannus-Corneal vascularization (localized malignant inflammatory proliferation)
    Immunological origin- auto immune
    Self sustained
    Rheumatoid nodules
    Lung symptoms
    Vessel Problems
    Pericardium problems
    Above three conditions alternate with joint problems
    Fever
  • Chronic Stage:
    Beginning stage may last several months or years and may not progress beyond this point
    More often moves on, intermittently with
    Worsening of joints
    New invasion of healthy joints
    May affect organs
    Qi and Blood circulation slowed
    Blood and turbid Phlegm stagnate joints (stubborn Bi)
    Joints show chronic inflammation
    Pain occurs spontaneously or from pressure or with movement
    Pain and stiffness worse at night and on awakening
    Disappears late in the morning or afternoon and require a long period of warming up
    Sensitive to weather
    Locations of joint pain depend on patient (antalgic attitudes)
