NECK PAIN, STIFFNESS AND TENSION

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Neck pain and stiffness
  • Obstruction of the Qi's downward movement from the head to the trunk:
    Obstruction at UB-10:
    Severe pain in the shoulders and neck
    Stiff neck
    Spasms
    Torticollis
    Obstruction at GB-21:
    Spasms of the trapezius muscle
    Obstruction at ST-11:
    Sore throat
    Difficulty tolerating anything tight around the neck
  • Obstruction of the Qi's upward movement from the trunk to the head:
    Obstruction at SI-14:
    Stiff neck
    Torticollis
    A Cold sensation at the nape
    Obstruction at SJ-15:
    Pain in the hand
    Pain in the Taiyang channels at the nape, back and shoulders
    Numbness or coolness around the ear
  • Diffuse Yang Deficiency at the nape of the neck:
    Cervical weakness requiring a cervical collar
    Pain under strain or exhaustion
    Pain is better with pressure and warmth
  • Torticollis: Wry neck, or torticollis, is a painfully twisted and tilted neck. The top of the head generally tilts to one side while the chin tilts to the other side. This condition can be congenital (present at birth) or acquired.
    Spasmodic torticollis: A constant contracture of the neck muscles causing a rotation or inclination of the head
