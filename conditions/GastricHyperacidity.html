GASTRIC HYPERACIDITY (SOUR STOMACH)

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
  • Gastric Hyperacidity #1
  • Bao He Wan
Points
  • REN-12, ST-25, ST-36, UB-10, UB-11, UB-17, UB-18, UB-20
  • LI-4, PC-6, SP-6, ST-36

SHAO YANG JING

Clinical Manifestations
  • Alternating chills and fever
  • Restlessness
  • Irritability
  • Anorexia
  • Sometimes fatigue
  • Nausea
  • Occasional vomiting
  • Headaches (side of head)
  • Hypochondriac pain
  • Rib pain
  • Achiness
  • Ear infection
  • Hearing difficulty
  • Red eyes
  • Stiff shoulders
  • Throat pain
  • Hip pain
  • T: Normal with bumps or dots on side
  • C: Thin white
  • P: Wiry
Treatment Principle
  • Harmonize the Liver and Gall Bladder
Herb Formulas
  • Xiao Chai Hu Tang
Points
  • GB-41, SJ-5
  • To harmonize the Liver and Gall Bladder: GB-34, LIV-3, LIV-14, SJ-5
  • To stop nausea and vomiting: PC-6, SP-4, ST-36
  • To treat headache: GB-1, GB-8, GB-14, Taiyang
  • To treat constipation: SJ-6, ST-25
  • To treat fever: DU-13, DU-14
  • To support the San Jiao and Regulate Yin: PC-5, PC-6
  • To support Yin: KI-7
  • To support the Ying: SP-10
  • Head: GB-8, SJ-20, Yintang
  • Eyes: GB-14, GB-20, ST-1, UB-1, UB-2
  • Ear: GB-2, SJ-5, SJ-17, SJ-21
  • Hypochondriac area: GB-34, GB-40
    Ear: Shenmen, Gall Bladder, Point 0

SHAO YANG FU

Clinical Manifestations
  • Alternating chills and fever (not so likely)
  • Restlessness
  • Irritability
  • Continuous vomiting
  • Headache (side of head)
  • Hypochondriac pain
  • Epigastric pain
  • Bitter taste in mouth
  • Digestive disruption
  • Anorexia
  • Nausea
  • Acid regurgitation
  • Burning diarrhea or
  • Constipation
  • Menstrual problems
  • Dry throat
  • Heart pain with vomiting
  • Throat pain
  • Hip pain
  • T: Red
  • C: Thin yellow
  • P: Wiry, forceful
Treatment Principle
  • Harmonize the Liver and Gall Bladder
  • Purge Internal Heat
Herb Formulas
  • Da Chai Hu Tang
Points
  • To harmonize the Liver and Gall Bladder: GB-34, LIV-3, LIV-14, SJ-5
  • For nausea and vomiting: PC-6, SP-4, ST-36
  • For headache: GB-1, GB-8, GB-14, Taiyang
  • For constipation: SJ-6, ST-25
  • For fever: DU-13, DU-14
  • For Stomach Heat: LI-4, LI-11, ST-44
  • To support the San Jiao and Regulate Yin: PC-5, PC-6
  • To support Yin: KI-7
  • To support the Ying: GB-41, SJ-5 , SP-10
  • To regulate menstruation: SP-6
  • For Shao Yang Fu with Tai Yang:
    For the Shao Yang: GB-34, LIV-3, LIV-14, SJ-6
    For the Tai Yang: GB-20, UB-12
  • For Shao Yang Fu with underlying Liver Qi Stagnation:
    To harmonize the Shao Yang: GB-34, LIV-3, SJ-6
    To clear Heat: LI-11, ST-44
    To calm the Shen: HT-7, PC-6
    Ear: Shenmen, Liver, Gall Bladder, bleed Heat points

STOMACH QI DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Epigastric fullness and distention
  • No desire for food and drink
  • Frequent belching
  • Borborygmus
  • Loss of sensation of taste
  • Stools not solid
  • Urine clear and abundant
  • T: Pale
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Weak and Deficient
Treatment Principle
  • Strengthen Qi
  • Invigorate the Spleen and Stomach
Herb Formulas
  • Xuan Fu Dai Zhe Tang
Points
  • REN-6, REN-12, SP-6, ST-21, ST-36, UB-20, UB-21

LIVER QI STAGNATION INVADING THE SPLEEN

Clinical Manifestations
  • Abdominal distention and pain
  • Gas
  • Borborygmus
  • Diarrhea
  • Distention and pain in the sides or flanks
  • Suffocating sensation in the chest
  • Tendency to sigh
  • Melancholy
  • Depression
  • Irritability
  • Inappropriate anger
  • Women:
    Irregular menses
    Dysmenorrhea
    Breast distention and pain before and during menses
  • T: Pink
  • C: White and greasy
  • P: Slippery and wiry
Treatment Principle
  • Soothe the Liver
  • Invigorate the Spleen
Herb Formulas
  • Chai Hu Gui Zhi Tang
Points
  • LIV-3, LIV-13, SP-6, ST-25, ST-36, ST-37

INTERNAL COLD

Clinical Manifestations
  • Chest pain
  • Shoulder stiffness
  • Abdominal distention and pain
  • Constipation
  • Cold feet
  • An aversion to Cold
  • Pale face
  • Fatigue
  • Headaches with heavy headedness
  • Abdominal weakness
  • Anorexia
  • T: Pale
  • C: Greasy, white
  • P: Weak or Weak and wiry
Treatment Principle
  • Tonify Qi
  • Break up Stagnant Qi
  • Reduce accumulation
  • Descend Qi
  • Warm the Middle Jiao
  • Disperse Cold
  • Transform Phlegm
Herb Formulas
  • Yan Nian Ban Xia Tang
Points

FLUID AND HEAT STAGNATION

Clinical Manifestations
  • Food Stagnation
  • Firm epigastric focal distention
  • Dry heaves with a foul odor
  • The sound of Fluids in the hypochondria
  • Diarrhea
  • Very loud borborygmus
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Foul breath
  • Fullness and oppression in the chest and abdomen
  • Heartburn
  • Acid regurgitation
  • Ascites
  • T: Normal
  • C: Thin, yellow and greasy
  • P: Wiry, taut and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Harmonize the Stomach
  • Reduce focal distention
  • Disperse clumping
  • Expel water
  • Descend Qi
Herb Formulas
  • Sheng Jiang Xie Xin Tang
Points
