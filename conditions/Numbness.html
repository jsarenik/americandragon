NUMBNESS, PARESTHESIA,TINGLING

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Numbness describes a loss of sensation or feeling in a part of the body.
  • Paresthesia is a sensation of tingling, tickling, pricking, or burning of a person's skin with no apparent long-term physical effect.
  • The manifestation of a paresthesia may be transient or chronic.
  • The most familiar kind of paresthesia is the sensation known as "pins and needles" or of a limb "falling asleep".
Treatment Principle

  • With back pain:
