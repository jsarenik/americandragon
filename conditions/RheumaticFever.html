RHEUMATIC FEVER

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Rheumatic fever (acute rheumatic fever) is a disease that can affect the Heart, joints, brain, skin and vessels. Rheumatic fever can develop if strep throat and scarlet fever infections are not treated properly. Early diagnosis of these infections and treatment with antibiotics are key to preventing rheumatic fever.
  • Signs and symptoms:
    Carditis; including endocarditis, myocarditis and pericarditis
    Tachycardia
    Cardiac enlargement
    Systolic and diastolic murmers at the apex of the Heart
    Pericardial friction rub
    Migratory polyarthritis mostly of the large joints of the extremities
    The affected joints are red, hot, tender and swollen
    Erythema annulare
    Subcutaneous nodules
    Mild or moderate fever
    Sweating
    Weakness
    Weight loss
    Elevation of antistreptolysin
