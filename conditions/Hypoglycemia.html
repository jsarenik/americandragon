HYPOGLYCEMIA (LOW BLOOD SUGAR)

COLD-DAMP DISTRESSES THE SPLEEN

Clinical Manifestations
  • Distention and fullness of chest, epigastrium and abdomen
  • Nausea
  • Acid reflux
  • Vomiting
  • Anorexia
  • Loose stools
  • Diarrhea
  • Reduced sensation of taste
  • No thirst or preference for hot drinks
  • Sticky sensation in mouth
  • Borborygmus
  • Dizziness
  • Heavy, tired extremities
  • Lassitude
  • Somnolence particularly after eating
  • Maybe insomnia at night
  • Variable fatigue, may feel better for activity and exertion
  • Yin jaundice
  • Heavy head
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Maybe edema
  • Maybe copious white vaginal discharge
  • Maybe cold limbs, fear of Cold or feeling chilled
  • T: Pink and swollen
  • C: Thick, white and greasy or sticky
  • P: Deep and slow or Soft or Slippery
Treatment Principle
  • Warm Cold
  • Transform Dampness
  • Harmonize the Stomach
  • Strengthen the Spleen
Herb Formulas
  • Ping Wei Sanmodified
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Er Chen Wan
    Xiang Sha Liu Jun Zi Wan
Points
  • Moxa or moxa needle REN-6, REN-12, SP-6, SP-9, ST-36, UB-20
  • DU-20, GB-20, KI-6, REN-12, SP-3, SP-6, SP-9, ST-36, ST-40, UB-62
  • Cold-Damp Dysentery: Moxa or moxa needle LI-4, REN-6, REN-12, SP-6, ST-25, ST-37
  • Cold-Damp Diarrhea: Moxa or moxa needle REN-6, REN-12, ST-25, ST-36, ST-37, UB-25
  • Cold-Damp Vaginal Discharge: Moxa or moxa needle GB-26, REN-4, REN-6, SP-6, ST-36, UB-30
  • Cold-Damp Jaundice (Yin Jaundice): Moxa or moxa needle DU-9, GB-24, LIV-13, SP-9, ST-36, UB-19, UB-20 , UB-48

SPLEEN QI AND BLOOD DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Sallow, pale complexion
  • Mental and physical fatigue
  • Daytime drowsiness and a desire for sleep worse with exertion and after meals
  • May be Blood deficient insomnia at night
  • Anorexia
  • Nausea
  • Four limbs feel tired and heavy
  • Abdominal and epigastric distention after eating
  • Maybe acid regurgitation or vomiting
  • Loose stools
  • Shortness of breath
  • T: Pale and flabby - may have teethmarks
  • C: White
  • P: Even, forceless and Deficient or Deficient and weak
Treatment Principle
  • Strengthen the Spleen
  • Benefit Qi
  • Dry Dampness if necessary
Herb Formulas
  • Xiang Sha Liu Jun Zi Tang modified
  • Ren Shen Yang Rong Tang
  • Shi Quan Da Bu Tang
  • With Spleen Qi Sinking: Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Bu Zhong Yi Qi Wan
    Ba Zhen Wan
    Ren Shen Yang Ying Tang
Points
  • DU-20, KI-6, REN-12, Sishencong, SP-3, SP-6, ST-36, ST-40, UB-62
  • LIV-13, REN-12, SP-3, SP-5, SP-6, ST-36, UB-20

SPLEEN YANG DEFICEINCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Sallow complexion
  • Anorexia (maybe with vomiting)
  • Four limbs feel tired and heavy
  • Abdominal and epigastric distention after eating
  • Maybe acid regurgitation or vomiting
  • Loose stools or watery stools containing undigested food
  • Shortness of breath
  • Pain in the epigastrium with cold sensations (pain is relieved by warmth)
  • Cold limbs
  • Feeling chilled
  • Frequent, clear urination
  • Mental fatigue
  • Maybe scanty urine with edema
  • Maybe clear, watery vaginal discharge
  • T: Pale
  • C: Thin and white
  • P: Deep and weak
Treatment Principle
  • Warm the Center
  • Dispel Cold
Herb Formulas
  • Huang Qi Jian Zhong Tang
Points
  • Moxa LIV-13, REN-12, SP-3, SP-5, SP-6, ST-36, UB-20

SPLEEN AND STOMACH DEFICIENCY COLD

Clinical Manifestations
  • Mild constant epigastric pain; relieved by warmth and pressure
  • Anorexia
  • Abdominal pain and fullness after meals
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Loose, unformed stools
  • Excessive salivation
  • Cold limbs
  • Lassitude
  • Weakness
  • Sallow or pale, dull complexion
  • Maybe edema
  • Maybe scanty urination
  • Maybe clear, watery, copious vaginal discharge
  • T: Pale
  • C: White and greasy
  • P: Deep, thready and forceless
Treatment Principle
  • Strengthen the Spleen
  • Warm Yang
  • Warm the Center
  • Dispel Cold
Herb Formulas
  • Xiao Jian Zhong Tang
Points
  • Moxa or moxa needle LIV-13, REN-12, SP-3, SP-6, ST-36, UB-20, UB-21

SPLEEN DEFICIENCY WITH STOMACH EXCESS

Clinical Manifestations
  • Rapid hungering after eating
  • Sweating
  • Hunger and nervousness 2-4 hours after eating
  • Emaciation
  • Thirst
  • A clamoring Stomach
  • Abdominal fullness after eating
  • Restlessness with fatigue
  • Loose stools or
  • Constipation
  • Lassitude
  • T: Enlarged with lateral cracks
  • C: Yellow, especially in the middle
  • P: Thready, wiry and maybe rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Regulate the Spleen and Stomach
  • Strengthen the Spleen
  • Clear the Stomach
Herb Formulas
  • Ban Xia Xie Xin Tang modified
Points
  • REN-12, SP-6, ST-36, ST-44
    For spontaneous perspiration: + KI-7, LI-4
    For loose stools: + SP-9
    For constipation: + SJ-6
    For abdominal fullness after eating: + PC-6, SP-4
    For severe fatigue: + SP-3

LIVER QI STAGNATION INVADES THE SPLEEN

Clinical Manifestations
  • Weakness and discomfort with nervousness and irritability 2-4 hours after a meal
  • Abdominal distention and pain especially after meals which are worse with emotional upset or stress
  • Hypertension
  • Flank, chest, breast and diaphragmatic distention and pain
  • Gas
  • Borborygmus
  • Diarrhea
  • Distention and pain in the sides or flanks
  • Suffocating sensation in the chest
  • Tendency to sigh
  • Melancholy
  • Depression
  • Mood changes
  • Fatigue
  • Maybe a headache
  • Maybe spontaneous perspiration
  • Loose stools
  • Reduced appetite
  • Inappropriate anger
  • Women:
    Irregular menses
    Dysmenorrhea
    Breast distention and pain before and during menses
  • T: Pink or Pale
  • C: White or White and greasy
  • P: Slippery and wiry or Thready and wiry
Treatment Principle
  • Soothe the Liver
  • Invigorate the Spleen Tonify Qi
Herb Formulas
  • Xiao Yao San  modified
  • For Liver Qi Stagnation transforming into Heat with frequent anger and permanent irritability and a bitter taste in the mouth: Xiao Chai Hu Tang modified
Points
  • LIV-3, PC-6, SP-3, ST-36
    For nausea: + SP-4
    For frequent hunger and thirst: + ST-44
    For severe fatigue and weakness after meals: + UB-20, UB-21
    For Liver Qi Stagnation transforming into Heat: - LIV-3, + LIV-2
    For dizziness: + GB-20
    For tremors: + LI-4
    For spontaneous perspiration: + KI-7, LI-4
  • LIV-3, LIV-13, SP-6, ST-25, ST-36, ST-37

KIDNEY YANG DEFICIENCY WITH WATER FLOODING

Clinical Manifestations
  • Edema of whole body particularly at and below the waist
  • Pitting edema
  • Low back pain, aching and heaviness
  • Abdominal distention with full sensations
  • Oliguria
  • Cold limbs
  • Face gray and congested or pale
  • Feeling nervous and cold with mental weariness
  • In severe cases:
    Ascites
    Edema of scrotum
  • T: Pale and swollen
  • C: Thin, white or Thick, white and sticky
  • P: Deep and thready
  • With Water Flooding the Lungs:
    Cough with copious, thin watery phlegm
    Asthma with phlegmy sound (wheezing)
    Dyspnea
    Facial edema
    Edema of limbs
    Dysuria
    T: Pale and swollen
    C: White and moist or White and greasy
    P: Superficial and tight
Treatment Principle
  • Warm the Kidneys
  • Promote diuresis
Herb Formulas
  • Ji Sheng Shen Qi Wan
Points
  • KI-7, REN-6, REN-9, SP-6, SP-9, ST-28, ST-36, UB-20, UB-22, UB-23, UB-39

QI DEFICIENCY WITH PHLEGM STAGNATION

Clinical Manifestations
  • Mental confusion
  • Faintness
  • Heavy headedness, especially 2-4 hours after eating
  • Poor Appetite
  • The sound of Phlegm in the throat
  • May vomiting of clear Fluids or drool
  • Lassitude
  • Somnolence
  • Tremors of the limbs
  • T: Pale
  • C: White
  • P: Wiry and slippery
Treatment Principle
  • Strengthen the Spleen
  • Tonify Qi
  • Transform Phlegm
Herb Formulas
  • Di Tan Tang  modified
Points
  • DU-20, SP-9, ST-36, ST-40
    For severe heavy headedness and dizziness: + GB-20
    For vomiting of clear Fluids or drool: + REN-12
    For severe fatigue and weakness: + UB-20, UB-21
    For tremors of the limbs: + LI-4, LIV-3
    For somnolence: + LI-3
    For a poor appetite: + REN-21, ST-21
