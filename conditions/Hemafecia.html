HEMAFECIA, HEMATOCHEZIA, BLOOD IN THE STOOLS, RECTAL BLEEDING

WIND-HEAT IN THE STOMACH AND INTESTINES (INTESTINAL WIND)

Clinical Manifestations
  • Bleeding of fresh Blood from the bowel which often precedes the bowel movement
  • The bleeding may follow the passage of stool
  • The bleeding may be alarmingly copious
  • Abdominal pain or
  • Absence of pain, itching, swelling or distention or
  • A burning sensation in the rectum and anus
  • Dry mouth
  • Tendency toward constipation
  • Dry or difficult stools
  • T: Red
  • C: Yellow
  • P: Slippery and rapid or Thready and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat from the Intestines
  • Cool the Blood
  • Stop bleeding
Herb Formulas
  • Huai Jiao Wan modified
  • Huai Hua San modified
  • Topical: Mix 3g Alumen Ming Fan in a little water, dissolve 30g petroleum jelly and mix in the Ming Fan. Place on sterile gauze and insert into the anal canal.
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Yi Zi Tang
    Zheng Gan Zhi Gen Duan
    To stop bleeding:
    Yun Nan Bai Yao
    Shen Tian Qi Pian
    For Intestinal bleeding with Heat: Zhong Gan Zhi Gen Dan
    For Damp-Heat: Huang Lian Jie Du Wan
    With Spleen and Stomach Deficiency: Xiang Sha Yang Wei Wan
Points
  • Ahshi points, DU-1, Erbai, SP-1, ST-25, ST-37, UB-30, UB-32, UB-57
    With pain in the rectum or lower Intestine: + UB-2, UB-52
    With severe bleeding: + PC-4, SP-10
  • Ear:
    1. Large Intestine, Small Intestine, Distal Segment Rectum, Subcortex, Shenmen
    2. Hemorrhoids, Large Intestine, Distal Segment Rectum, Shenmen

  • Confusion of mind
  • Delirium

  • Excessive skin rashes all over body
  • Hemorrhage:

    Hemoptysis
    Epistaxis
    Hemafecia
    Hematuria

    C: None

  • Promote Blood Circulation
Herb Formulas

  • Da Huang Zhi Xue Jiao Nang

DAMP-HEAT IN THE LARGE INTESTINE

Clinical Manifestations
  • Difficulty passing stools
  • Diarrhea with tenesmus which intensifies after defecation
  • Frequent stools which are small in quantity
  • Stools are yellow or brown, thin, watery, with foul smell or sticky and pasty like jam or sticky fluid or containing pus and Blood
  • The stools may be loose or firm depending on the ratio of Dampness to Heat
  • Burning hot sensation in anus
  • Abdominal pain, fullness and distention
  • Urine short and red
  • Sustained fever despite profuse perspiration or
  • Raging fever in the afternoon
  • Severe cases: High fever and coma
  • Mouth dry but no desire to drink
  • A sticky sensation in the mouth
  • A bitter taste in the mouth
  • Body heavy
  • Limbs tired
  • Fullness in chest
  • Lump in epigastrium
  • Anorexia
  • Vomiting
  • T: Red
  • C: Yellow and greasy
  • P: Slippery and rapid or Soft and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Transform Dampness
  • Cool the Blood
  • Stop bleeding
Herb Formulas
  • Di Yu San Er Hao
  • Shao Yao Tang
  • For lingering Damp-Heat with mild Yin and Blood Deficiency: Zhu Ju Wan  modified
  • For lingering Damp-Heat with marked Yin Deficiency: Zhu Ju Wan+ Liu Wei Di Huan Wan  modified
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    For Damp-Heat: Huang Lian Jie Du Pian
    For Damp or Toxic Heat: Chuan Xin Lian
    For Damp-Heat with a tendency toward constipation or sluggish stools: Niu Huang Qing Huo Wan
    For bleeding from Internal Heat: Zhong Guo Zhi Gen Duan
    For bleeding :
    Yun Nan Bai Yao
    Sheng Tian Qi Pian
Points
  • Acupuncture must be given frequently for good results. 1 to 2 times daily is appropriate in severe cases.
  • LI-4, LI-11, SP-9, ST-25, ST-37, ST-44, UB-25
  • Ah Shi Points, LI-4, LI-11, SP-15, ST-25, UB-25, UB-57
    With rectal or lower Intestinal pain: + UB-2, UB-54
    With a high fever: + DU-14
    With nausea: + PC-6, REN-12
    With tenesmus: + DU-1, REN-4
    With Dampness prominent: + SP-9
    With Yin Deficiency: + REN-4, SP-6
  • Ear: Large Intestine, Small Intestine, Stomach, Kidney, Subcortex, Shenmen

  • Dark, purplish or pale Blood preceding, following or

  • Sallow complexion

  • Four limbs feel tired and heavy
  • Abdominal and epigastric distention after eating
  • Maybe acid regurgitation or vomiting
  • Intolerance to Cold

  • Cold limbs
  • Feeling chilled

STOMACH COLD

Clinical Manifestations
  • General signs and symptoms:
    Epigastrium Cold with pain
  • Mild cases:
    Constant pain
  • Severe cases:
    Sudden bouts of sharp pain
    Pain is aggravated by Cold and relieved by warmth and hot drinks
    Cold hands and feet
  • Cold Penetrates the Stomach and Intestines:
    Pain is sudden and sharp
    Pain is relieved by warmth
    Nausea
    Diarrhea
    Loose stools
    T: Pink
    C: White
    P: Deep and tight
  • Retention of Fluid in the Stomach due to Cold:
    Pain is dull
    Pain is relieved by warmth
    Succussion sound in the epigastrium on palpation
    Vomiting of clear, watery fluid
    T: Pink
    C: White and sticky
    P: Thready or Thready and slow
  • Stomach Deficiency with Stagnation of Cold:
    Pain is dull
    Pain is relieved by warmth and pressure
    Vomiting clear, watery fluid
    T: Pale
    C: Thin and white
    P: Deep and slow or Deep and thready
Treatment Principle
  • Warm the Stomach
  • Warm Cold
Herb Formulas
  • In children: Ding Yu Li Zhong Tang
Points
  • Moxa or moxa needle PC-6, REN-6, REN-12, SP-4, ST-21, ST-36, UB-20, UB-21

BLOOD STAGNATION IN THE STOMACH

Clinical Manifestations
  • Bleeding of dark, purple Blood from the bowel or
  • Sticky, tarry stools or
  • Mixed dark and fresh Blood
  • Epigastric or abdominal pain
  • The pain may be intermittent or detected on palpation
  • The pain is fixed, stabbing and /or severe
  • Pan is worse with pressure
  • The pain may also be found in the left and right lower abdomen
  • Dark complexion
  • Fixed, palpable masses
  • Bleeding with clots
  • Purple lips
  • Purple nails
  • Dry skin
  • Blue, dark or purplish discoloration of skin in small or large areas,
  • Varicose and spider veins
  • Cold hands and feet
  • Tendency to hemorrhage dark blood or clots
  • T: Purple, possible purple or red spots
    Red purple if there is Heat
    Blue purple if there is Cold
  • C: White
  • P: Choppy or Wiry and choppy
Treatment Principle
  • Activate the Blood
  • Break up Stagnation
  • Move Qi
  • Stop bleeding
Herb Formulas
  • Shi Xiao San + Dan Shen Yin  modified
  • With masses: Ge Xia Zhu Yu Tang
  • With Cold in the lower abdomen: Shao Fu Zhu Yu Tang
  • For severe Blood Stagnation in the Intestines with Heat: Tao He Cheng Qi Tang

    With Qi and Blood stagnation: Xue Fu Zhu Yu Wan

    + Yun Nan Bai Yao
