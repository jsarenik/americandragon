TAI YIN DEFICIENCY WITH COLD

Clinical Manifestations
  • Severe abdominal and epigastric pain
  • Prefers warmth and pressure
  • Abdominal distension
  • Cold body
  • Diarrhea with undigested food
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Cold and stiff or rigid extremities
  • Reduced appetite
  • Fullness in the epigastrium and abdomen (likes pressure)
  • Lack of thirst
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting clear fluid
  • Loose stools
  • Anorexia
  • Trismus
  • Constipation (Cold type)
  • Gastric spasms
  • Clear, frequent urination
  • Mild perspiration
  • Maybe syncope
  • T: Pale and maybe moist
  • C: White
  • P: Deep, minute and slow or P:  Slow, sinking or soft and weak
Treatment Principle
  • Warm Yang
  • Dispel Cold
  • TonifY Qi
  • Strengthen the Spleen
Herb Formulas
  • Fu Zi Li Zhong Wan
Points
  • For pain and Cold: Moxa the abdomen.
    To strengthen the Spleen and Stomach: ST-36, UB-20, UB-21 (moxa if severe)
    For abdominal pain and nausea: PC-6, SP-4
    For frequent, clear urination:  Moxa REN-4, REN-6 UB-23
    For diarrhea: Moxa REN-8
