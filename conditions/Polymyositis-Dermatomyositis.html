POLYMYOSITIS/DERMATOMYOSITIS

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Polymyositis is a type of chronic inflammation of the muscles (inflammatory myopathy) related to dermatomyositis and inclusion body myositis. Its name means "inflammation of many muscles". The inflammation is predominantly of the endomysium in polymyositis, whereas dermatomyositis is characterized by primarily perimysial inflammation. The muscle weakness associated with polymyositis involves the muscles closest to the trunk, such as those in the hips, thighs, shoulders, upper arms and neck. The weakness affects both the left and right sides of the body and tends to gradually worsen.
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
  • Initial-Stage: Decoct 30g Hb. Impatientis Tou Gu Cao, 25g Ram. Cinnamomi Gui Zhi and 9g Flos Carthami Hong Hua and apply as a warm wash three times a day.

WIND-DAMP-HEAT DAMAGING THE BLOOD

Clinical Manifestations
  • Dark, red, swollen skin lesions which may itch
  • Generalized pain and weakness which tends to be worse in hot weather
  • Possible hot, swollen, red, painful joints
  • Aggravation of symptoms in women before and during menstruation
  • T: Red or Pale with a red tip or Dry or Scanty (may be slimy at the root)
  • C: Yellow
  • P: Thready, slippery and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Disperse Wind
  • Clear Heat
  • Dry Dampness
  • Nourish and invigorate the Blood
Herb Formulas
  • Yang Xue Xiao Feng Zao Shi Fang
  • Da Qin Jiao Tang
  • Ma Huang Lian Qian Chi Shao Dou Tang
Points
  • DU-10, LI-11, SP-6, SP-10, local points
    For severe Blood Deficiency: + ST-36, UB-17
    With Blood Stasis: + UB-17
    For severe itching: + GB-30

LUNG HEAT DAMAGING THE FLUIDS

Clinical Manifestations
  • The initial symptoms either fever or
  • Weakness of the limbs
  • Lack of strength and numbness arise after the fever subsides
  • Heart vexation
  • Thirst
  • Dry, irritated throat
  • Maybe scanty, yellow urination
  • A tendency toward dry stools
  • T: Red
  • C: Yellow
  • P: Rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Moisten Dryness
  • Nourish the Lungs
  • Generate Fluids
Herb Formulas
  • Qing Zao Jiu Fei Tang  modified

Points
  • GB-34, KI-7, LI-4, LU-5, SJ-6, UB-11
    For a high fever: + DU-14, LI-11
    For fatigue: + ST-36

DAMP-HEAT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Heavy, encumbered extremities
  • Wilting and limpness
  • Lack of strength
  • Maybe minor simultaneous swelling and numbness
  • Condition worse in the lower extremities
  • Possible fever or emission of Heat
  • Chest and epigastrium glomus
  • Short, reddish urination
  • T: Red
  • C: Slimy and yellow or Slimy and yellowish white
  • P: soft and rapid or Slippery and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Resolve Dampness
  • Soothe the sinews
  • Unblock the channels
Herb Formulas
  • Si Miao Wan  modified
Points
  • REN-3, SP-9, ST-36, ST-44 + local points
    For Dampness predominant with chest and epigastric glomus and oppression and heavy, swollen limbs: + REN-12, SP-6

    For numb, insensitive and cold limbs: + REN-4
