BEHCET'S DISEASE

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Behcet's Disease is a rare immune-mediated small-vessel systemic vasculitis that often presents with mucous membrane ulceration and ocular problems. This is a triple-symptom complex of recurrent oral aphthous ulcers, genital ulcers, and uveitis. As a systemic disease, it can also involve visceral organs such as the gastrointestinal tract, pulmonary, musculoskeletal, cardiovascular and neurological systems. This syndrome can be fatal due to ruptured vascular aneurysms or severe neurological complications.

HEAT IN THE SPLEEN AND STOMACH

Clinical Manifestations
  • Mouth, eye and genital sores of various sizes which are mostly painful
  • The sores are primarily located in the oral cavity
  • Diffuse red-colored macular lesions on the limbs
  • Thirst with a desire for cold drinks
  • Rapid hungering
  • A clamoring Stomach
  • Possible alcohol addiction
  • Bad breath
  • Swollen, painful gums
  • T: Red
  • C: Yellow
  • P: Slippery and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Dispel Dampness
Herb Formulas
  • Gan Cao Xie Xin Tang
  • Xie Huang San
  • For more severe Excess Heat: Yu Nu Jianmodified
Points
  • LI-4, PC-7, REN-12, ST-44
    For eye sores: + GB-1, SJ-23, Taiyang, bleed UB-2
    For genital sores: + LIV-5, REN-2
    For macular lesions on the skin: + REN-10, UB-40
    For a sticky mouth with a slimy tongue coat: + SP-9

SPLEEN DEFICIENCY WITH DAMPNESS

Clinical Manifestations
  • Oral, eye or genital sores which endure and do not close
  • Pale colored, flat or concave lesions
  • Lassitude
  • Lack of strength
  • Anorexia
  • Abdominal distention and discomfort
  • Loose stools
  • Long, clear urination
  • Cold limbs
  • Maybe low-grade fever
  • Dizziness
  • A distended feeling in the head
  • T: Pale and swollen with teethmarks
  • C: White
  • P: Thready, moderate and slightly slow
Treatment Principle
  • Strengthen the Spleen
  • Tonify Qi
  • Eliminate Dampness
Herb Formulas
  • Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tangmodified
Points
  • SP-3, SP-9, ST-36
    With abdominal distention: + ST-25
    With undigested food in the stools, anorexia and nausea after meals: + ST-21
    With Phlegm: + ST-40

    With edema in the lower limbs: + REN-3, SP-6
    With eye sores: + GB-1, SJ-23, Taiyang. bleed UB-2
    With external genital sores: + REN-1, REN-2
    With sores in the mouth: + PC-7

SPLEEN AND KIDNEY YANG DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Enduring disease with repeated recurrences
  • Commonly precipitated by Cold and chill in the fall
  • Oral or genital sores which are not particularly painful
  • Sores may be accompanied by cold pain
  • The sores are pale red
  • The sores tend to grow hollow
  • Dry, rough, painful eyes
  • A somber, white facial complexion
  • Dizziness
  • Head distention
  • Fatigue
  • Anorexia
  • Loose stools
  • Abdominal pain better with warmth and pressure
  • Lower and upper back aching and pain
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Cold limbs
  • Easy lower limb and facial edema
  • T: Pale, watery and glossy
  • C: White
  • P: Deep and thready or Deep, slow and forceless
Treatment Principle
  • Warm and supplement the Spleen and Kidneys
Herb Formulas

  • Topical: Sheng Ji Yu Hong Gao

  • Oral and genital sores which do not heal

  • Decreased visual acuity
  • Pale-red or purple-red nodular skin lesions

  • Maybe hemafecia

  • Scanty Qi
  • Disinclination to speak
  • Heart palpitations
  • Fearful throbbing

DAMP-HEAT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Relatively advanced sores of all sizes in the genital region
  • The area is covered in yellowish-white, thick, turbid matter or which exudes pus
  • The lesions and surrounded by painful redness and swelling
  • The sores make movement difficult
  • Lower abdominal fullness and cramping
  • Maybe a red colored macular rash on the external genitalia or lower limbs
  • This rash may be nodular or have a puss-filled head with severe aching pain
  • Urination is short and red or hot
  • Astringent, painful, strangurious urination
  • T: Red
  • C: Yellow or Yellow at the root or Yellow and slimy at the root
  • P: Slippery and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Resolve Dampness
  • Relieve toxicity
Herb Formulas
  • Huang Lian Jie Du Tang + Wu Wei Xiao Du Yin
Points
  • LIV-2, LIV-5, REN-1, REN-2, SP-9, ST-44
    With a fever: + LI-11
    With lower limb edema: + REN-3, SP-6
    With pustules, vesicles or folliculitis: + DU-10, UB-40
    With eye sores: + GB-1, SJ-23, Taiyang, bleed UB-2
    For constipation: + SJ-6
    For a sore throat: + LU-5, bleed LI-1, LU-11

TOXIC HEAT

Clinical Manifestations
  • Intense fever
  • Strong thirst
  • Dry heaves
  • Severe, stabbing headache
  • Extreme irritability
  • Delirious speech (severe)
  • Rash (severe)
  • Epistaxis (severe)
  • Maculae
  • Mania
  • Convulsions
  • Lips chapped, dark and scorched
  • Coma
  • Hematemesis
  • Extreme coldness of the extremities
  • Restlessness
  • Spasms or convulsions
  • Blurred vision
  • T: Dark red
  • C: Dry or prickly
  • P: Deep, thin and rapid or Floating, large and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat
  • Relieve toxicity
  • Cool the Blood
  • Drain Fire
Herb Formulas
  • Qing Wen Bai Du Yin
