CHRONIC FATIGUE SYNDROME (CFIDS, POST-VIRAL FATIGUE SYNDROME)

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Chronic Fatigue Syndrome is a chronic disease characterized by:
    Disabling fatigue
    Exhaustion
    Muscle pain
    Headaches
    Joint pain
    Confusion
    Poor concentration
    Poor memory
    Insomnia
    Light-headedness
    Dizziness
    Blurred vision
    Eye pain
    Light sensitivity
    Gastrointestinal disturbances
    Abdominal pain
    Depression
    Fever
    Chills
    Night sweats
    A recurrent sore throat
    Lymph node tenderness
    Skin rashes
    Neurological complaints
  • It frequently follows a viral infection
  • It can occur as a sequela to:
    Otitis media
    Hepatitis
    Glandular fever
  • Acute Stage:
    Continuous or repeated flu-like symptoms
    Pharyngalgia
    Blocked or runny nose
    Chills
    Swollen glands
    Nausea
    Vomiting
    Diarrhea
    Cough
    Genera; fatigue
    Lack of energy
    Exhaustion
    Weariness
    Lack of expression
    Memory loss
    Lack of concentration
    Reduced capacity for work
    Insomnia
    dizziness
    Nervousness
    General migratory soreness and pain in the:
    Muscles
    Neck
    Head
    Body
    Stomach
    Costal and hypochondriac regions
    Abdomen
  • Chronic Stage:
    The acute symptoms may recur occasionally
    General exhaustion
    Fatigue
    Insomnia
    Drowsiness
    Forgetfulness
    Reduced or lost capacity for work
    Easily infected by pathogenic factors
    Aching or pain in the muscles (worse with Dampness)
