COLITIS, ULCERATIVE COLITIS

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Passage of watery stool and pus
  • Abdominal pain or tenderness
  • Colic
  • Intermittent or irregular fever
  • May be hemorrhage
  • May be perforation
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas

  • Nonspecific acute: Huo Xiang Zheng Qi Tang
  • Chronic:
    Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang
    Colitis #1
    Colitis #2

  • LI-4, LI-10, ST-25, ST-36, ST-37, ST-44, UB-22, UB-24, UB-25, UB-27

  • Spastic: REN-6, ST-25, ST-27, ST-28, ST-37, UB-24, UB-25, UB-31

YANG MING FU

Clinical Manifestations
  • Abdominal pain (worse with pressure)
  • The skin over the pain may feel warmer to the touch
  • High fever (sometimes rises and falls)
  • Great thirst
  • A dry mouth
  • Profuse perspiration
  • Aversion to Heat
  • Abdominal fullness and distension (worse with pressure)
  • Dryness
  • Constipation (hard dry knots)
  • Dislikes pressure
  • Restlessness
  • Irritability
  • Concentrated urine
  • Mental disturbances:
    Mentally unclear
    Delirious speech
  • T: Red and dry, or black and moist

  • P: Deep, strong and forceful

  • Purge

INTESTINAL WIND (WIND-HEAT IN THE STOMACH AND INTESTINES)

Clinical Manifestations
  • Bleeding of fresh Blood from the bowel which often precedes the bowel movement
  • The bleeding may follow the passage of stool
  • The bleeding may be alarmingly copious
  • Abdominal pain or
  • Absence of pain, itching, swelling or distention or
  • A burning sensation in the rectum and anus
  • Dry mouth
  • Tendency toward constipation
  • Dry or difficult stools
  • T: Red
  • C: Yellow
  • P: Slippery and rapid or Thready and rapid
Treatment Principle
  • Clear Heat from the Intestines
  • Cool the Blood
  • Stop bleeding
Herb Formulas
  • Huai Jiao Wan modified
  • Huai Hua San modified
  • Topical: Mix 3g Alumen Ming Fan in a little water, dissolve 30g petroleum jelly and mix in the Ming Fan. Place on sterile gauze and insert into the anal canal.
  • Chinese Patent Medicines:
    Yi Zi Tang
    Zheng Gan Zhi Gen Duan
    To stop bleeding:
    Yun Nan Bai Yao
    Shen Tian Qi Pian
    For Intestinal bleeding with Heat: Zhong Gan Zhi Gen Dan
    For Damp-Heat: Huang Lian Jie Du Wan
    With Spleen and Stomach Deficiency: Xiang Sha Yang Wei Wan
Points
  • Ahshi points, DU-1, Erbai, SP-1, ST-25, ST-37, UB-30, UB-32, UB-57
    With pain in the rectum or lower Intestine: + UB-2, UB-52
    With severe bleeding: + PC-4, SP-10
  • Ear:
    1. Large Intestine, Small Intestine, Distal Segment Rectum, Subcortex, Shenmen
    2. Hemorrhoids, Large Intestine, Distal Segment Rectum, Shenmen
