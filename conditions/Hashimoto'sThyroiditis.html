HASHIMOTO'S THYROIDITIS

GENERAL

Clinical Manifestations
  • Autoimmune thyroiditis leading to hypothyroidism
  • Slowly developing persistent inflammation of the thyroid
  • Decreased gland function
  • Primarily in women
  • Generally a family history of the disease
  • Intolerance to Cold
  • Weight gain
  • Fatigue
  • Constipation
  • Enlarged neck or
  • Goiter
  • Joint pain
  • Facial edema
  • There may be signs of hyperthyroidism in the early stages
Treatment Principle
Herb Formulas
  • For early-stage hyperthyroidism:
    Qi and Yin Deficiency: Yi Qi yang Yin Zhong Yao
    Yin     Deficiency with Stomach Heat: Yang Yin Qing Re Zhong Yao
    Liver Qi Stagnation: Shu Gan Liu Qi Zhi Zhong Yao
  • To move the Blood: Li Qi Huo Xue Zhang Yao
Points
  • Moxa DU-14, DU-4, UB-23 alternate with
    Moxa REN-17, REN-12, REN-4

LIVER QI STAGNATION WITH PHLEGM

Clinical Manifestations
  • Swollen lumps in the front of the throat which are soft and painless
  • A swollen feeling in the front of the throat or
  • A plum pit sensation in the back of the throat
  • A tendency towards anger
  • Chest oppression
  • Sighing
  • Possible chest or flank pain
  • T: Normal or Dusky
  • C: White and greasy
  • P: Wiry
Treatment Principle
  • Rectify Qi
  • Soothe Stagnation
  • Transform Phlegm
  • Scatter nodulation

  • Si Hai Shu Yu Wan modified

QI DEFICIENCY WITH BLOOD STAGANTION

Clinical Manifestations
  • Pain in the lower abdomen and pelvic region
  • Spotting between periods or before periods
  • Abdominal swelling
  • Low back pain
  • Sometimes no symptoms at all
  • Periods that are either heavy or scanty, with light-colored, watery menses
  • Infertility
  • Sometimes no symptoms at all
  • A preference for warmth
  • Feels better with pressure on the abdomen or
  • Dislikes pressure
  • Large clots
  • Fatigue
  • Bloating
  • Soft bowel movements
  • A pale complexion
  • Lethargy
  • Headaches
  • Low grade fevers
  • Depression
  • Anxiety
  • T: Pale and Swollen with teethmarks and/or Purple
  • C: White
  • P: Deep, thin and weak or Thin, soft and weak
Treatment Principle
  • Tonify Qi
  • Invigorate the Blood
Herb Formulas
  • Liu Jun Zi Tang modified
Points
  • Moxa or moxa needle UB-17,  UB-20, UB-23, REN-12, ST-36, SP-6, SP-10, DU-20

YANG DEFICIENCY

Clinical Manifestations
  • Aversion to Cold
  • Watery stools with undigested food
  • Lower back pain
  • Weakness of the lower extremities
  • A cold sensation in the lower half of the body
  • Tenseness in the lower abdomen
  • Pain and cramps in the lower abdomen
  • Panting and irritability to the point of having difficulty lying down
  • Will breathe most comfortably while leaning against something
  • Nocturia
  • May be numbness in the lower abdomen
  • Urinary difficulty with edema or Spasmodic pain in the lower abdomen
  • Excessive urination
  • If severe, may be incontinence of urine
  • May have persistent diarrhea
  • Dysuria
  • May have flushed face (Yang floating upward)
  • Thirst with frequent, copious urination (Xiao Ke)
  • Fatigue (severe)
  • May have severe perspiration (Yang floating upward)
  • Constipation
  • Lassitude
  • Pale face
  • Edema of the face and feet
  • Severe edema of the legs (may prevent walking)
  • Maybe cough
  • Maybe asthma
  • T: Pale, may be swollen
  • C: Thin, white and moist
  • P: Deep, Deep and thready and/or Weak and slow
Treatment Principle
  • Warm and Tonify Kidney Yang
Herb Formulas
  • Jin Gui Shen Qi Wan
