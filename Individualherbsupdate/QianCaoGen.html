NAME: QIAN CAO GEN - 茜草根 - RADIX RUBIAE

Pharmaceutical Latin: Radix Rubiae
Common English: Madder Root
Rubia Root
Heart-Leaf Madder Root
India Madder Root

CATEGORY

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Cools the Blood and stops bleeding

Bleeding due to Blood Heat with epistaxis, hematemesis, hemoptysis, hemafecia, hematuria and uterine bleeding

Invigorates the Blood and dispels Blood Stasis

Pain due to Blood Stasis, especially chest and flank pain

Retained lochia

Pain from traumatic injury and joint pain

Early-stage carbuncles

Disperses Wind-Dampness

Bi pain

Unblocks the channels and vessels

Wind pain in the bones and joints

Cools the Liver and transforms Phlegm

Chronic, stubborn cough with pain in the chest due to Liver Fire Attacking the Lungs

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with no Blood Stasis symptoms.
  • Contraindicated for those with Jing Deficiency.
  • Use caution for those with Spleen and Stomach Deficiency Cold.
  • Use caution for those with Blood Deficiency.
  • Use caution for those with Yin Deficiency Fire.
  • Contraindicated during pregnancy.
  • Do not use long term.

NOTES

  1. Some sources include this herb in the “Blood Invigorating“ category.
  2. This herb is especially useful for hemoptysis and hematemesis.
  3. Some sources say that this herb resolves Phlegm and stops cough.
  4. This herb is more effective at stopping bleeding if dry-fried or charred.
  5. It can be used alone for hematemesis due to Heat.
  6. This herb has shown to be effective in inhibiting the growth of cancer cells.
  7. This herb invigorates the Blood when combined with wine and stops bleeding when roasted.
  8. It is said to cure hemostasis and arthralgia due to Blood Deficiency without any side effects.
  9. It benefits women when combined with Rx. Angelicae Sinensis Dang Gui and Rx. Paeoniae Alba Bai Shao.
  10. One source says that this herb stops epistaxis, leukorrhagia, puerperal faintness, agalactia, irregular menstruation, hematochezia, hemorrhoids and fistula.
  11. Another source says that this herb relieves pain caused by Wind, promotes menstruation, induces abortion and cures jaundice and abdominal masses.
  12. This herb has a preventative and remedial action on stone formation.
  13. Qian Cao Gen , Hb. Sive Rx. Cirsii Japonici Da Ji and Hb. Cirsii Xiao Ji all enter the Blood to cool Heat, stop bleeding and dispel Blood Stasis. Qian Cao Gen is used more in gynecology to treat vaginal bleeding due to Heat and also enters the Lungs to treat cough due to Liver Fire Attacking the Lungs. Xiao Ji facilitates urination and treats Bloody Lin. Da Ji disperses clumps and reduces sores, especially when fresh.
  14. Both Qian Cao Gen and Fol. Callicarpae Formosanae Zi Zhu cool the Blood and stop bleeding. Qian Cao Gen also invigorates the Blood, unblocks the collaterals and is better for bleeding associated with Blood Stasis and Heat in the Blood. Zi Zhu is astringent, resolves toxicity and treats sores.
  15. Charred Rubia Root Qian Cao Tan is not so cold, does not cool the Blood nor remove Stasis as well, but is better at stopping bleeding. It is used for all types of bleeding.
