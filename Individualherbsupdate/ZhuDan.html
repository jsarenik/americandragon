NAME: ZHU DAN - 猪胆 - FEL SUIS SCROFAE DOMESTICAE

Pharmaceutical Latin: Fel Suis Scrofae Domesticae 
Common English: Pig Gallbladder

CATEGORY

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Clears the Lungs, transforms Phlegm and relieves cough and dyspnea

Hypersensitivity, cholecystitis, hepatitis, difficulty digesting fats, phlegm accumulation, asthma, jaundice, pharyngitis, pertussis, typhoid fever

Clears Heat, clears vision and expels toxins

Internal Heat with rashes and itching, allergies, diarrhea, dysentery, sores and boils, red eyes, carbuncles, furuncles, conjunctivitis

Fevers

NOTES

  1. One source says that this herb is a purgative, diuretic and treats eruptions on children's heads.
  2. Pig Bones Zhu Gu 猪骨, treats poisoning from horse liver, putrid meat and bad fruits and vegetables and smallpox (charred jawbone). Bone (spine) soup relieves thirst. The charred head bones are used for diarrhea. The heated and pulverized jaw bone is used topically for venereal sores.
  3. Pork Zhu Rou 猪肉, is bitter, slightly cooling and slightly toxic. In medicine, only pure black pigs are used. It is used for Kidney problems, chronic insanity, dysentery and chronic inflammation. It can cause dysentery if used with plums, Rz. Coptidis Huang Lian, Rx. Platycodi Jie Geng and Rz. Picrorhizae Hu Huang Lian. If used with Fr. Xanthii Cang Er Zi, it can cause convulsions. it can cause pimples on the face. if used with Rx. Zingiberis Recens Sheng Jiang. If used with Rz. Fagopyri Cymosi (buckwheat) Jin Qiao Mai, it can cause hair loss and rheumatism.
  4. Pig Bile Zhu Dan Qi clears the Lungs, transforms Phlegm, clears Heat and neutralizes poisons.
