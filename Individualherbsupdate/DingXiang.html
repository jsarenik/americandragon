INDIVIDUAL HERBS

NAME: DING XIANG - 丁香 - FLOS CARYOPHYLLI - CLOVE

Pharmaceutical Latin: Flos Caryophylli
Gemma Eugeniae
Flos Syzygii Aromatici
Common English: Clove
Eugenia

CATEGORY

Herbs that Warm the Interior and Expel Cold

PROPERTIES

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Warms the Middle Jiao, opens the Stomach, relieves pain and regulates and descends Qi

Stomach Cold with vomiting, hiccup, abdominal pain and diarrhea

Spleen and Stomach Deficiency Cold with anorexia, vomiting and diarrhea

Warms the Kidneys and assists Yang

Impotence or clear vaginal discharge from Cold in the Womb (usually accompanied by weak legs) due to Kidney Yang Deficiency

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with Febrile disease.
  • Contraindicated for those with Internal Heat
  • Contraindicated for those with Yin Deficiency.
  • Use caution during pregnancy.
  • Use only for Deficiency Cold.
INCOMPATIBILITIES
  • Do not combine with Tub. Curcumae Yu Jin.

NOTES

  1. This herb is similar in action to Fr. Evodiae Wu Zhu Yu, but is more important for the treatment of reflux Qi.
  2. Some sources say that this herb unblocks the pulse.
  3. Male Clove Gong Mu Xiang is more powerful.
  4. Mother Clove Mu Dong Xiang is less powerful in descending Qi, but is effects last longer and are often combined with Gong Ding Xiang for a more powerful; longer lasting effect. Mu Ding Xiang also enters the Blood to invigorate Blood and induce labor. it has also been used topically to treat Cold vagina.
  5. This herb has long been an ingredient in perfumes, incenses and herbal formulas.
  6. Some sources say that this herb treats parasites.
  7. Cx. Caryophylli Ding Xiang Pi is the bark of the same plant and is used to dispel Cold and relieve pain.
