INDIVIDUAL HERBS

NAME: SHU DI HUANG - 熟地黄 - RADIX REHMANNIAE PREPARATA

Pharmaceutical Latin: Radix Rehmanniae Preparata
Common English: Cooked (Baked) Rehmannia Root
Prepared Chinese Foxglove Root

CATEGORY

Herbs that Tonify the Blood

PROPERTIES

Taste Temperature Entering Meridians Dosage
Sweet
Slightly Warm
Heart
Kidney
Liver
9-30g
Tincture: 2-4ml

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Nourishes the Blood

Blood Deficiency with facial pallor, dizziness, palpitations, insomnia, irregular menstruation, uterine bleeding and postpartum bleeding

Nourishes Liver and Kidney Yin

Kidney and Liver Yin Deficiency with a weak low back and limbs, dizziness, tinnitus, tidal fevers, night sweats and nocturnal emissions

Strongly enriches Yin and relieves wasting and thirsting disorder

Lower Jiao wasting (it can be used as a stand-alone herb for this)

Nourishes Jing and Blood and fills the Marrow

Exhausted Jing and Blood problems such as delayed development during childhood, blunted affect, premature aging, diminished mental acuity, graying of hair, impotence and memory loss

Arrests coughing and wheezing Kidney Deficient coughing, wheezing and asthma, such as inability of the Kidneys to grasp Qi

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with Qi or Phlegm Stagnation.
  • Contraindicated for those with fullness and pain in the abdomen, nausea and diarrhea.
  • Contraindicated for those with postpartum loss of appetite or nausea.
  • Use caution for those with Spleen and Stomach Deficiency.
  • Use caution for those with Qi Stagnation.
  • Overuse can lead to abdominal distention and loose stools.
  • Do not allow it to contact bronze or iron.

NOTES

  1. It adds to marrow - improves marrow and brain.
  2. It is good for muscles.
  3. It is good for Blood and internal organs.
  4. It helps Jin Ye.
  5. It helps Blood circulation.
  6. It is good for eyesight and hearing.
  7. It helps hair remain black.
  8. It is good for most ailments in the man's body.
  9. It is good for menstrual disorders in the woman’s body.
  10. This is a very cloying and rich substance which easily leads to Dampness Stagnation and impedes digestion.
  11. It warms the Gallbladder and augments Heart Blood.
  12. This herb should be used whenever there is internal injury or insufficiency, excessive mental or emotional activity that exhausts the Spirit, continual worry that injures the Blood and unleashed desire that consumes the Jing; or in order to adjust menstruation, pregnancy and postpartum period.
  13. This herb treats metabolic toxicosis with fatigue, malaise, headaches, muscle aches and chronic skin rashes.
  14. This herb treats blood sugar imbalances.
  15. Both Shu Di Huang and Rx. Polygoni Multiflori He Shou Wu nourish Blood, enrich Yin and tonify the Kidneys and Liver. He Shou Wu primarily enters the Liver to adjust and tonify the Blood while secondarily enriching Kidney Yin and astringing Jing. Shu Di Huang enters the Kidneys to augment Jing and marrow and strongly tonify true Yin, while secondarily nourishing Blood and tonifying the Liver. Sheng Di Huang cools the Blood while Sheng He Shou Wu treats malarial disorders resolves toxicity and moistens the Intestines.
  16. Both Shu Di Huang and Colla Corii Asini E Jiao enter the Liver and Kidney channels, nourish Blood, enrich Kidney Yin and treat Blood Deficiency and gynecological disorders such as irregular menstruation or pregnancy or postpartum disorders. Shu Di Huang generates Jing and augments marrow, while E Jiao enriches Lung Yin and stops bleeding. Both substances are greasy and can interfere with digestion.
  17. Dry-fried Cooked Rehmannia Chao Shu Di Huang has it greasiness reduced and is used for those with weak digestion.
  18. Charred Cooked Rehmanniae Shu Di Huang Tan nourishes Blood with an enhanced ability to stop bleeding.
  19. Ginger-prepared Cooked Rehmannia Jiang Zhi Shu Di Huang no longer has a cloying effect on the diaphragm and is most appropriate for those with Phlegm-Dampness in the area of the diaphragm.
  20. Cooked Rehmannia with Amomum Fruit Sha Ren Ban Shu Di is less greasy and doesn't interfere with digestion.
