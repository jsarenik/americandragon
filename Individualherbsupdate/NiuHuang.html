NAME: NIU HUANG - 牛黃 - CALCULUS BOVIS - CATTLE GALLSTONE - BOS

Pharmaceutical Latin: Calculus Bovis
Common English: Cattle Gallstone

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Clears the Heart, opens the Orifices aromatically, awakens the Spirit and vaporizes Phlegm

Delirium or coma due to Hot Phlegm Obstructing the Pericardium or Warm-Heat pathogen

Wind-Stroke, convulsions or seizures

Clears the Liver, relieves Fire toxicity, extinguishes Wind and stops tremors

Extreme Heat Generates Liver Wind with high fever, spasms, tremors or convulsions

Drains Heat and resolves Fire toxicity

Carbuncles, boils and a wide variety of Hot swellings (Chuang Yung) (can be used as a stand-alone herb)

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated during pregnancy.
  • Contraindicated for those with no Heat Excess.
  • Contraindicated for those with Spleen and Stomach Deficiency.
  • Contraindicated for infants with diarrhea due to poor digestion of milk.
  • Contraindicated for those with Wind-Stroke in the channels with facial paralysis, numbness of the skin or partial paralysis of the limbs.
INCOMPATIBILITIES
  • Antagonizes Fossilia Ossis Mastoid Long Gu.
  • Antagonizes Rx. Rehmanniae Sheng Di Huang.
  • Antagonizes Rx. Gentianae Long Dan Cao.
  • Antagonizes Rx. Dichroa Chang Shan.
  • Counteracts Rx. Achyranthis Bidentatae Niu Xi.
HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS
  • Niu Huang has a mild sedative effect. It reduces the stimulation induced by caffeine and enhances the sedation induced by morphine and barbiturates and other sedatives.

NOTES

  1. Some sources classify this herb as a Liver Wind Calming herb.
  2. It has a digestive stimulating action which is used to treat digestive conditions entailing enzymatic and biliary insufficiency with loss of fat breakdown and food allergies.
  3. This herb has been shown to inhibit the growth of cancer cells.
  4. For epilepsy induced by terror or mental disturbance, crush this herb, mix with honey and feed to the patient.
  5. For collapsed smallpox, grind 2 pieces of Niu Huang with 0.3g Cinnabaris Zhu Sha to a powder, mix with honey and rouge and apply to the lesion once a day.
  6. Taken orally, it treats furuncles, carbuncles, cellulitis and many other types of infections.
  7. Used topically, it drastically detoxifies and stops pain.
  8. Calculus Artificialis Bovis Ren Gong Niu Huang is produced by treating cattle or pig gall by artificial means until a stone is produced. It is believed comparable to the natural substance for treating Heat toxin, but not as effective in opening the Orifices.
  9. This herb is not as effective at opening Orifices as Secretio Moschus She Xiang, but is very effective at clearing Heat and relieving toxicity.
  10. This herb also cools the Blood.
  11. Both Niu Huang and Arisaema cum Bile Dan Nan Xing cool Heat, eliminate Phlegm, settle convulsions and treat loss of consciousness due to Wind-Stroke or Phlegm collapse due to Phlegm-Heat, recurrent childhood convulsions and patterns of withdrawal mania. Niu Huang more powerfully clears the Hear, opens the Orifices, cools the Blood and resolves toxicity to treat high fever, delirium, impaired consciousness and swollen sores due to Heat toxin. Dan Nan Xing more strongly eliminates Phlegm, expels Wind arrests convulsions and better treat Phlegm-Heat.
  12. Both Niu Huang and Venenum Bufonis Chan Su are good for resolving toxicity, reducing swelling, opening the Orifices and awakening consciousness. Niu Huang has a powerful cooling nature that resolves toxicity and clears Heat and an aromatic property that dislodges Phlegm and opens Orifices. It is used for muddled consciousness due to febrile disorders or Wind-Stroke. Chan Su is acrid,warm mobilizing and dispersing and treats sores and toxic swellings by reducing the swelling and stopping pain. It also opens Orifices and clears away turbid filth to treat fainting due to Heat-Stroke and vomiting, diarrhea and abdominal pain due to exposure to turbid during the hottest days of summer.
