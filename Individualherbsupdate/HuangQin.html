ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Clears Heat and dries Dampness

Damp-Heat in the Stomach or Intestines with diarrhea or dysenteric disorder

Damp-Warmth with fever, a stifling sensation in the chest and thirst with no desire to drink

Damp-Heat in the Lower Jiao with painful urinary dysfunction

Damp-Heat jaundice (auxiliary), infectious hepatitis

Drains Fire and detoxifies

Heat and Fire especially in the Upper Jiao (Lung) with high fever, irritability, thirst, cough and expectoration of thick, yellow sputum

Upper respiratory tract infection

Hot sores and swellings (topical or internal)

Cools the Blood and stops bleeding

Xue Stage Heat or Blood Heat causing bleeding with epistaxis, hemoptysis, hematemesis and hemafecia

Clears Heat and calms the fetus

Fetal restlessness due to Heat

Calms ascending Liver Yang

Liver Yang Rising with headache, irritability, red eyes, flushed face and bitter taste

High Blood pressure

Gallbladder Heat

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with Deficiency Cold in the Middle Jiao.
  • Contraindicated for those with Cold diarrhea.
  • Contraindicated for those with Deficiency Heat in the Lungs.
  • Use caution for those with restless fetus due to Cold.
  • Use caution for those with Blood Deficiency with abdominal pain.
  • Use caution for those with edema due to Spleen Deficiency.
  • Use caution for those with loose stools due to Kidney Deficiency.
  • Use caution for those with amenorrhea due to Blood Deficiency.
INCOMPATIBILITIES
  • Counteracts Cx. Moutan Mu Dan Pi.
  • Counteracts Rz. & Rx. Veratri Li Lu.
HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS
  • This herb may have a synergistic effect with beta-lactam antibiotics such as ampecillin, amoxicillin and cefotaxime to restore the effectiveness of these drugs against beta-lactum-resistant Staphylococces aureus and methicillin-resistant Staphylococces aureus .

NOTES

  1. It makes the placenta and uterus healthy.
  2. Sautéed in sake, it brings energy up.
  3. Sautéed in stool water, it brings body energy down.
  4. It has a similar effect as aspirin.
  5. It is said to protect against scarlet fever.
  6. It is important for calming Internal Wind and treating the Gallbladder.
  7. It quiets uterus during pregnancy and therefore prevents miscarriage.
  8. This herb has been shown to inhibit the growth of cancer cells.
  9. It is said to function in nine ways: 1. Clear Lung-Heat; 2. Cool Summerheat; 3. Clear Wind-Heat in the Upper Jiao and skin; 4. Clear all types of Heat; 5. Nourish Yin and subdue Yang after birth;
    6. Invigorate the Qi of the chest; 7. Diminish Phlegm above the diaphragm; 8. Clear Upper Jiao Heat and Spleen Dampness; 9. Calm the fetus.
  10. Both Huang Qin and Gypsum Fibrosum Shi Gao cool Lung Heat and drain Fire from the Qi Level. Huang Qin clears Heat, dries Dampness, cools the Blood to stop bleeding and calms the fetus when disturbed by Heat. Shi Gao cools, releases and vents Heat from the muscle layer. It eliminates Heat so fluids can regenerate. It drains Heat from the Qi Level and clears Dry-Heat from the Lungs and Stomach.
  11. Bland Scutellaria Dan Huang Qin is the normal herb.
  12. Young Scutellaria Roots Zi Huang Qin enters the Large Intestine channel to drain the Large Intestine and Lower Jiao Heat.
  13. Dried Scutellaria Ku Huang Qin enters the Lung channel to clear and drain Lung Fire and release External Wind-Heat.
  14. Dry-fried Scutellaria Chao Huang Qin enters the Blood Level to treat Lower Jiao Heat or disturbed fetal Qi.
  15. Wine-fried Scutellaria Jiu Chao Huang Qin clears and eliminates Damp-Heat from the Upper Jiao.
  16. Charred Scutellaria Huang Qin Tan or Scorched Scutellaria Jiao Huang Qin cools Heat and stops bleeding for  hemorrhage due to Blood Heat.
  17. Huang Qin is a comprehensive agent for the Liver and Gallbladder, including fat metabolism and a remedy for various hypersensitivity or allergic reactions including food allergies.
