INDIVIDUAL HERBS

NAME: YU MI XU - 玉米鬚 - STIGMA MAYDIS - CORNSILK

Pharmaceutical Latin: Stigma Maydis
Common English: Cornsilk

CATEGORY:

Herbs that Drain Dampness (Diuretics)

PROPERTIES:

Taste Temperature Entering Meridians Dosage
Sweet
Bland
Neutral
Bladder
Gallbladder
Liver
(Kidney)
9-60g

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS:

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Promotes urination, reduces edema and unblocks painful urinary dysfunction

Lin Syndrome (Damp-Heat induced painful urinary dysfunction, especially Stone Lin) and edema

As a stand-alone herb in large doses for edema due to nephritis

Clears Damp-Heat from the Liver/ Gallbladder

Liver/Gallbladder Damp-Heat (with hepatitis, cholecystitis, cholelithiasis)

Removes jaundice

Jaundice (Yin or Yang)

Treats Xiao Ke

Wasting and thirsting (Xiao Ke)

CONTRAINDICATIONS AND INCOMPATIBILITIES:

CONTRAINDICATIONS
INCOMPATIBILITIES
  • This herb should be monitored carefully when used in conjunction with warfarin (Coumadin) as it contains vitamin K which may reduce the anticoagulant effect of warfarin (Coumadin).
  • Concurrent use with diuretics such as chlorothiazide, hydrochlorothiazide, furosimide (Lasix), bumetanide (Bumex) and torsemide (Demadex) may lead to increased elimination of water and electrolytes.

NOTES

  • This herb can be used alone for edema and nephritis.
  • 30 g of this herb in decoction can treat diabetes mellitus.
  • It also acts locally on prostate disorders which give rise to an inability to pass urine properly.
