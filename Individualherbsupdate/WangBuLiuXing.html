Actions Indications/Syndromes

Promotes the movement of Blood, promotes lactation and invigorates the channels

Reduces swelling and drains abscesses

For painful swellings, especially of the breast or testicles

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated during pregnancy.
  • Contraindicated for those with Blood loss disorders and continuous menstrual bleeding.
  • Use caution and in low dosage for those with Deficiency.

NOTES

  1. This herb is said to very fast acting and cannot be stopped by any means.
  2. It disperses swelling of carbuncles.
  3. This herb has been used to treat herpes zoster topically.
  4. This herb is known to be effective in treating prostatitis or benign prostatic hypertrophy.
  5. This herb treats difficult labor.
  6. Both Wang Bu Liu Xing and Squama Manitis Chuan Shan Jia promote movement without restraint, excel at unblocking the channels and collaterals to promote movement of Blood and enhance the flow of breast milk and reduce swollen sores. Wang Bu Liu Xing is bitter and neutral with a strong downward action allowing it to induce labor and direct breast milk downward Chuan Shan Jia is salty and cold with a stronger piercing property which allows it to disseminate and unblock organs in the interior, while tracking down Wind and invigorating the collaterals in the exterior. It also expels pus.
  7. Dry-fried Vaccaria Seeds Chao Liu Xing Zi is warm and is stronger in its ability to invigorate the Blood and promote menstruation and is used to treat amenorrhea due to Blood Stasis.
