*CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated during pregnancy.
  • Combine with tonics to avoid harm.
  • Reported side effects are: dry mouth, bitter taste in the mouth, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea.
INCOMPATIBILITIES
HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS
  • Use caution for those taking insulin, sulfonylureas and other antidiabetics such as tolbutamide (Orinase), glipizide (Glucotrol) and glyburide (DiaBeta/Micronase) as there may be a synergistic effect leading to hypoglycemia.

NOTES

  1. Some sources consider this to be a “Damp-Heat Clearing” herb.
  2. This herb invigorates the Blood, breaks up Stasis, expels Wind, eliminates Dampness, cools Heat, resolves toxicity, transforms Phlegm and stops cough.
  3. It attacks all toxic Swellings, stops sore throat, facilitates urination and travels through all the channels and collaterals.
  4. Used in conjunction with tonic herbs, it is an important herb for painful urinary dysfunction in older people with Qi and Blood Deficiency.
  5. The fresh herb, ground and applied topically can be used for burns, sores, hemorrhoids, and other skin infections.
  6. Wine-fried Bushy Knotweed Rhizome Jiu Chao Hu Zhang more strongly invigorates Blood and soothe sinews and is less likely to irritate digestion.
  7. One source says that this herb tonifies the muscles and bones and increases strength.
  8. This herb has been shown to inhibit the growth of cancer cells.
  9. One source says that this herb is usually indicated for abdominal masses and regulating menstruation.
  10. Another source says that this herb is effective for joint diseases and Blood Stagnation.
  11. For abdominal masses, tinnitus, heaviness of the limbs, irregular menstruation and impotence, mix this herb with 600g of Sm. Dolichorus Bai Bian Dou and 200ml of old wine and decoct with 2000ml of water. Take 5ml each time.
  12. One source says that washing ones hands and feet with this herb cure swelling and pain of the hands and feet.
