INDIVIDUAL HERBS

NAME: DI FU ZI - 地膚子 - FRUCTUS KOCHIAE

Pharmaceutical Latin: Fructus Kochiae
Common English: Kochia Fruit
Broom Cypress
Belvedere Fruit

CATEGORY

Herbs That Drain Dampness (Diuretics)

PROPERTIES

Taste Temperature Entering Meridians Dosage
Sweet
Bitter
Cold
Urinary Bladder
Kidney
3-15g
Tincture: 2-4ml

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Clears Damp-Heat and promotes urination

Damp-Heat Painful Urinary Dysfunction due to Damp-Heat with dark, burning and scanty urination

Expels Dampness and stops itching

Damp skin disorders where itching is a main symptom (eczema, scabies) (internal and topical)

Damp-Heat in the external genitalia

Clears Wind-Heat affecting the eyes and head

Swollen, painful eyes and head and skin disorders due to Wind-Heat

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Do not use when there are no Damp-Heat symptoms.
INCOMPATIBILITIES
  • Do not use with Os Sepiae Sepiellae Hai Piao Xiao.

NOTES

  1. Both Di Fu Zi and Rx. Sophorae Flavescentis Ku Shen clear Damp-Heat, expel Wind, alleviates itching and facilitate urination. Di Fu Zi is superior at expelling Wind, resolving Dampness and alleviating itching while Ku Shen is better at clearing Damp-Heat. It is used externally to clear Heat toxin which causes itchy sores and internally it treats dysentery and jaundice due to Damp-Heat.
  2. This herb can be used alone for urinary tract infections during pregnancy.
