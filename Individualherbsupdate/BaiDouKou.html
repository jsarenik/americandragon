NAME: BAI DOU KOU - 白豆蔻 - FRUCTUS AMOMI ROTUNDUS

Pharmaceutical Latin: Fructus Amomi Rotundus
Common English: Fructus Amomi Kravanh

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Promotes the movement of Qi, aromatically transforms Dampness and strengthens the Stomach

Dampness Obstructing the Middle Jiao and Damp-Warmth (a stifling sensation in the chest, fullness in the epigastrium, anorexia and a very greasy tongue coat)

Warms the Middle Jiao, descends Qi and stops vomiting

Vomiting due to Spleen and Stomach, Deficiency Cold, Spleen and Stomach Cold, Cold Dampness and Food Stagnation

Rebellious Stomach Qi with vomiting, belching

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with Yin or Blood Deficiency.
  • Contraindicated for those with nausea and vomiting not due to Cold or Yang Deficiency.
  • Use caution when there is no Cold-Damp.

NOTES

  1. Some sources say that this herb transforms Stagnation, digests food and relieves alcohol intoxication.
  2. It also treats eye disorders such as superficial visual obstruction and redness of the canthus.
  3. It is particularly effective for dredging and dispersing Lung Qi blockage.
  4. This herb also treats nervous Deficiency with mental and physical fatigue.
  5. It is also said that this herb treats diarrhea with undigested food, especially raw vegetables.
  6. This herb is good for colic and vomiting milk.
  7. It is often used with Fr. Amomi Sha Ren for pain and distention of the abdomen with vomiting and diarrhea. Sha Ren is better for problems of the Middle and Lower Jiaos such as diarrhea, while Bai Dou Kou is more appropriate for problems of the Upper and Middle Jiaos such as vomiting.
  8. Bai Dou Kou, Fr. Tsaoko Cao Guo and Sm. Myristicae Rou Dou Kou all warm the Stomach and awaken the Spleen. Bai Dou Kou has a superior clear aroma and intense acridity. It has no astringent taste in the mouth and excels at raising upward, opening and draining Upper Jiao Qi Stagnation. Cao Guo and Rou Dou Kou are restricted to the Middle Jiao.
  9. Both Bai Dou Kou and Fr. Amomi Cao Dou Kou mobilize the Qi, ease the Stomach, warm the Middle and transform Dampness. Cao Dou Kou does not mobilize the Qi as well nor does it disseminate Lung Qi as effectively but is warmer and better at drying turbid Dampness. It may substitute for Bai Dou Kou.
  10. Round Cardamon Kernels Bai Dou Kou Ren are more potent at promoting the flow of Qi and transforming Dampness, warming the Stomach and alleviating nausea. They are used to treat nausea, vomiting, diarrhea and abdominal pain due to Stomach Cold and Qi Stasis.
  11. Round Cardamon Husks (Pericarpium Amomi Rotundum) Bai Dou Kou Ke are not as warm as the Kernels but they do regulate the Qi ease the Middle and alleviate nausea. They are used for Qi Stasis, abdominal distension, anorexia and nausea.
  12. Round Cardamon Flowers (Flos Amomi Rotundum) Bai Dou Kou Hua are neutral , acrid, unbind the Stomach, relieve the chest and eliminate distention.
