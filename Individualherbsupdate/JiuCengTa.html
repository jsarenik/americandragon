INDIVIDUAL HERBS

NAME: JIU CHENG TA  九層塔

Pharmaceutical Latin: Herba Ocimi Basilici
Common English: Basil

CATEGORY

Herbs that Regulate Qi

PROPERTIES

Taste Temperature Entering Meridians Dosage
Acrid
Warm
Stomach
Liver
Kidney
5-12g

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Regulates the Middle Jiao and promotes digestion

Stomach Cold with Qi Stagnation with stomach cramps which are better with warmth and worse with pressure and poor appetite in children

Invigorates Blood and dispels water

Obstetric and gynecological disorders from Qi and Blood Stagnation with irregular menstruation

Postpartum Blood Stagnation

Opens the channels and collaterals and relieves pain

Soreness and pain in the tendons and bones

Eliminates toxins and disperses Wind

Toothache and eye swelling and pain (when combined with Heat-clearing and toxin eliminating herbs)

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated during pregnancy.
