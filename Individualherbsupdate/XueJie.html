INDIVIDUAL HERBS

NAME: XUE JIE - 血竭 - SANGUIS DRACONIS - DRAGON'S BLOOD

Pharmaceutical Latin: Sanguis Draconis
Resina Daemonoropis
Common English: Dragon's Blood
Resinous Discharge
Calamus Gum

CATEGORY

Herbs that Regulate Blood: Herbs that Invigorate the Blood

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Invigorates the Blood, dispels Blood Stasis and alleviates pain

Pain from Blood Stasis, especially due to traumatic injury such as injury from falls, fractures, contusions and sprains

Stops bleeding

Applied topically for bleeding due to external injury

Protects the surface of ulcers, prevents decay and generates flesh

Chronic non-healing ulcers and cancers

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated internally during pregnancy or menstruation.
  • Contraindicated for those with bleeding due to Deficiency Heat.
  • Contraindicated for those with Blood Stasis signs or symptoms.

NOTES

  1. This herb has recently used for bleeding from the upper digestive tract.
  2. This can be use as a stand-alone herb.
  3. Xue Jie, Olibanum Ru Xiang and Myrrh Mo Yao all promote movement of Blood, stop pain, generate flesh and assist in the healing of sores. Xue Jie enters the Blood Level and is most able to generate flesh, help heal sores and stop bleeding. Ru Xiang and Mo Yao both enter the Qi and Blood levels and are better at transforming Stasis, reducing swelling, and stopping pain and are most appropriate for Qi and Blood Stagnation in the chest and abdomen, pain in the joints due to Wind-Damp Bi and painful, swollen sores.
