INDIVIDUAL HERBS

NAME: BIAN XU - 扁蓄 - HERBA POLYGONI AVICULARIS

Pharmaceutical Latin: Herba Polygoni Avicularis
Common English: Knotgrass
Knotweed
Polygonum

CATEGORY

Herbs that Drain Dampness (Diuretics)

PROPERTIES

Taste Temperature Entering Meridians Dosage
Bitter
Slightly Cold
(Cool)
(Neutral)
Bladder
8-16g
Tincture: 2-4ml

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Drains Damp-Heat from the Urinary Bladder, promotes urination and unblocks painful urinary dysfunction

Damp-Heat Painful Urinary Dysfunction (Lin Syndrome)

Damp-Heat jaundice

Expels parasites and stops itching

Damp lesions with itch including tinea

Intestinal parasites such as tapeworm, hookworm and pinworm

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with difficult urination due to abdominal weakness.
  • Overdose can cause Qi Deficiency.
  • It may cause dermatitis or gastrointestinal disturbance.
  • Over consumption can cause depletion of Jing.
INCOMPATIBILITIES
HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS
  • Concurrent use with diuretics such as chlorothiazide, hydrochlorothiazide, furmoside (Lasix), bumetinide (Bumex) and torsemide (Demadex) may lead to increased elimination of water and/or electrolytes.

NOTES

  1. This herb is most appropriate when Heat and Dampness are equally severe.
  2. This herb is also used for Damp-Heat jaundice and dysenteric disorders, due to Damp-Heat and Damp-Heat sores and itchy rashes.
  3. It treats weeping rashes, hemorrhoids, the three parasites, sudden turmoil disorder and vaginal erosion.
  4. it can be used topically as a wash for itching due to parasites.
  5. Both Bian Xu and Hb. Dianthi Qu Mai clear Heat and facilitate the removal of pathogenic water to unblock painful urinary dysfunction. They are often used together to treat burning, painful and difficult urination Bian Xu is bitter, directs downward and directly enters the Bladder to cool Damp-Heat and is superior for scanty, dark and difficult -to-pass urine due to Damp-Heat. It can also be used for Damp-Heat jaundice. Qu Mai facilitates the function of the Small Intestine to guide Internal Heat outward and is better for burning pain during urination or hematuria due to Heat. It also breaks up blood Stasis and unblocks the channels to treat amenorrhea.
  6. In Western herbology, Bian Xu is also used to treat gout and rheumatism.
