CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with diarrhea from Deficiency Cold (Yang Deficiency).
  • Contraindicated for those with eczema or rashes from External Wind Attack with incomplete expression.
  • Contraindicated postpartum for those with Blood Stagnation or those who are still bleeding.
  • Do not use in large doses or over a prolonged period for those with Liver disorders.
  • This herb may be associated with drowsiness, so use caution when driving or operating heavy machinery.
INCOMPATIBILITIES
  • Antagonizes Hb. Dendrobii Shi Hu.
  • Antagonizes Hb. Cephalanoplos Xiao Ji.
  • Incompatible with Rz. et Rx. Veratri Li Lu.
HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS
  • Use caution for those taking anticoagulants such as heparin, warfarin (Coumadin), and enoxaprin (Lovenox) or antiplatelet drugs such as aspirin, dipyridamole (Persantine), and clopidogrel (Plavix).
  • Use caution for those taking insulin, sulfonylureas and other anti-diabetics such as tolbutamide (Orinase), glipizide (Glucotrol) and glyburide (DiaBeta/Micronase) as there may be a synergistic effect leading to hypoglycemia.
  • Use caution with sedatives as this herb has a sedative and analgesic effect on the central nervous system. It prolongs sleeping time induced by barbiturates and has a protective effect against seizures induced by cardiazol.

NOTES

  1. This is a commonly used herb for treating women’s disorders.
  2. This herb is used to soften and comfort the Liver.
  3. It is important in stopping painful spasms in the abdomen and extremities and abdominal pain due to dysenteric disorders.
  4. Some sources classify this herb as an Astringent.
  5. Used in formulas to prevent miscarriage due to weakness of the Blood or Liver Stagnation.
  6. It is used to treat pain in Blood Deficiency disorders.
  7. It can also be considered an herb that Extinguishes Wind.
  8. It is an herb that both tonifies and drains.
  9. It enters the Spleen channel and supports the Spleen when attacked by Liver Qi.
  10. This herb pacifies the Liver, nourishes Blood and astringes Yin, while Rx. Paeoniae Rubra Chi Shao, cools the Blood, breaks up Stagnation and clears Heat from the Liver. They are often used in combination.
  11. Bai Shao and Rx. Angelica Sinensis Dang Gui both nourish Blood and alleviate pain. Dang Gui unblocks, disperses, drains and lubricates the Intestines while Bai Shao astringes Yin to extinguish Internal Wind and stop sweating and nourishes Liver blood to soften the Liver to relieve painful cramping, and other pain in the flanks and abdomen due to Liver disorders.
  12. Dry-fried White Peony Chao Bai Shao is less cold, but its ability to nourish Blood, harmonize the collaterals,  moderate the Spleen, relax the Liver and alleviate pain is enhanced. It is for Spleen Deficiency or Spleen and Liver  Disharmony with diarrhea and abdominal pain.
  13. Earth-fried White Peony Tu Chao Bai Shao is used for nausea or diarrhea with abdominal pain due to Spleen and Stomach Deficiency.
  14. Wine-fried White Peony Jiu Chao Bai Shao has a mild ability to invigorate the Blood and is used when Blood Deficiency is coupled with Blood Stasis or Cold.
  15. Vinegar-fried White Peony Cu Chao Bai Shao is more able to soften the Liver, alleviate pain and stop bleeding and is used to nourish Blood and tonify the Liver. It is used in combination to treat irregular menstruation or metrorrhagia due to disharmony between the Chong and Ren channels.
  16. Charred White Peony Bai Shao Tan is best for retaining Blood and stopping bleeding. It also helps reduce diarrhea and dysenteric disorders.
