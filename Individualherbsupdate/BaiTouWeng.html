INDIVIDUAL HERBS

NAME: BAI TOU WENG  白头翁 - RADIX PULSATILLAE

Pharmaceutical Latin: Radix Pulsatillae
Common English: Pulsatilla Root
Chinese Anemone Root
Anemone
Asian Pasqueflower Root

CATEGORY

Herbs that Clear Heat and Purge Fire: Herbs that Clear Heat and Dry Dampness

PROPERTIES

Taste Temperature Entering Meridians Dosage
Bitter
Cold
Large Intestine
Stomach
(Liver)
6-18g
Tincture: 2-5ml

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Clears Heat, alleviates dysentery, cools the Blood and resolves Fire toxicity

Dysentery, Damp-Heat in the Stomach or Intestines, hematochezia

Toxic Fire dysentery

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Only use the dried herb not the fresh.
  • Contraindicated for those with chronic dysentery or diarrhea due to Spleen and Stomach Deficiency Cold.
  • Contraindicated for women with vaginal discharge due to Stomach Deficiency.
  • Contraindicated for those with dysenteric disorders that exhibit pale Blood.

NOTES

  1. This herb is used when a Heat syndrome is damaging fluids or causing bleeding.
  2. An extract is used in douches for trichomonas vaginitis.
  3. It is in the same family as goldenseal.
  4. It treats Hot dysenteric disorders with a bearing-down sensation or tenesmus, especially where bleeding is involved.
  5. It can be used as an enema for dysenteric disorders.
  6. This herb can be used alone internally or topically for hemorrhoids.
  7. This herb can be used alone Internally or topically for scrofula.
  8. Other sources say that this herb treats warm malarial disorders with frequent thrashing, delirium, chills, fever, abdominal masses, accumulation and goiter; drives out Blood, stops pain, treats metal sores, nosebleeds, toxic red dysenteric disorders, abdominal pain, toothache, pain in all of the joints, scrofula on the lower neck, all types of Wind, warms the lower back, brightens the eyes and dissolves warts.
  9. Bai Tou Weng, Fr. Bruceae Ya Dan Zi and Hb. Portulacae Ma Chi Xian all clear Heat, resolve toxicity and treat dysenteric disorders. They are used together to treat bleeding dysenteric disorders due to Heat toxin. Ma Chi Xian primarily treats red dysenteric disorders with mucus and Blood, partly because it cools the Blood and stops bleeding. Ya Dan Zi primarily treats dysenteric disorders that alternate between mild and severe, or seem to be cured and then recur. Bai Tou Weng treats both types.
