INDIVIDUAL HERBS

NAME: BAI HE - 百合 - BULBUS LILII - LILY BULB

Pharmaceutical Latin: Bulbus Lilii
Common English: Lily Bulb
Brown's Lily Bulb
Lilium

CATEGORY

Herbs that Tonify the Yin

PROPERTIES

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Nourishes Yin, moistens the Lungs, clears Heat and stops coughing

Dry coughs or sore throat, hemoptysis, due to Lung Yin Deficiency, dry Lungs or Lung Heat

Especially useful for Lung Yin Deficiency

Clears Heat from the Heart and calms the Spirit

Intractable low-grade fever, insomnia with much dreaming, restlessness and irritability in the aftermath of febrile disease

Heart Fire

Palpitations due to insufficiency of Qi and Yin

Nourishes Stomach Yin and harmonizes the Middle Jiao

 Pain associated with Stomach Yin Deficiency

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Use caution for those with cough due to Wind-Cold or Phlegm.
  • Use caution for those with early-stage coughs.
  • Contraindicated for those with diarrhea due to Spleen or Stomach Deficiency.
INCOMPATIBILITIES
HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS
  • This herb prolongs sleeping time produced by barbiturates, antihistamines, narcotic analgesics, benzodiazepines and others.

NOTES

  1. Some sources say that this herb has a diuretic action.
  2. This herb is common in formulas for dry, hacking cough.
  3. It is used for febrile stress in the chest produced by dry, hacking cough, palpitations and restlessness.
  4. One source says that it nourishes Heart Yin.
  5. It is Sweet and Cold, but moistens without being slimy.
  6. It can be applied externally to treat swellings and ulcers ( true of many Yin tonics of the Lily family).
  7. Crush the fresh herb and apply topically as a paste for non-ulcerating sores.
  8. This herb is also said to Tonify Qi and Blood.
  9. This herb can be used alone for Lung abscess.
  10. This herb is also said to inhibit the growth of cancer cells.
  11. It is often used as a food and cooked with porridge to nourish Lung Yin.
  12. It treats mental disorientation, palpitations, insomnia and a feeling of constraint due to Yin Deficiency or Disharmony of the Ying and Wei following an externally contracted disease.
  13. It is said that this herb can supplement the Spleen to keep the Lungs pure, stop coughing and promote diuresis.
  14. Both Bai He and Rx. Adenophorae/Glehniae Sha Shen nourish Yin, clear the Lungs, moisten Dryness, stop coughing and are used in the treatment o dry coughs. Sha Shen is better for augmenting the Stomach, generating fluids and stopping thirst, while clearing the lungs and expelling Phlegm. Bai He moistens the Lungs, stops hemoptysis, clears the Heart and quiets the Spirit.
  15. Honey-prepared Lily Bulb Mi Zhi Bai He is stronger at moistening the Lungs, stopping coughs and tonifying Lung Qi. It is used for chronic coughs due to Lung Deficiency.
  16. Lily Root Bai He Gen is preferred for relieving apprehension and anxiety. It also treats dysuria and edema.
  17. Traditionally drinking Lily Flower Tea or eating the petals is said to help overcome worry and sorrow.
  18. Bai He is also effective for numerous psychological and cardiac imbalances related to Heart Yin Deficiency: palpitations, deep cardiac pain with a feeling of emptiness in the cardiac region, insomnia, profuse dreams, vexation, agitation and neurasthenia.
  19. When a dry cough is predominant, honey mix-fried Bai He should be used.
  20. If vexation and agitation or insomnia is predominant, uncooked Bai He should be prescribed.
