NOTES

  1. This herb is included in formulas for treating loss of body fluids and failure of the Spleen and Kidneys to filter Dampness.
  2. Contains starch similar to arrowroot and is used as a food especially during illness with weakened digestion.
  3. It is sold in Chinese grocery stores as foxnut.
  4. Both Qian Shi and Sm. Nelumbinis Lian Zi are seeds that tonify and bind, with sweet, neutral and astringent properties. They both enter the Spleen and Kidneys to tonify those organs and treat chronic diarrhea and spermatorrhea. Qian Shi also eliminates Dampness and is better at treating Deficiency disorders in which Dampness predominates, such as vaginal discharge. Lian Zi nourishes the Heart, reestablishes communication between the Heart and Kidneys and treats incessant uterine bleeding.
  5. Dry-fried Euryales Seeds Chao Qian Shi are warming and thus able to tonify the Spleen and Kidneys and to stabilize and conserve. They are used to treat spermatorrhea due to failure of the Jing Gate
  6. Bran-fried Euryale Seeds Fu Chao Qian Shi are warm and better to tonify and stabilize especially for chronic diarrhea due to Spleen Deficiency.
