INDIVIDUAL HERBS

NAME: GOU QI ZI - 枸杞子 - FRUCTUS LYCII - LYCIUM FRUIT

Pharmaceutical Latin: Fructus Lycii
Common English: Lycium Fruit
Chinese Wolfberry Fruit
Matrimony Vine Fruit

CATEGORY

Herbs that Tonify Blood (Herbs that Tonify the Yin)

PROPERTIES

Taste Temperature Entering Meridians Dosage
Sweet
Neutral
Liver
Lung
Kidney
5-18g
Tincture: 2-4ml

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Nourishes and tonifies Liver and Kidney Blood and Yin

Blood and Yin Deficiency with sore back and legs, low-grade abdominal pain, impotence, nocturnal emissions, wasting and thirsting disorder and consumption

Liver and Kidney Deficiency

Benefits Jing and brightens the eyes

Liver and Kidney Deficiency where Jing and Blood cannot nourish eyes with dizziness, blurred vision and diminished visual acuity

Nourishes Yin and moistens the Lungs

Consumptive cough and wasting and thirsting disorder

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with external Excess Heat.
  • Use caution for those with Spleen Deficiency with Dampness and loose stools or diarrhea.
  • Use with caution for those with Internal Full Heat, including high fever.
  • Use with caution during pregnancy.

NOTES

  1. This herb is neither hot nor cold so it is good for any Kidney and Liver Deficiency.
  2. It works if cooked in foods.
  3. Buy it in cans - darker is better.
  4. This herb is one of the primary tonics.
  5. It is widely used for weakened condition, impotence and old age disabilities.
  6. In formulas, it is often specific for eye problems due to Kidney and Liver Deficiency.
  7. In China, Lycium wine is a famous tonic.
  8. It can be used alone and soaked in grain-based alcohol for blurred vision.
  9. This herb has a reputation for improving skin quality and enhancing vitality, beauty and long life.
  10. Folk medicine - 10g steamed and taken 2-3 times per day for wasting and thirsting. Add Rx. Trichosanthis Tian Hua Fen and Rz. Dioscoreae Shan Yao to increase its effectiveness.
  11. Gou Qi Zi, Sm. Cuscutae Tu Si Zi and Sm. Astragali Complanati Sha Yuan Zi all tonify the Kidney Yin and Yang and are often used together. Gou Qi Zi better nourishes Kidney and Lung Yin, slightly tonifies Kidney Yang and tonifies Liver Blood to improve vision. Tu Si Zi and Sha Yuan Zi both tend toward the Yang and astringe Jing.
  12. Both Gou Qi Zi and Fr. Broussonetiae Chu Shi Zi are sweet, moistening, enrich and tonify Liver and Kidney Yin and Blood and are used for achy, weak lower back and legs, dizziness, Light-headedness and visual disturbances. Gou Qi Zi is neutral and can tonify Jing and treat coughs by moistening the Lungs. Chu Shi Zi is cold and clears Heat, especially in the Liver channel affecting the eyes. it also promotes urination.
  13. Gou Qi Zi, Fr. Ligustri Lucidi Niu Zhen Zi and Fr. Mori Sang Shen all tonify the Liver and Kidney Yin. Gou Qi Zi also assists Kidney Yang and Sang Shen tonifies the Blood. Nu Zhen Zi tonifies the Yin and cools Heat from Yin Deficiency.
  14. Dry-fried Lycium Fruit Chao Gou Qi Zi has a warm nature which makes it easier to digest.
  15. Salt-fried Lycium Fruit Ian Chao Gou Qi Zi enters the Kidneys, moves downward, augments Jing and is easier to digest.
  16. Cuscuta-fried Lycium Fruit Tu Si Zi Chao Gou Qi Zi is more Yang-tonifying, enabling it to replenish Jing and fortify Yang. It is used for debilitated primal Yang Qi.
