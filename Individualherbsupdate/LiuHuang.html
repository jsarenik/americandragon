ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Relieves toxicity, kills parasites and stops itching

Important topically for scabies, tinea, Damp-festering sores, ulcers, carbuncles and recalcitrant Yin flat abscess and furuncles

Itching

Tonifies Ming Men Fire and strengthens Yang

Kidney Yang Deficiency with painful lower back and knees and impotence

Kidney and Spleen Yang Deficiencies with diarrhea

Asthma due to Kidneys unable to grasp Qi

Constipation or diarrhea in the elderly caused by Cold

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated during pregnancy.
  • Contraindicated for those with Yin Deficiency with Heat signs.
  • Use caution with internal use.

NOTES

  1. Both Liu Huang and Realgar Xiong Huang resolve toxicity and kill parasites and are used in treating scabies, tinea and rashes. Liu Huang is better against tinea and itching lesions, its hot nature makes it unsuitable for treating Yang lesions. Xiong Huang is more effective at resolving toxins and treating abscesses and sores.
  2. This herb can be used individually topically for scabies, herpetic lesions, sores and fungal infections.
