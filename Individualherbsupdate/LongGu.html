NAME: LONG GU - 龙骨 - FOSSILIA OSSIS MASTODI - OS DRACONIS - DRAGON BONE

Pharmaceutical Latin: Fossilia Ossis Mastodi
Os Draconis
Stegodon
Common English: Dragon Bone
Fossilized vertebrae and bones of the extremities of mammals

CATEGORY

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Settles anxiety and calms the Spirit

Agitated Heart and Spirit with emotional distress, restlessness, insomnia, palpitations with anxiety, seizures or withdrawal mania

Calms the Liver and anchors and preserves Floating Yang

Irritability, restlessness, dizziness, vertigo, blurred vision and a bad temper due to Liver Yin Deficiency with Liver Yang Rising

Prevents leakage of fluids

Loss of fluids due to Deficiency with spermatorrhea, night sweats, spontaneous sweating, nocturnal emissions, vaginal discharge and uterine bleeding

Topically generates muscles and astringes ulcers

Chronic non-healing sores and ulcerations

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with Damp-Heat.
  • Contraindicated for those with External disorders.
  • Contraindicated for those with diarrhea, dysenteric disorder, gynecological bleeding or hematuria due to Blood Heat.
  • Contraindicated for those with loss of Jing due to Excess Fire.
  • Do not mix with fish.
INCOMPATIBILITIES
  • Counteracts Gypsum Fibrosum Shi Gao.

NOTES

  1. Dens Draconis Long Chi 龙齿 “Dragon’s Teeth” or fossilized teeth are astringent and Cool, and have basically the same functions as Long Gu, however the “teeth” are especially effective for palpitations with anxiety, insomnia, dream-disturbed sleep, convulsions and delirium. It is also used to treat arthritis, osteomyelitis and gastralgia. The dosage is 9-16g. For restlessness or night crying in children, use with Rx. Paeoniae Alba Bai Shao, Rx. Ophiopogonis Mai Men Dong, Rx. et Rz. Rhei Da Huang and Rz. Cimicifugae Sheng Ma.
  2. This herb is a mineral not an animal part. It is the fossilized remains of various animals which are rich in a variety of calcium salts and other minerals.
  3. It can be used alone topically for non-healing sores, ulcers eczema or rashes.
  4. Some sources say that this herb settles fright and stabilizes Liver Wind.
  5. This herb treats disorders of the Dai channel.
  6. Both this herb and Concha Ostreae Mu Li have astringent properties. Mu Li also softens hardness and dissipates nodules, while Long Gu is better at calming the Spirit. They are often used together to enhance their ability to subdue Rising Yang.
  7. Both Long Gu and Concha Ostreae Mu Li are similar in many of their qualities and are often used together. Long Gu is sweet, astringent enters the Heart and Liver channels and is better at stopping abandonment, suppressing fright calming the Spirit. It does not soften hardness or disperse clumping and has much less Yin -nourishing ability so is less used when Yin Deficiency causes night sweats. Mu Li is salty, astringent, enters the Kidneys to soften hardness, transform Phlegm and cool Heat.
  8. Calcined Dragon bone Duan Long Gu has an enhanced ability to restrain, inhibit and bind to stop sweating, astringe Jing, stop bleeding, bind up the Intestines and generate flesh. It is primarily used to treat night sweats, spontaneous sweating, spermatorrhea, vaginal discharge, continuous uterine bleeding, chronic diarrhea or dysenteric disorder and is applied externally to assist the closure of non-healing sores.
