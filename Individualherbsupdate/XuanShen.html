Pharmaceutical Latin: Radix Scrophulariae
Common English: Scrophularia

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes
Clears Heat and cools the Blood

Xue Stage Heat or Blood Heat with hemorrhage (hematemesis), fever, dry mouth and a purplish tongue

Nourishes Yin

Yin Deficiency with Heat signs especially as sequelae from Warm Febrile Disease with Dry Lungs, insomnia, constipation, an unclear Shen and irritability

Yin Deficiency dry Lungs

Heart Yin Deficiency

Severe throat pain and swelling

Drains Fire

Extreme throat pain or red, swollen eyes

Relieves toxicity

 Ying Stage Heat

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with Cold-Dampness of the Spleen/Stomach.
  • Contraindicated for those with dizziness and abdominal pain from Blood Deficiency.
  • Contraindicated for those with accumulated thin mucus leading to chills, fever and uncomfortable fullness.
  • Do not use with brass or iron utensils.
INCOMPATIBILITIES
  • Incompatible with Rz. et Rx. Veratri Li Lu.
  • Antagonizes Rx. Astragali Huang Qi.
  • Antagonizes Rz, Zingiberis Officinalis Gan Jiang.
  • Antagonizes Fr. Zizyphi Jujube Da Zao.
  • Antagonizes Fr. Corni Officinalis Shan Zhu Yu.
HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS
  • Use caution when used in conjunction with insulin, sulfonylureas and other antidiabetics such as tolbutamide (Orinase),glipizide (Glucotrol), and glyburide (DiaBeta/Micronase) as there may be a synergistic effect leading to hypoglycemia.

NOTES

  1. Do not store in brass or iron.
  2. Named after the disease scrofula which is a type of tuberculosis affecting the lymph nodes of the neck.
  3. It is a valuable remedy for scrofula.
  4. Some sources classify this as a Yin Tonic.
  5. It mainly targets the cardiovascular system.
  6. It moistens Dryness.
  7. It is a neurocardiac sedative and targets chronic neurogenic stress conditions of the Heart.
  8. Steamed Scrophularia Zheng Xuan Shen is better for nourishing Yin and draining Deficiency Fire.
  9. Both Xuan Shen and Rx. Rehmanniae Sheng Di Huang clear Heat, cool the Blood, enrich Yin and generate fluids. Xuan Shen focuses on the Yin, directs Fire downward, resolves toxicity, disperses clumps, cools the Lungs and benefits the throat. It emphasizes regulating the Lungs and Kidneys. Sheng Di Huang is stronger at cooling Blood, while enriching Yin and stopping bleeding. It focuses at the Blood level and regulates disorders of the Heart, Liver and Kidneys.
