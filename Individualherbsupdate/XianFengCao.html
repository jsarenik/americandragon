NOTES

  • This herb can be used as a beverage in summertime to prevent Summerheat.
  • It can be used alone topically for toxic swellings.
  • It is said that the nature of the herb is sweet and bland, with a neutral property, though other sources list it as bitter. The applications listed include influenza, swollen and sore throat, enteritis, dysentery, jaundice, intestinal carbuncle, epilepsy in children, malnutrition in infants, and hemorrhoids.
  • Other sources say that this herb treats rheumatoid arthritis, sprain, insect and scorpion sting, diarrhea, dysentery, hepatitis, acute nephritis, stomach ache, and sore throat.
  • The decoction of this herb cures dysentery, laryngalgia, dysphagia, vomiting, cardiac spasm and esophageal dilatation, and is effective in removing toxic materials, stopping diarrhea, and clearing away heat.
  • This herb has been shown to inhibit the growth of cancer cells.
  • This herb has been used to treat cecitis in the recent years.
  • For esophageal cancer, 15-30 grams per day is decocted and taken orally.
  • External application of this herb with Fr. Arctii Niu Bang Zi and blended with pig fat can cure finger cut.
  • It is said that this herb, bitter in taste, mild in nature, and non-toxic mainly cures spider and fly bites through Internal and External application.
  • It is also said that scorpion sting can be cured by external application of this herb.
