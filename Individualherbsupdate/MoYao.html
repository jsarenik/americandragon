INDIVIDUAL HERBS

NAME: MO YAO - 没药 - MYRRH

Pharmaceutical Latin: Myrrh    
Resina Commiphorae  
Common English: Myrrh
Commiphora

CATEGORY

Herbs that Regulate Blood: Herbs that Invigorate Blood

PROPERTIES

Taste Temperature Entering Meridians Dosage
Bitter
Neutral

Tincture: 1-3ml
Essential Oil: 2 drops in a gel cap with olive oil

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Invigorates the Blood, dispels Blood Stasis, alleviates pain and reduces swelling

Blood Stasis with pain from trauma, sores, carbuncles, swellings, fixed abdominal masses, painful obstruction, chest pain, abdominal pain and amenorrhea

Generates flesh and promotes healing

Chronic non-healing sores

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated during pregnancy.
  • Contraindicated in the absence of Stasis.
  • Contraindicated for those with excessive uterine bleeding.
  • Contraindicated for those with excessive loss of postpartum lochia with abdominal pain due to Deficiency.
  • Use with caution for those with weak Stomachs.
  • Do not use long term.
  • When combined with Olibanum Ru Xiang, lower the dosage.

NOTES

  1. This herb is the resin from the stem and was applied to corpses in ancient Egypt as a preservative.
  2. This herb acts as an astringent when applied to the lips.
  3. This herb is primarily used for severe pain from Blood Stasis.
  4. One source says that this herb regulates Qi.
  5. Both Mo Yao and Olibanum Ru Xiang invigorate the Blood, disperse Stasis, stop pain, reduce swelling and generate flesh. Ru Xiang is warm, piercingly aromatic, powerfully stops pain, acts more on Qi, more powerfully invigorates the Blood and relaxes the sinews. Mo Yao is neutral, bitter, powerfully breaks up Stagnation, drains, disperses Blood and acts more on the Blood.
  6. Unprepared Myrrh Shen Mo Yao is generally used externally or in pill form.
  7. Prepared Myrrh Zhi Mo Yao has a less disagreeable effect on digestion, tastes better, is stronger at invigorating Blood , stopping pain, restraining and inhibiting and generating flesh.
