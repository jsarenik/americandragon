ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Astringes leakage of Blood and stops bleeding

Hemoptysis, hematemesis, epistaxis, hemafecia and excessive uterine bleeding

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Because this herb is very astringent, it must be used with significant Blood-invigorating herbs when signs of Stasis are present.
  • Contraindicated for those with Intestinal bleeding and dysentery due to Damp-Heat.

NOTES

  1. This herb is usually used charred.
  2. This herb is mainly used for bloody Phlegm.
  3. It can be used alone for uterine bleeding or excessive menstrual bleeding.
  4. Both Zong Liu Pi and Nodus Nelumbinis Rhizomatis Ou Jie astringe bleeding. Ou Jie is sweet and astringent and both stops bleeding and reduces Blood Stasis and when fresh, cools Blood Heat. Zong Lu Pi is bitter and astringent and is more strongly binding and restraining than Ou Jie. It is used primarily for bleeding that is not associated with Blood Stasis. It also dries Dampness and therefore is appropriate for treating vaginal discharge.
  5. Aged Trachycarpus Chen Zong Pi are traditionally the only fiber used.for medicinal purposes. The older and more used, the better effect.
  6. Charred Trachycarpus Chen Zong (Zong Lu Tan, Zong Pi Tan) enters the Blood, stopping all types of bleeding and can be used for epistaxis, hematemesis, Intestinal Wind, and continuous uterine bleeding whenever there is no Heat internally. This is the most commonly used form. It is most appropriate when bleeding is heavy as long as the Stasis and obstruction have already been completely eliminated.
  7. Fructus Trachycarpi Zong Lu Zi is the fruit of the same plant and has similar functions and indications. Zong Lu Zi is more effective in treating hypertension, insomnia or excessive dreaming. the dosage is 6-30g.
