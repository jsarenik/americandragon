ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Resolves Shao Yang disorders and reduces fever (harmonizes the Exterior and Interior)

Shao Yang Stage with alternating chills and fever, a bitter taste,dizziness, tinnitus, flank pain, irritability, vomiting and a stifling sensation in the chest

Deficiency Heat (auxiliary)

Gallbladder Fire

Spreads Liver Qi and relieves Stagnation

Liver Qi Stagnation with dizziness, vertigo, chest and flank pain, emotional instability and menstrual problems

Disharmony between the Liver and Spleen with epigastric and flank pain, a stifling sensation in the chest, abdominal bloating, nausea and indigestion

Liver/Gallbladder Disharmony

Raises Yang Qi (specifically the Clear Qi of the Stomach and Gallbladder)

Spleen and Stomach Yang Deficiency, Qi collapse (prolapse) with hemorrhoids, vaginal discharge, bleeding or exhaustion

Disperses Wind-Heat and resolves Phlegm and congestion

Wind-Heat

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with Yin Deficiency cough.
  • Contraindicated for those with Yin and/or Fluid Deficiency (severe Yin Damage).
  • Contraindicated for those with Liver Fire or Yang Rising ascending to the head.
  • This herb may cause nausea or vomiting. To counteract, use smaller dosage.
  • Do not use alone continuously.

NOTES

  1. Northern Bupleurum Bei Chai Hu is harder and more effective in resolving the Shao Yang and reducing Heat. It is used to treat External Wind-Heat.
  2. Southern Bupleurum Nan Chai Hu is relatively thin and soft and is more effective in spreading Liver Qi and relieving Stagnation.
  3. This herb treats Heat from Deficiency, gynecological problems, boils, furuncles and eye and ear diseases.
  4. It is one of the most important of the Cold herbs. It relaxes Liver tension, calms Wind, cools Fire, and is thus used to treat convulsions, muscle tension, lumps, tumors, skin ailments, digestive disturbances, Lung Heat, menstrual irregularity and Excess bleeding due to Heat. It may release pent-up anger and Heat. Rx. Paeoniae Alba Bai Shao moderates this effect.
  5. Bupleurum is the primary herb in harmonization therapies.
  6. It immediately stimulates the immune system.
  7. It is good for all types of toxicosis, especially Liver/Gallbladder infections.
  8. It is good for allergies.
  9. Large doses have been used to treat malaria and blackwater fever.
  10. Spring Bupleurum Chun Chai Hu is dispersing and draining and is good for spreading Liver Qi.
  11. Dry-fried Bupleurum Chao Chai Hu is less cool but is better able to lift clear Yang.
  12. Vinegar Bupleurum Cu Cha Hu has an enhanced ability to soothe the Liver, harmonize the Blood and stop pain.
  13. For topical application, use raw.
  14. For Internal conditions, roast in sake.
  15. For Phlegm and Heat, roast in honey water.
  16. For Liver and Gall Bladder Fire, roast in pork gall bladder water.
