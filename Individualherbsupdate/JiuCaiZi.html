INDIVIDUAL HERBS

NAME: JIU CAI ZI - 韮菜子 - SEMEN ALLII TUBEROSI

Pharmaceutical Latin: Semen Allii Tuberosi
Common English: Chinese Leek Seeds
Allium Seeds

CATEGORY

Herbs that Tonify the Yang

PROPERTIES

Taste Temperature Entering Meridians Dosage
Acrid
Sweet
Warm
Kidney
Liver
3-15g
tincture: 1-3ml

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Supplements the Liver, warms the Kidneys, fortifies Yang and astringes Jing

Impotence, spermatorrhea, nocturnal emission, enuresis, urinary incontinence or frequency, vaginal discharge and a weak and sore lower back and knees

Warms the Stomach and treats chronic Stomach Qi Reversal

 Vomiting and chronic stubborn hiccups caused by Stomach Cold

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with Stomach Deficiency and Heat.
  • Contraindicated for those with Yin Deficiency Fire.
  • It will increase pain and itching for those with toxic sores.
  • It is only appropriate for patients with Kidney Qi unable to restrain and contain.

NOTES

  1. This herb warms the lower back and knees.
  2. Nocturnal emissions and loss of Jing is Yang unable to maintain Yin. Loss of Jing in the urine is the Yin failing to maintain Yang. This herb treats both.
  3. One source says that this herb should be dry-fried for use.
  4. It can be used alone as a powder for Kidney Yang Deficiency.
  5. Both Jiu Cai Zi and Actinolitum Yang Qi Shi strongly tonify the Kidney Yang. Yang Qi Shi is fiercely warming in nature, warms the Ming Men and reduces both mobile and fixed masses. Jiu Cai Zi warms the Yang and astringes the Jing and urine.
  6. Dry-fried Chinese Leek Seeds Chao Jiu Cai Zi are stronger at warming the Kidneys and astringing the Jing.
