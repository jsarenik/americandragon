NAME: GAN SONG (GAN SONG XIANG) - 干松 - RADIX SEU RHIZOMA NARDOSTACHYDIS

Pharmaceutical Latin: Radix seu Rhizoma Nardostachydis
Common English: Nardostachys Root
Chinese Spikenard
Indian Spikenard Root
Indian, Himalayan or Syrian Nard

CATEGORY

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Awakens the Spleen, promotes the movement of Qi, unblocks Stagnation, disperses Cold and alleviates pain

Abdominal and epigastric distention and pain with anorexia and chest oppression

Toothache

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with Qi Deficiency.
  • Contraindicated for those with Blood Heat.
  • Contraindicated during pregnancy.
  • Do not use continuously on its own due to its low cumulative toxicity.

NOTES

  1. This herb relieves abdominal cramping, particularly that of sudden turmoil disorder.
  2. It is used Internally and topically.
  3. It can be used topically for leg Qi and edema.
  4. This herb regulates Qi in the chest and abdomen.
  5. This herb treats palpitations, anxiety, insomnia, stress, tachycardia, hysteria, neurosis, hypertension, angina pectoris, seizures, cough, menstrual problems, hair loss, hemorrhoids, varicose veins, infections and sores and boils.
  6. One source says it treats Heart Yin Deficiency with Liver Yang Rising, Phlegm-Damp in the Lungs and Liver and Spleen Disharmony
  7. It is especially indicated for arrhythmia and tachycardia.
