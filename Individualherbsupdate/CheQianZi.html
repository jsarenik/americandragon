NAME: CHE QIAN ZI - 车前子 - SEMEN PLANTAGINIS

Pharmaceutical Latin: Semen Plantaginis
Common English: Plantago Seeds
Asian Plantain Seeds

CATEGORY

Herbs that Drain Dampness (Diuretics)

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Promotes urination and clears Damp-Heat

Damp-Heat, generally Damp-Heat in the Lower Jiao with edema, painful urinary dysfunction

Promotes urination to solidify the stool

Diarrhea due to Damp-Heat

Summerheat with Dampness

Clears Liver Heat and clears the eyes

Eye problems due to Liver and Kidney Yin Deficiency with dry eyes and diminished visual acuity

Heat in the Liver Channel with red, painful, swollen eyes and sensitivity to light

Clears the Lungs, expels Phlegm and stops coughing

Lung Heat induced cough with yellow copious Phlegm

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with no Damp-Heat symptoms.
  • Use caution during pregnancy.
  • Use caution for those with spermatorrhea due to Kidney Deficiency (supplement with Kidney tonics).
  • Use caution for those with polyuria.
  • Use caution for those with constipation.
  • Use caution for those with exhausted Yang.
  • Use caution for those with exhaustion from overwork.
INCOMPATIBILITIES
HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS
  • This herb may reduce the absorption of oral medications such as lithium. It may also inhibit the absorption of carbohydrates, requiring the adjustment of insulin dosage in diabetic patients.
  • Concurrent use with diuretics such as chlorothiazide, hydrochlorothiazide, furmoside (Lasix), bumetinide (Bumex) and torsemide (Demadex) may lead to increased elimination of water and/or electrolytes.

NOTES

  1. This herb is often used for pelvic inflammations, especially urinary tract infections.
  2. The whole plant, Hb. Plantaginis Che Qian Cao is less effective at promoting urination but better at clearing Heat. It also detoxifies. It is sweet and cold and treats abscesses and swellings (internally and topically). The dosage is 9-30g or 2-5ml as a tincture.
  3. The seed contains mucilage and soothes the vagina, colon and urinary tract.
  4. This herb is important for promoting urination, clearing Damp-Heat and clearing Heat from the Liver and Lung.
  5. The seeds are very small so to prevent them from making the decoction too gelatinous, decoct them in a separate cloth bag.
  6. Both Che Qian Zi and Rz. Alismatis Ze Xie facilitate water, reduce edema, clear Heat, enter the Kidney channel and drain Dampness. They are often used together to treat abdominal distention due to edema, Lin Syndrome and Summerheat-Damp diarrhea. Che Qian Zi benefits the Kidneys by removing pathogenic Dampness, clears the Liver and eyes and transforms Phlegm in the Lungs to treat pathogenic Heat leading to red eyes and Lung Heat transforming into Phlegm. Ze Xie clears and drains ministerial Fire to treat Blazing Fire due to Yin Deficiency.
  7. Unprepared Plantago Seeds Sheng Che Qian Zi are slippery and heavy and direct Heat and Dampness downward and out through the urine.
  8. Dry-fried Plantago Seeds Chao Che Qian Zi are less cold and are able to expel water and Dampness without injuring Spleen Qi. They are used for diarrhea and/or vaginal discharge due to Spleen Deficiency.
  9. Salt-processed Plantago Seeds Yan Zhi Che Qian Zi directs downward and facilitate urination to treat overflow of water and Dampness due to Kidney and Spleen Yang Deficiency.
