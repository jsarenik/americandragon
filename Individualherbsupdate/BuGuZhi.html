INDIVIDUAL HERBS

NAME: BU GU ZHI - 补骨脂 - FRUCTUS PSORALIAE

Pharmaceutical Latin: Fructus Psoraliae
Common English: Psoraliae Fruit
Scurf Pea Berry

CATEGORY

Herbs that Tonify the Yang

PROPERTIES

Taste Temperature Entering Meridians Dosage
Acrid
Bitter
Very Warm
Kidney
Spleen
(Pericardium)
3-9g
Tincture: 2-4ml

ACTIONS AND INDICATIONS

Actions Indications/Syndromes

Tonifies the Kidneys, strengthens Yang, stabilizes Jing and astringes urine

Kidney Yang Deficiency with impotence, premature ejaculation, enuresis, urinary frequency, a cold and painful lower back and extremities

Kidneys unable to grasp Qi

Weakening of Ming Men Fire

Tonifies and warms Spleen Yang and stops diarrhea

Diarrhea due to Spleen Yang Deficiency with borborygmus and abdominal pain

Most appropriate for those with both Spleen and Kidney Yang Deficiency
Topically for vitiligo Vitiligo (soaked in wine and applied topically)

CONTRAINDICATIONS, INCOMPATIBILITIES AND HERB/DRUG INTERACTIONS

CONTRAINDICATIONS
  • Contraindicated for those with deficient Yin with Heat signs or constipation.
  • Contraindicated for those with Damp-Heat causing atrophy of the bones.
  • Use cautiously for Cold Deficiency of the Stomach.
  • Use with caution during pregnancy.
INCOMPATIBILITIES
  • Antagonizes Rx. Glycyrrhizae Gan Cao.

NOTES

  1. The literal translation is “tonify bone resin”.
  2. It treats the Lower Jiao when Yuan Qi is Deficient or Cold.
  3. It generates Earth - the Spleen becomes warm and strong.
  4. It has recently been used for uterine bleeding, excessive menstrual bleeding, post-abortion excessive bleeding, bleeding due to birth control pill or IUD use, hemophilia, epistaxis and ulcers.
  5. It helps the Yang to ascend.
  6. When used for vitiligo, the affected area should be exposed to daylight after application for 10 minutes or 2-5 minutes under ultraviolet light. It should be washed to prevent local skin reactions. If reactions occur cease using Bu Gu Zhi.
  7. It has been shown to prevent miscarriage.
  8. Its hemostatic action has a slow onset.
  9. It also has a fracture healing ability.
  10. This herb can be used alone or served with rice congee for polyuria in the elderly.
  11. It can be used alone as a powder taken with warm liquor for low back pain.
  12. Both Bu Gu Zhi and Sm. Astragali Complanati Sha Yuan Zi tonify Kidney Yang, astringe Jing and treat urinary dysfunction. Bu Gu Zhi is very warm, tonifies Spleen Yang and astringes diarrhea. Sha Yuan Zi is not too warm or drying and brightens eyes. It tonifies the Liver and Kidney Deficiency that leads to blurred vision.
  13. Both Bu Gu Zhi and Fr. Alpiniae Oxyphyllae Yi Zhi Ren tonify the Spleen and Kidneys, fortify Yang, stop diarrhea, astringe Jing and urine and are commonly used to treat Spleen and Kidney Yang Deficiencies. Bu Gu Zhi Primarily warms the Kidneys thereby indirectly warms the Spleen Yang. It treats chronic diarrhea and cock-crow diarrhea due to Spleen and Kidney Yang Deficiencies. It also treats lower back pain. Yi Zhi Ren more strongly warms Spleen Yang, and secondarily warms the Kidneys to treat diarrhea associated with with the exposure of the Spleen and Stomach to Cold. it also treats uterine bleeding.
  14. Dry-fried Psoralea Chao Bu Gu Zhi is less bitter and drying and less acrid and dispersing to better warm the Yang and stop diarrhea.
  15. Salt-fried Psoralea Yan Chao Bu Gu Zhi also known as Prepared Psoralea Zhi Bu Gu Zhi directly enters the Kidney channel to better tonify and astringe the Lower Jiao. it is often used to treat urinary incontinence, impotence and Kidneys failing to grasp Qi.
  16. Wine-fried Psoralea Jiu Chao Bu Gu Zhi has an enhanced acrid flavor and dispersing ability to strengthen its ability to treat Wind-Damp Bi.
